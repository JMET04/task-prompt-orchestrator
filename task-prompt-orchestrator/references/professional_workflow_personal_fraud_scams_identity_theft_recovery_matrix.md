# Professional Workflow Personal Fraud Scams Identity Theft Recovery Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle personal scams, suspected fraud, phishing messages, identity theft, account takeover, unauthorized payments, credit freeze or monitoring, evidence logs, reporting and escalation, disputes, chargebacks, device/account recovery, victim support boundaries, or fraud-prevention review.

Use this for personal organization, urgent containment checklists, evidence preservation, communication drafts, account recovery planning, and official-channel escalation. Do not use it to replace emergency services, law enforcement, legal advice, financial-institution decisions, credit-bureau decisions, cybersecurity incident response, forensic investigation, platform enforcement, tax advice, mental-health crisis support, or identity-theft recovery services.

## FRDSCP - Fraud Scam Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FRDSCP01 | What suspicious event is being handled? | Define scam contact, unauthorized charge, account takeover, identity misuse, phishing link, fake marketplace, or impersonation. |
| FRDSCP02 | Who is affected? | Capture account holder, household member, dependent, elder, business owner, traveler, seller, buyer, or shared-account user. |
| FRDSCP03 | What assets or accounts may be affected? | List bank, card, payment app, email, phone, social media, government ID, credit file, marketplace, or device. |
| FRDSCP04 | What is known versus suspected? | Separate confirmed facts, user observations, third-party claims, guesses, and unknowns. |
| FRDSCP05 | What immediate risk exists? | Identify active money movement, live attacker access, identity document exposure, coercion, threats, or safety danger. |
| FRDSCP06 | What official sources are relevant? | Use bank, card issuer, platform support, credit bureaus, government identity site, police, regulator, or consumer agency. |
| FRDSCP07 | What deadline or time sensitivity matters? | Note dispute windows, report deadlines, account lock time, travel needs, payroll date, bill due date, or threat escalation. |
| FRDSCP08 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect account numbers, IDs, passwords, recovery codes, police reports, medical details, and minor/vulnerable-person information. |
| FRDSCP09 | What should not be concluded? | Do not decide legal fraud, liability, reimbursement, criminal identity, tax impact, or technical root cause. |
| FRDSCP10 | What scope artifact should be produced? | Create incident brief, affected-account list, priority map, or fraud response checklist. |

## LOSSCT - Loss Containment

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| LOSSCT01 | What must be stopped first? | Prioritize active transfers, unauthorized login, card use, SIM swap, malware suspicion, extortion, or ongoing contact. |
| LOSSCT02 | Which institution should be contacted urgently? | Contact bank, card issuer, payment app, platform, mobile carrier, employer payroll, credit bureau, or local emergency service. |
| LOSSCT03 | What account access should be locked? | Freeze card, change password, revoke sessions, disable payment method, lock credit, or suspend compromised account. |
| LOSSCT04 | What communication should be paused? | Stop replying to suspicious sender, avoid clicking links, and move to verified official contact channels. |
| LOSSCT05 | What payment or transfer details matter? | Record amount, recipient, date, transaction ID, payment rail, app, bank, and status without attempting unsafe recovery. |
| LOSSCT06 | What safety escalation is needed? | Use emergency services or trusted support when threats, coercion, stalking, violence, or self-harm risk is present. |
| LOSSCT07 | What evidence should be preserved before cleanup? | Save screenshots, messages, URLs, transaction IDs, call logs, emails, and device alerts before deleting or resetting. |
| LOSSCT08 | What high-risk action should be avoided? | Avoid confronting suspects, sending more money, installing remote tools, sharing codes, or paying recovery scammers. |
| LOSSCT09 | What containment status should be tracked? | Mark each affected account as open, locked, reported, disputed, recovered, monitored, or unresolved. |
| LOSSCT10 | What containment artifact should be produced? | Create urgent action list, contact queue, lockout tracker, or status board. |

## PHISMS - Phishing Suspicious Messages

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PHISMS01 | What suspicious message was received? | Capture email, SMS, phone call, voicemail, DM, QR code, ad, marketplace message, or support pop-up. |
| PHISMS02 | What sender details should be preserved? | Save sender address, phone number, profile link, display name, domain, headers if available, and timestamp. |
| PHISMS03 | What link or attachment is involved? | Record URL, shortened link, QR destination, attachment name, file type, and whether it was opened. |
| PHISMS04 | What claim or pressure tactic appears? | Note fake urgency, prize, debt, delivery, bank alert, romance, job, investment, tech support, or government impersonation. |
| PHISMS05 | What action did the user take? | Record clicked, replied, entered credentials, downloaded file, shared code, paid money, or ignored. |
| PHISMS06 | What official verification path exists? | Use known official app, website typed directly, card back phone number, saved contact, or agency source. |
| PHISMS07 | What should be reported? | Forward to platform, email provider, mobile carrier spam channel, impersonated company, or consumer agency when appropriate. |
| PHISMS08 | What should not be done? | Do not click links again, call numbers in the suspicious message, open attachments, or share verification codes. |
| PHISMS09 | What education note should be captured? | Record warning signs, repeated sender pattern, impersonated brand, and prevention rule for future. |
| PHISMS10 | What phishing artifact should be produced? | Create message evidence card, verification checklist, report draft, or prevention note. |

| IDTHEF02 | What misuse signal appeared? | Capture new account notice, credit inquiry, tax notice, benefit claim, collection letter, medical bill, or address change. |
| IDTHEF03 | What official identity recovery source applies? | Use government identity-theft portal, credit bureaus, tax agency, DMV/passport office, benefit agency, or insurer. |
| IDTHEF04 | What credit-file action may be needed? | Prepare credit freeze, fraud alert, credit report review, inquiry dispute, or monitoring checklist. |
| IDTHEF05 | What government document issue exists? | Track lost/stolen ID, replacement, fraud marker, police report, agency appointment, or certified copies. |
| IDTHEF06 | What affected institutions need notice? | Notify bank, card issuer, employer, benefits office, medical insurer, landlord, school, or relevant provider. |
| IDTHEF07 | What evidence should be organized? | Collect letters, credit reports, account statements, police report, identity affidavit, emails, and portal confirmations. |
| IDTHEF08 | What dependent or elder issue exists? | Flag minor, elder, caregiver, power-of-attorney, or vulnerable-person boundaries for qualified support. |
| IDTHEF09 | What should not be assumed? | Do not assume source of breach, legal liability, fraudster identity, or guaranteed removal of records. |
| IDTHEF10 | What identity-theft artifact should be produced? | Create recovery checklist, agency contact sheet, evidence packet, or identity-theft timeline. |

## ACCTKO - Account Takeover

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ACCTKO01 | Which account is compromised or suspicious? | Identify email, bank, social media, payment app, phone carrier, cloud drive, shopping, work, or government account. |
| ACCTKO02 | What access signal exists? | Record password reset, unfamiliar login, changed recovery info, missing funds, sent messages, or locked account. |
| ACCTKO03 | What recovery channel is trusted? | Use official app, typed website, saved support contact, identity verification flow, or account recovery form. |
| ACCTKO04 | What credentials should be rotated? | Change password, passkey, MFA, recovery email, recovery phone, app passwords, and reused credentials. |
| ACCTKO05 | What sessions or devices should be removed? | Log out unknown sessions, revoke app access, remove unknown devices, and review connected accounts. |
| ACCTKO06 | What communications should be sent? | Warn contacts if scam messages were sent, notify work/school if needed, and use concise factual wording. |
| ACCTKO07 | What evidence should be saved? | Save login alerts, account changes, transaction logs, messages sent by attacker, support tickets, and timestamps. |
| ACCTKO08 | What platform limits should be noted? | Record verification delays, appeal windows, locked account rules, content removal process, and support ticket IDs. |
| ACCTKO09 | What should not be shared? | Do not provide passwords, MFA codes, recovery codes, ID scans, or payment details outside verified official flows. |
| ACCTKO10 | What account-takeover artifact should be produced? | Create recovery checklist, session review, support ticket log, or contact warning draft. |

## PAYFRD - Payment Fraud

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PAYFRD01 | What payment issue occurred? | Identify unauthorized card charge, bank transfer, payment app transfer, subscription charge, check fraud, or ATM withdrawal. |
| PAYFRD02 | What transaction details are needed? | Capture amount, date, merchant/recipient, transaction ID, card/account, location, status, and receipt if any. |
| PAYFRD03 | What institution should be contacted? | Contact card issuer, bank, payment app, merchant, payroll, check issuer, or marketplace through verified channels. |
| PAYFRD04 | What card or account containment is needed? | Freeze card, replace card, stop payment, revoke merchant token, change PIN, or lock payment app. |
| PAYFRD05 | What dispute window applies? | Extract source-stated deadline from bank/card/app terms or statement notice without legal interpretation. |
| PAYFRD06 | What provisional credit or reimbursement note matters? | Track issuer statements, case number, provisional credit, reversal, denial, or requested documentation. |
| PAYFRD07 | What recurring-payment issue exists? | Identify subscription, stored card, app store, trial, family sharing, or merchant billing agreement. |
| PAYFRD08 | What related accounts need review? | Check email, shopping account, shipping address, password reuse, device wallet, and saved payment methods. |
| PAYFRD09 | What should not be done? | Do not send more money, use unverified recovery services, or disclose full card/account details in insecure channels. |
| PAYFRD10 | What payment-fraud artifact should be produced? | Create transaction list, dispute packet, contact log, or payment-method cleanup checklist. |

## CRDFRZ - Credit Freeze Monitoring

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CRDFRZ01 | Is credit-file protection needed? | Consider freeze, fraud alert, report review, or monitoring after identity exposure or suspicious inquiry. |
| CRDFRZ02 | Which credit bureaus or agencies apply? | Identify relevant national credit bureaus, specialty bureaus, check systems, tenant reports, or local equivalents. |
| CRDFRZ03 | What account setup is required? | Track bureau login, PIN, recovery method, freeze status, and secure storage of access details. |
| CRDFRZ04 | What report items should be reviewed? | Check accounts, inquiries, addresses, names, employers, collections, public records, and unfamiliar balances. |
| CRDFRZ05 | What dispute evidence should be prepared? | Collect identity affidavit, police report, letters, statements, screenshots, and proof of address. |
| CRDFRZ06 | What temporary lift may be needed? | Plan lift date, creditor name, application window, and reminder to refreeze after legitimate application. |
| CRDFRZ07 | What monitoring cadence is useful? | Set reminders for initial review, follow-up, free report intervals, alert checks, and unresolved items. |
| CRDFRZ08 | What dependents need protection? | Include minor child, elder, dependent adult, or deceased-person identity safeguards when appropriate. |
| CRDFRZ09 | What should not be promised? | Do not promise prevention of all fraud, removal of all items, or reimbursement outcomes. |
| CRDFRZ10 | What credit-protection artifact should be produced? | Create freeze tracker, report review checklist, dispute packet, or monitoring calendar. |

## EVILOG - Evidence Log

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| EVILOG01 | What evidence exists now? | Inventory screenshots, emails, texts, call logs, URLs, receipts, statements, account alerts, IDs, and reports. |
| EVILOG02 | What timeline should be built? | Record first contact, user actions, money movement, account changes, reports filed, and responses received. |
| EVILOG03 | What evidence source and integrity matter? | Keep original files, timestamps, sender metadata, transaction IDs, and unedited copies where possible. |
| EVILOG04 | What sensitive data should be redacted for sharing? | Remove unnecessary account numbers, IDs, passwords, recovery codes, minor info, and unrelated private data. |
| EVILOG05 | What evidence is missing? | List needed bank statements, police report, platform logs, device alerts, shipping proof, or support tickets. |
| EVILOG06 | What submission proof matters? | Save confirmation numbers, emails, portal screenshots, certified mail, fax logs, and representative names. |
| EVILOG07 | What folder structure is needed? | Organize by incident, account, date, institution, evidence type, submitted status, and outcome. |
| EVILOG08 | What contradiction needs review? | Flag inconsistent dates, amounts, names, merchant details, IP/device info, or statements. |
| EVILOG09 | What should not be altered? | Avoid editing evidence misleadingly, deleting original messages, or publishing sensitive evidence publicly. |
| EVILOG10 | What evidence artifact should be produced? | Create evidence index, incident timeline, missing-evidence list, or submission tracker. |

## REPORG - Reporting Organization

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| REPORG01 | Who should receive a report? | Identify bank, card issuer, platform, marketplace, credit bureau, police, regulator, employer, school, or government agency. |
| REPORG02 | What report type is needed? | Prepare fraud report, identity-theft report, police report, platform abuse report, dispute, complaint, or support ticket. |
| REPORG03 | What facts must be included? | Include who, what, when, where, account, transaction, contact method, evidence list, and requested action. |
| REPORG04 | What official form is required? | Use institution forms, agency reports, affidavits, dispute forms, or portal workflows when available. |
| REPORG05 | What report number should be captured? | Store case number, report number, ticket ID, officer/representative name, and contact channel. |
| REPORG06 | What escalation path exists? | Note supervisor, fraud department, executive support, regulator complaint, external review, or legal-support handoff. |
| REPORG07 | What reporting deadline matters? | Track source-stated deadlines, response dates, dispute windows, and evidence due dates. |
| REPORG08 | What duplicate reports should be coordinated? | Link bank, police, platform, credit bureau, and government reports so facts remain consistent. |
| REPORG09 | What should not be reported as fact? | Label suspicions clearly and avoid unsupported accusations or invented details. |
| REPORG10 | What reporting artifact should be produced? | Create report packet, agency contact map, report tracker, or escalation checklist. |

## DISPUT - Disputes Chargebacks Refunds

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| DISPUT01 | What dispute or refund is being pursued? | Identify unauthorized transaction, goods not received, defective product, scam purchase, subscription charge, or merchant issue. |
| DISPUT02 | Which route is appropriate to organize? | Separate merchant refund, card dispute, bank claim, payment-app case, marketplace case, or regulator complaint. |
| DISPUT03 | What evidence supports the dispute? | Collect receipts, order page, tracking, messages, cancellation proof, return proof, screenshots, and statements. |
| DISPUT04 | What deadline or rule should be checked? | Extract source-stated filing window, response time, return window, and documentation requirements. |
| DISPUT05 | What amount breakdown is needed? | Separate item cost, tax, shipping, fees, currency, tip, subscription period, partial refund, and remaining loss. |
| DISPUT06 | What merchant communication should be kept? | Save refund request, response, refusal, promises, chat transcripts, and support ticket. |
| DISPUT07 | What case status should be tracked? | Mark filed, evidence requested, provisionally credited, merchant responded, denied, appealed, closed, or unresolved. |
| DISPUT08 | What escalation may be needed? | Prepare appeal, regulator complaint, marketplace escalation, bank supervisor call, or legal-professional handoff. |
| DISPUT09 | What should not be done? | Avoid duplicate contradictory claims, chargeback abuse, threats, or misrepresenting facts. |
| DISPUT10 | What dispute artifact should be produced? | Create dispute packet, refund message, case tracker, or evidence checklist. |

| DEVRCV07 | What professional support may be needed? | Flag malware suspicion, workplace device, enterprise account, stalkingware concern, or complex forensic needs. |
| DEVRCV08 | What communication recovery matters? | Check email forwarding, phone number porting, voicemail PIN, recovery email, and message filters. |
| DEVRCV09 | What should not be attempted? | Do not install unknown cleanup tools, follow scammer tech-support steps, or erase evidence before saving. |
| DEVRCV10 | What recovery artifact should be produced? | Create device/account cleanup checklist, credential reset plan, or access revocation log. |

## SUPBND - Support Boundaries

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SUPBND01 | What emotional or practical support is needed? | Identify trusted person, family help, elder support, translation, transportation, childcare, or paperwork assistance. |
| SUPBND02 | Is coercion or threat present? | Escalate to emergency services or specialist support when threats, blackmail, domestic abuse, stalking, or extortion exist. |
| SUPBND03 | Is a vulnerable person involved? | Flag minor, elder, dependent adult, disability, language barrier, or cognitive support needs. |
| SUPBND04 | What shame-reduction message helps? | Use factual, nonblaming language to support reporting, containment, and recovery steps. |
| SUPBND05 | What trusted delegation is safe? | Define helper tasks, account access limits, privacy boundaries, and written permission where needed. |
| SUPBND06 | What professional boundary applies? | Route legal, financial, medical, cybersecurity, tax, immigration, or law-enforcement questions to qualified support. |
| SUPBND07 | What communication with household matters? | Notify family about exposed shared accounts, cards, address, devices, mail, or safety concerns. |
| SUPBND08 | What recovery workload should be paced? | Break tasks into urgent, next-day, weekly, and monitoring phases to reduce overwhelm. |
| SUPBND09 | What should not be shared publicly? | Avoid posting sensitive evidence, IDs, financial details, intimate content, or suspect accusations. |
| SUPBND10 | What support artifact should be produced? | Create support plan, trusted-helper checklist, boundary note, or phased recovery plan. |

## FRDREV - Fraud Review Prevention

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FRDREV01 | What review cadence is needed? | Review after containment, after disputes close, monthly for monitoring, and after any new suspicious notice. |
| FRDREV02 | What accounts remain at risk? | List unresolved accounts, reused passwords, exposed IDs, active payment methods, credit reports, and open cases. |
| FRDREV03 | What prevention controls should be added? | Add MFA, passkeys, password manager, credit freeze, transaction alerts, mail security, and trusted contact rules. |
| FRDREV04 | What financial monitoring is useful? | Track statements, alerts, credit reports, recurring charges, app balances, and new-account notices. |
| FRDREV05 | What communication pattern should be blocked? | Block scam numbers, report phishing, update spam filters, remove compromised listings, and educate household members. |
| FRDREV06 | What documents should be archived? | Store incident timeline, reports, dispute outcomes, letters, receipts, restored-account notes, and prevention checklist. |
| FRDREV07 | What unresolved loss or risk remains? | Mark unpaid loss, denied dispute, active investigation, identity misuse, emotional harm, or professional handoff. |
| FRDREV08 | What lessons should update other workflows? | Sync with password recovery, personal finance, document vault, mail security, consumer complaints, and safety plans. |
| FRDREV09 | What should be retired or closed? | Close stale cards, old accounts, unnecessary payment methods, unused apps, and exposed recovery channels. |
| FRDREV10 | What review artifact should be produced? | Create fraud recovery report, prevention checklist, monitoring calendar, or residual-risk log. |
