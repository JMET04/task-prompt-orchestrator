# Professional Workflow Commercial Financial ROI Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must prove commercial feasibility, manage budget, price work, forecast revenue, control spend, justify ROI, or satisfy financial approval. Combine it with resource/capacity, strategy/portfolio, governance/policy, vendor/procurement, and evaluation metrics when the workflow affects real money.

## Commercial Scope

| Code | Commercial question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| COMSCOPE01 | What financial decision does this workflow support? | Separate exploration, budgeting, pricing, investment, approval, delivery, and optimization decisions. |
| COMSCOPE02 | Who owns the commercial outcome? | Identify budget owner, business owner, finance reviewer, approver, operator, and beneficiary. |
| COMSCOPE03 | What money boundary applies? | Define currency, entity, geography, tax context, accounting period, and spend authority. |
| COMSCOPE04 | What is in and out of the business case? | Scope included costs, benefits, risks, scenarios, and excluded externalities. |
| COMSCOPE05 | What commercial time horizon matters? | Choose one-time, monthly, quarterly, annual, multi-year, lifecycle, or payback window. |
| COMSCOPE06 | What baseline will be compared? | Define current-state cost, revenue, throughput, quality, conversion, churn, or risk baseline. |
| COMSCOPE07 | What decision threshold is required? | State minimum margin, ROI, payback, NPV, savings, utilization, or strategic exception threshold. |
| COMSCOPE08 | What non-financial value must be translated? | Convert quality, time, risk, compliance, brand, CX, resilience, or learning value into decision evidence. |
| COMSCOPE09 | What commercial assumptions are explicit? | Record volume, price, adoption, conversion, labor rate, vendor rate, utilization, inflation, and risk assumptions. |
| COMSCOPE10 | What commercial artifact proves scope? | Produce business-case brief, budget charter, pricing brief, investment memo, or finance decision log. |
