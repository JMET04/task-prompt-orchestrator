# Professional Workflow Conflict Mediation Difficult Conversations Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage non-legal conflicts, difficult conversations, apologies, boundary talks, interpersonal repair, roommate friction, friend or family disagreements, team tension, community disputes, expectation resets, informal mediation, or follow-up agreements.

This supports preparation, de-escalation, communication, documentation, and repair planning. It does not replace legal advice, HR investigations, therapy, domestic-violence services, emergency services, law enforcement, formal mediation, safeguarding authority, or crisis support.

## Conflict Conversation Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CNFSCP01 | What conflict or conversation is being handled? | Classify misunderstanding, repeated friction, boundary issue, unmet expectation, apology, repair, decision dispute, or informal mediation. |
| CNFSCP02 | Who is directly involved? | Identify speaker, listener, affected people, mediator, observer, decision-maker, dependent, or group. |
| CNFSCP03 | What relationship context matters? | Note family, friend, partner, roommate, neighbor, classmate, teammate, coworker, volunteer, or community tie. |
| CNFSCP04 | What is the desired outcome? | Define clarity, apology, agreement, boundary, repair, decision, pause, escalation, or respectful closure. |
| CNFSCP05 | What should not be attempted informally? | Stop for violence, threats, coercion, harassment, stalking, abuse, discrimination, legal claims, or safety risk. |
| CNFSCP06 | What stakes are involved? | Capture safety, housing, money, work, reputation, family harmony, access, privacy, or emotional load. |
| CNFSCP07 | What timing matters? | Consider urgency, cooldown, deadline, event, shared task, sleep, childcare, and privacy. |
| CNFSCP08 | What communication mode fits? | Choose in-person, phone, video, text, email, written note, mediated meeting, or no-contact. |
| CNFSCP09 | What evidence or memory should be handled carefully? | Avoid weaponizing logs, screenshots, private messages, or third-party stories. |
| CNFSCP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce conflict brief, safety boundary note, conversation goal, or preparation checklist. |

## Parties Roles And Power

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PARTYM01 | Who has power or dependency? | Identify landlord, manager, parent, teacher, caregiver, host, group leader, elder, or financial controller. |
| PARTYM02 | Who is affected but not present? | Consider children, roommates, team members, neighbors, clients, family, or community members. |
| PARTYM03 | What role should each person play? | Define speaker, listener, note-taker, facilitator, witness, support person, or decision owner. |
| PARTYM04 | What support person is appropriate? | Choose neutral friend, counselor, mentor, mediator, advocate, or professional where needed. |
| PARTYM05 | What role confusion exists? | Separate friend versus manager, parent versus mediator, helper versus rescuer, and witness versus advocate. |
| PARTYM06 | What privacy expectations apply? | Decide who may know, what can be shared, and what stays confidential. |
| PARTYM07 | What cultural or family norm matters? | Account for hierarchy, saving face, directness, elder respect, group harmony, or ritual apology. |
| PARTYM08 | What participation should be voluntary? | Avoid forcing vulnerable people into unsafe or coerced conversations. |
| PARTYM09 | What imbalance requires escalation? | Route severe power abuse, retaliation, or coercion to qualified support. |
| PARTYM10 | What party artifact is required? | Produce role map, affected-party list, privacy rule, or support-person plan. |

## Facts And Shared Reality

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FACTCL01 | What happened according to each side? | Separate observations, interpretations, feelings, assumptions, and accusations. |
| FACTCL02 | What facts can be verified? | Record dates, times, messages, agreements, payments, locations, tasks, and prior decisions. |
| FACTCL03 | What remains uncertain? | Mark unclear intent, missing context, different memories, and unverifiable claims. |
| FACTCL04 | What evidence is relevant and fair? | Use only necessary messages, photos, receipts, schedules, or notes. |
| FACTCL05 | What evidence should be withheld or redacted? | Protect private third-party data, sensitive details, and irrelevant personal history. |
| FACTCL06 | What pattern is recurring? | Identify repeated lateness, noise, avoidance, criticism, broken promises, or unclear expectations. |
| FACTCL07 | What impact can be stated without blame? | Describe lost time, stress, cost, safety concern, confusion, or reduced trust. |
| FACTCL08 | What story might the other person tell? | Prepare a charitable but realistic account of their perspective. |
| FACTCL09 | What fact-check should happen before speaking? | Re-read agreements, verify dates, ask clarifying question, or pause. |
| FACTCL10 | What fact artifact is required? | Produce fact timeline, impact note, uncertainty list, or evidence summary. |

## Needs Interests And Requests

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| NEEDIN01 | What need sits underneath the complaint? | Identify respect, safety, rest, fairness, autonomy, reliability, privacy, inclusion, or predictability. |
| NEEDIN02 | What does each side want? | Capture stated positions and likely underlying interests. |
| NEEDIN03 | What request is specific and doable? | Convert complaint into action, timing, owner, and observable behavior. |
| NEEDIN04 | What request is too broad or controlling? | Avoid demands for personality change, mind-reading, punishment, or impossible certainty. |
| NEEDIN05 | What tradeoff is acceptable? | Consider schedule, money, space, attention, privacy, effort, and convenience. |
| NEEDIN06 | What boundary is non-negotiable? | Define safety, consent, privacy, money, property, work, or health limits. |
| NEEDIN07 | What can be offered in return? | Offer apology, adjustment, information, help, shared cost, or trial period. |
| NEEDIN08 | What would count as enough? | Define minimum acceptable repair, next step, or pause condition. |
| NEEDIN09 | What request should wait? | Delay complex, emotionally loaded, or low-priority requests until stability returns. |
| NEEDIN10 | What needs artifact is required? | Produce needs map, request list, tradeoff table, or boundary statement. |

## Emotion And De-Escalation

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| EMODEE01 | What emotion is active? | Identify anger, fear, shame, grief, disappointment, embarrassment, resentment, or overwhelm. |
| EMODEE02 | What regulation is needed before contact? | Use pause, breathing, walk, sleep, draft-not-send, support call, or grounding. |
| EMODEE03 | What trigger should be anticipated? | Note tone, interruption, accusation, silence, sarcasm, public setting, or old wound. |
| EMODEE04 | What words lower heat? | Use "I may be missing context", "help me understand", "I want to solve this", or "let's pause". |
| EMODEE05 | What words raise heat? | Avoid always/never, labels, insults, diagnosis, threats, contempt, and public shaming. |
| EMODEE06 | What pause rule is needed? | Define timeout signal, restart time, and no-punishment pause agreement. |
| EMODEE07 | What nervous-system cue matters? | Watch yelling, shutdown, shaking, rapid texting, tears, or urge to escalate. |
| EMODEE08 | What support after conversation helps? | Plan debrief, journaling, rest, therapy, peer support, or practical next step. |
| EMODEE09 | What emotional boundary stops the talk? | Stop for intimidation, self-harm threats, violence, stalking, or coercion. |
| EMODEE10 | What de-escalation artifact is required? | Produce cooldown plan, trigger map, pause script, or support plan. |

## Boundary Setting

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| BOUNDN01 | What boundary is needed? | Define time, space, money, privacy, contact, tone, physical access, favors, or decision authority. |
| BOUNDN02 | Why is the boundary needed? | Connect to safety, capacity, values, wellbeing, reliability, or fairness. |
| BOUNDN03 | What behavior is allowed? | State specific acceptable actions and channels. |
| BOUNDN04 | What behavior is not allowed? | State clear limits without overexplaining or attacking. |
| BOUNDN05 | What consequence is realistic? | Define leaving, pausing, declining, changing channel, involving manager, or ending arrangement. |
| BOUNDN06 | What boundary wording is concise? | Use direct, calm, specific language with one request and one consequence. |
| BOUNDN07 | What pushback is likely? | Prepare for guilt, bargaining, anger, minimization, or repeated asks. |
| BOUNDN08 | What consistency is needed? | Plan reminders, documentation, and follow-through. |
| BOUNDN09 | What boundary needs outside support? | Escalate safety, harassment, legal, workplace, or caregiving issues. |
| BOUNDN10 | What boundary artifact is required? | Produce boundary script, consequence plan, pushback response, or contact rule. |

## Conversation Plan

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CONVPL01 | What opening should be used? | Start with purpose, care for relationship, and request for a focused talk. |
| CONVPL02 | What setting is best? | Choose private, neutral, time-limited, safe, accessible, and low-distraction context. |
| CONVPL03 | What agenda fits? | Set issue, facts, impacts, each perspective, requests, options, agreement, and next check. |
| CONVPL04 | What statement should be prepared? | Use observation, feeling/impact, need, request, and willingness to listen. |
| CONVPL05 | What questions invite understanding? | Ask what they experienced, what they need, what would help, and what constraints exist. |
| CONVPL06 | What should be avoided in the first talk? | Avoid pile-on issues, old history, audience, ultimatums, or surprise accusations. |
| CONVPL07 | What note-taking is appropriate? | Keep brief action notes, not a surveillance-style transcript, unless needed. |
| CONVPL08 | What exit plan is needed? | Define pause, reschedule, bring mediator, written follow-up, or safety exit. |
| CONVPL09 | What success looks like for one conversation? | Aim for clarity, lower heat, next step, or agreement, not perfect resolution. |
| CONVPL10 | What conversation artifact is required? | Produce agenda, opening script, questions, or follow-up template. |

## Informal Mediation Meeting

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CONMED01 | What mediation format is appropriate? | Choose informal facilitator, neutral friend, community mediator, manager, written exchange, or professional service. |
| CONMED02 | What ground rules are needed? | Set respectful speech, time limits, no interruptions, confidentiality, pause rule, and decision scope. |
| CONMED03 | What neutrality requirement exists? | Choose facilitator without hidden stake or power over vulnerable parties. |
| CONMED04 | What agenda should guide the meeting? | Use opening, each account, shared facts, needs, options, agreement, and review date. |
| CONMED05 | What issue is in scope? | Limit to specific behaviors, decisions, resources, or expectations. |
| CONMED06 | What is out of scope? | Exclude legal determinations, therapy, punishment, diagnosis, and unrelated history. |
| CONMED07 | What options should be generated? | Brainstorm schedules, communication rules, repairs, shared costs, role changes, or trial periods. |
| CONMED08 | What power imbalance needs correction? | Add support person, private caucus, written input, or formal escalation. |
| CONMED09 | What meeting should not proceed? | Stop if threats, intimidation, retaliation risk, or bad-faith participation appears. |
| CONMED10 | What mediation artifact is required? | Produce meeting plan, ground rules, options list, or neutral summary. |

## Agreement And Commitments

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| AGREEC01 | What agreement is being made? | Define behavior, payment, schedule, access, apology, repair, communication, or decision. |
| AGREEC02 | Who owns each action? | Assign owner, due date, method, and proof of completion. |
| AGREEC03 | What is the trial period? | Set start, end, review date, and what will be tested. |
| AGREEC04 | What should be written down? | Record simple commitments, not private emotional disclosures. |
| AGREEC05 | What measure shows compliance? | Use observable behavior, receipts, messages, attendance, repaired item, or reduced incidents. |
| AGREEC06 | What happens if agreement breaks? | Define reminder, second meeting, escalation, role change, or boundary enforcement. |
| AGREEC07 | What flexibility is allowed? | Include exception rules, notice requirements, and renegotiation path. |
| AGREEC08 | What should not be agreed under pressure? | Avoid coerced apologies, unsafe contact, unfair payments, or silence agreements. |
| AGREEC09 | What confirmation message is needed? | Send concise summary with actions, dates, and next review. |
| AGREEC10 | What agreement artifact is required? | Produce commitment note, action tracker, trial plan, or confirmation message. |

## Follow-Up And Accountability

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FOLLOWC01 | What follow-up cadence is needed? | Set next day, week, month, milestone, or incident-triggered review. |
| FOLLOWC02 | What should be checked? | Review actions, behavior change, remaining tension, new facts, and unintended effects. |
| FOLLOWC03 | What reminder is respectful? | Use neutral, brief, specific message tied to prior agreement. |
| FOLLOWC04 | What progress should be acknowledged? | Name changed behavior, effort, repair, reliability, or improved tone. |
| FOLLOWC05 | What slippage should be addressed? | Raise missed action, repeated pattern, unclear ownership, or new impact. |
| FOLLOWC06 | What accountability support helps? | Use shared calendar, checklist, mediator check-in, manager, or group agreement. |
| FOLLOWC07 | What should be documented? | Keep agreement, follow-up notes, dates, and unresolved issues. |
| FOLLOWC08 | What should not become monitoring? | Avoid obsessive tracking, surveillance, or public scorekeeping. |
| FOLLOWC09 | What closure condition exists? | Define stable period, completed repair, mutual acknowledgement, or decision to disengage. |
| FOLLOWC10 | What follow-up artifact is required? | Produce check-in note, accountability tracker, progress summary, or closure note. |

## Escalation And Outside Help

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ESCALC01 | What indicates informal resolution is insufficient? | Identify threats, harassment, safety, repeated breach, legal issue, discrimination, or severe power imbalance. |
| ESCALC02 | What escalation path fits? | Choose manager, HR, landlord, school, mediator, counselor, advocate, attorney, regulator, or emergency service. |
| ESCALC03 | What evidence should be organized? | Prepare timeline, messages, incidents, witnesses, policies, agreements, and impact notes. |
| ESCALC04 | What immediate safety step is needed? | Leave, call emergency services, contact advocate, change locks, block contact, or avoid meeting. |
| ESCALC05 | What professional boundary applies? | Route legal, medical, psychological, employment, housing, or safeguarding determinations to qualified channels. |
| ESCALC06 | What communication should be used when escalating? | Keep concise, factual, evidence-based, and specific about requested action. |
| ESCALC07 | What retaliation risk exists? | Consider privacy, documentation, safe contacts, and support plan. |
| ESCALC08 | What internal policy should be checked? | Review workplace, school, housing, platform, or community rules. |
| ESCALC09 | What outcome is requested from outside help? | Ask for mediation, investigation, safety plan, policy enforcement, accommodation, or decision. |
| ESCALC10 | What escalation artifact is required? | Produce escalation packet, safety note, evidence timeline, or professional referral list. |

## Repair Apology And Reconnection

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| REPAIRR01 | What harm or rupture needs repair? | Identify hurt feelings, broken trust, missed commitment, disrespect, exclusion, damage, or silence. |
| REPAIRR02 | What apology is appropriate? | Include specific action, impact, responsibility, regret, repair offer, and behavior change. |
| REPAIRR03 | What should not be in the apology? | Avoid excuses, blame-shifting, "if you were hurt", pressure for forgiveness, or public performance. |
| REPAIRR04 | What repair action is possible? | Offer replacement, reimbursement, changed routine, truthful correction, time, or support. |
| REPAIRR05 | What forgiveness boundary matters? | Respect no response, time needed, reduced contact, or relationship ending. |
| REPAIRR06 | What reconnection pace is healthy? | Use small, consistent actions instead of demanding immediate normalcy. |
| REPAIRR07 | What trust evidence should change? | Show reliability, transparency, changed behavior, and reduced defensiveness. |
| REPAIRR08 | What repeated harm requires stronger boundary? | Escalate patterns, abuse, manipulation, or unsafe contact. |
| REPAIRR09 | What gratitude or appreciation helps? | Acknowledge effort, listening, patience, and changed behavior when genuine. |
| REPAIRR10 | What repair artifact is required? | Produce apology draft, repair plan, trust rebuild note, or closure message. |

## Conflict Review And Learning

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CONFREV01 | What conflict period should be reviewed? | Review conversation, week, agreement trial, mediation, escalation, or relationship closure. |
| CONFREV02 | What improved? | Note clarity, reduced incidents, apology, boundary, agreement, safer distance, or better process. |
| CONFREV03 | What remains unresolved? | Identify facts, feelings, actions, trust, power, or structural issues still open. |
| CONFREV04 | What did I contribute? | Reflect on timing, tone, assumptions, avoidance, unclear requests, or overreaction. |
| CONFREV05 | What did I learn about the relationship? | Capture reliability, limits, support, compatibility, or need for distance. |
| CONFREV06 | What boundary should continue? | Keep contact rules, meeting cadence, money limits, privacy rules, or escalation path. |
| CONFREV07 | What pattern should be prevented? | Add early signal, routine check-in, written agreement, or decision rule. |
| CONFREV08 | What records should be kept or deleted? | Keep agreements and safety evidence; delete inflammatory drafts or unnecessary private material. |
| CONFREV09 | What support should be sought? | Consider therapy, coaching, legal advice, HR, mediator, advocate, or trusted mentor. |
| CONFREV10 | What review artifact is required? | Produce conflict retrospective, prevention plan, boundary update, or learning note. |
