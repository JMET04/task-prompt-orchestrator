# Professional Workflow Appointment Booking Calendar Coordination Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage personal appointments, bookings, reservations, availability, calendar holds, reminders, confirmations, preparation packets, rescheduling, cancellations, waitlists, check-in, or appointment follow-up. Use this for coordination, organization, reminders, and records, not to replace professional scheduling systems, medical triage, legal deadlines, immigration advice, emergency services, regulated dispatch, or provider decisions.

## SCHSCP - Scheduling Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SCHSCP01 | What appointment or booking is needed? | Define medical visit, service call, school meeting, interview, reservation, consultation, inspection, pickup, or personal meeting. |
| SCHSCP02 | Why is scheduling needed now? | Link to deadline, symptom follow-up, repair, renewal, event, application, travel, relationship commitment, or recurring routine. |
| SCHSCP03 | Who must attend? | Identify user, family member, caregiver, guest, provider, child, interpreter, decision-maker, or pet owner. |
| SCHSCP04 | What outcome should the appointment produce? | Seek diagnosis, quote, decision, document, service completion, advice, reservation, approval, or next step. |
| SCHSCP05 | What timing constraints exist? | Note due date, urgency, business hours, school/work calendar, transport, childcare, recovery time, or timezone. |
| SCHSCP06 | What location or mode applies? | Choose in-person, phone, video, home visit, office, school, venue, government office, or hybrid. |
| SCHSCP07 | What authority or provider rules apply? | Follow booking portal, cancellation policy, referral rules, ID requirements, deposits, and official instructions. |
| SCHSCP08 | What should not be handled here? | Route emergencies, clinical triage, court deadlines, immigration strategy, or professional dispatch to the right authority. |
| SCHSCP09 | What records should be created? | Save confirmation, time, location, provider, requirements, forms, costs, and follow-up notes. |
| SCHSCP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce scheduling brief, booking checklist, appointment request, or calendar intent note. |

## AVAILC - Availability And Constraint Matching

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| AVAILC01 | What time windows are possible? | List preferred dates, unavailable blocks, work hours, school hours, caregiving, sleep, and travel buffers. |
| AVAILC02 | What urgency level applies? | Mark same day, this week, before deadline, routine, seasonal, or flexible. |
| AVAILC03 | What duration is needed? | Include appointment length, forms, arrival buffer, travel, waiting, recovery, and post-visit tasks. |
| AVAILC04 | What participant calendars must align? | Compare user, family, provider, caregiver, interpreter, driver, roommate, or team availability. |
| AVAILC05 | What timezone matters? | Convert provider, traveler, remote participant, and daylight-saving time correctly. |
| AVAILC06 | What recurring pattern is needed? | Plan weekly, monthly, quarterly, annual, treatment series, class series, or maintenance schedule. |
| AVAILC07 | What conflict should be avoided? | Avoid overlapping commitments, school pickup, medication timing if user-provided, commute peaks, or major events. |
| AVAILC08 | What tradeoff is acceptable? | Choose earliest versus preferred provider, distance versus cost, solo versus accompanied, or morning versus evening. |
| AVAILC09 | What hold should be placed? | Add tentative calendar block, travel buffer, prep block, reminder, or backup option. |
| AVAILC10 | What availability artifact is required? | Produce availability table, option shortlist, conflict map, or calendar hold plan. |

## FINDPR - Provider Venue Or Slot Search

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FINDPR01 | What provider, venue, or office should be searched? | Identify clinic, repair service, school office, restaurant, agency, salon, venue, advisor, or service provider. |
| FINDPR02 | What eligibility or fit criteria matter? | Check location, service type, insurance if user-provided, language, accessibility, price, reviews, and availability. |
| FINDPR03 | What official source should be used? | Prefer provider website, booking portal, official phone, referral list, venue page, or verified marketplace. |
| FINDPR04 | What slot information is required? | Capture date, time, duration, provider, location, price, cancellation rule, and preparation requirements. |
| FINDPR05 | What comparison criteria rank options? | Compare urgency, distance, cost, expertise, reliability, accessibility, reviews, and schedule fit. |
| FINDPR06 | What access issue blocks booking? | Note login, referral, payment method, membership, insurance card, ID, verification code, or phone-only booking. |
| FINDPR07 | What scam or wrong-source risk exists? | Verify domains, phone numbers, addresses, payment requests, impersonation, and too-good-to-be-true slots. |
| FINDPR08 | What backup provider is needed? | Keep alternate slot, second venue, different office, waitlist, or walk-in option. |
| FINDPR09 | What search should be stopped? | Stop when good-enough slot exists, criteria are met, or search cost exceeds benefit. |
| FINDPR10 | What search artifact is required? | Produce slot shortlist, provider comparison, booking source log, or option decision table. |

| WAITLT02 | What waitlist information is required? | Provide contact, preferred windows, service type, flexibility, referral, and cancellation readiness. |
| WAITLT03 | How should openings be monitored? | Check portal, email, phone, app, text alerts, office callback, or periodic search. |
| WAITLT04 | What response speed is needed? | Set fast-reply plan for limited slots, callback window, and pre-approved schedule options. |
| WAITLT05 | What backup appointment should be kept? | Maintain good-enough slot while waiting for better provider, sooner date, or better location. |
| WAITLT06 | What tradeoff decides slot acceptance? | Compare earlier date, provider fit, travel, cost, preparation time, and cancellation consequences. |
| WAITLT07 | What waitlist communication is appropriate? | Send polite check-in, updated availability, urgency context, or withdrawal note. |
| WAITLT08 | What waitlist record should be saved? | Keep date joined, staff contact, priority note, portal status, and promised callback. |
| WAITLT09 | When should monitoring stop? | Stop after booked slot, deadline passed, alternative chosen, or waitlist no longer useful. |
| WAITLT10 | What waitlist artifact is required? | Produce waitlist tracker, monitoring cadence, slot decision rule, or backup plan. |
