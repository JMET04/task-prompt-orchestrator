# Professional Workflow Personal Technology Devices Smart Home Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle personal technology, computers, phones, tablets, peripherals, home networks, Wi-Fi, smart home devices, account setup, subscriptions, backups, device migration, troubleshooting, repair, privacy/security settings, family tech support, digital media devices, or home automation routines. This matrix supports organization, safe setup, and troubleshooting coordination; it does not replace manufacturer support, cybersecurity incident response, licensed electrical work, or legal/privacy advice.

## Personal Technology Scope And Safety Boundary

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| TECHSCOPE01 | What personal technology area is in scope? | Define computer, phone, tablet, printer, router, smart home, streaming, gaming, wearable, storage, account, or family tech setup. |
| TECHSCOPE02 | Who uses or owns the technology? | Identify individual, family, child, elder, roommate, employee-owned personal device, guest, caregiver, or shared household. |
| TECHSCOPE03 | What outcome is needed? | State setup, migration, troubleshooting, privacy hardening, backup, repair, purchase, automation, training, or cleanup outcome. |
| TECHSCOPE04 | What access and permission boundary applies? | Confirm user authority, avoid bypassing accounts, respect privacy, and require the user to complete passwords, MFA, payments, or consent. |
| TECHSCOPE05 | What devices and services are involved? | Inventory device model, OS, app, account, network, subscription, accessory, cloud service, and warranty status. |
| TECHSCOPE06 | What risk level exists? | Flag data loss, security compromise, financial account access, child safety, medical device link, smart lock, camera, or electrical risk. |
| TECHSCOPE07 | What source of truth should be used? | Prefer device settings, official support pages, account portals, receipts, warranty docs, and user-confirmed observations. |
| TECHSCOPE08 | What must not be exposed? | Protect passwords, recovery codes, private photos, messages, health data, financial data, location, and children's data. |
| TECHSCOPE09 | What fallback is acceptable? | Define manual workaround, safe mode, offline use, backup device, manufacturer support, repair shop, or replacement option. |
| TECHSCOPE10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce tech inventory, issue brief, permission note, risk boundary, or support handoff packet. |
