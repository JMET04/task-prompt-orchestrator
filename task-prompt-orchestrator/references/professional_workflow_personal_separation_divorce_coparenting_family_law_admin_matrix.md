# Professional Workflow Personal Separation Divorce Coparenting Family Law Admin Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle personal separation, divorce administration, co-parenting coordination, custody or visitation records, parenting plans, child support or spousal support records, family court notices, mediation preparation, household separation logistics, communication logs, property/debt inventory, child wellbeing handoffs, safety-aware separation planning, court-order tracking, or recurring family-law admin review.

Use this for personal organization, safety-aware planning, deadline tracking, document gathering, communication drafts, child-focused handoff sheets, and professional handoff preparation. Do not use it to provide legal advice, custody strategy, divorce strategy, financial advice, therapy, crisis counseling, child-protection decisions, domestic-violence services, or emergency guidance, and do not replace an attorney, court, mediator, custody evaluator, therapist, financial advisor, domestic-violence advocate, child-protection agency, school official, or emergency service.

## SEPSCP - Separation Divorce Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SEPSCP01 | What family-law admin situation is being organized? | Define separation, divorce, co-parenting, custody schedule, support records, mediation, court date, safety planning, or post-order admin. |
| SEPSCP02 | Who is included in the family system? | Map adults, children, stepchildren, dependents, caregivers, attorneys, mediators, schools, clinicians, and trusted support. |
| SEPSCP03 | What jurisdiction or official process appears? | Capture court, county, state/province, agency, mediator, legal aid, or attorney without interpreting law. |
| SEPSCP04 | What documents or orders already exist? | Inventory petitions, notices, temporary orders, agreements, parenting plan, support orders, financial forms, and mediation notes. |
| SEPSCP05 | What deadline or event is driving the workflow? | Track response deadline, hearing, mediation, disclosure due date, exchange time, payment date, school deadline, or move date. |
| SEPSCP06 | What safety or coercion concern exists? | Flag violence, threats, stalking, coercive control, child safety, unsafe exchanges, weapon concern, or emergency need for qualified help. |
| SEPSCP07 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect addresses, child details, financials, court records, health records, school records, attorney communications, and safety plans. |
| SEPSCP08 | What support roles are involved? | Identify attorney, mediator, advocate, therapist, financial professional, school counselor, childcare provider, and trusted helper. |
| SEPSCP09 | What should not be decided here? | Do not decide legal rights, custody, support, safety risk, property division, settlement, or court strategy. |
| SEPSCP10 | What scope artifact should be produced? | Create family-law admin brief, deadline map, document inventory, safety boundary note, or professional handoff packet. |

| SAFSEP04 | What digital safety needs review? | Organize device access, shared accounts, location sharing, passwords, cloud photos, car apps, smart home, and financial accounts. |
| SAFSEP05 | What physical access should be documented? | Track keys, locks, garage codes, alarm codes, vehicle access, documents, valuables, and safe storage. |
| SAFSEP06 | What child exchange safety issue exists? | Prepare neutral locations, third-party handoff, school pickup rules, written confirmations, and emergency contacts for professional review. |
| SAFSEP07 | What confidential address or contact issue exists? | Flag address confidentiality, PO box, school records, court filings, and safe notification methods for qualified guidance. |
| SAFSEP08 | What support network should know? | Identify trusted people who can help with transportation, childcare, check-ins, emergency contacts, and documentation. |
