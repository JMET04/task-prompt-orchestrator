# Professional Workflow Sports Fitness Recreation Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle sports participation, fitness programming, recreational activities, coaching support, team operations, classes, clubs, leagues, competitions, outdoor recreation, equipment, facility readiness, participant safety, member experience, or performance review. This matrix supports planning and operations; it does not replace medical advice, emergency services, certified coaching judgment, or local safety rules.

## Sports Fitness And Recreation Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SPRTSCOPE01 | What activity or sport is in scope? | Define sport, fitness class, outdoor activity, recreation program, league, club, camp, tournament, or individual training. |
| SPRTSCOPE02 | Who participates? | Identify beginner, youth, adult, older adult, athlete, casual participant, coach, parent, volunteer, spectator, or member. |
| SPRTSCOPE03 | What outcome is desired? | State participation, skill development, health routine, competition readiness, social connection, event delivery, or program retention. |
| SPRTSCOPE04 | What risk and safety boundary applies? | Route injuries, medical conditions, emergencies, and high-risk activities to qualified professionals and official rules. |
| SPRTSCOPE05 | What venue or environment matters? | Capture gym, field, court, pool, trail, road, studio, home, school, workplace, community center, or travel venue. |
| SPRTSCOPE06 | What season or cadence applies? | Define single session, weekly class, training block, season, tournament, camp, challenge, or annual club cycle. |
| SPRTSCOPE07 | What resources are available? | Inventory coaches, staff, volunteers, facilities, equipment, budget, registration tools, transport, and communication channels. |
| SPRTSCOPE08 | What inclusion requirement matters? | Account for accessibility, age, ability, language, culture, cost, gender safety, adaptive equipment, and beginner confidence. |
| SPRTSCOPE09 | What rules or standards govern the activity? | Identify official sport rules, facility policies, league bylaws, safeguarding, insurance, permits, and local regulations. |
| SPRTSCOPE10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce activity brief, participant profile, safety boundary, resource map, or program charter. |

## Fitness Goal Training And Progress Planning

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| TRAINPLAN01 | What training goal is being planned? | Define endurance, strength, flexibility, skill, mobility, recovery, competition prep, consistency, or general fitness. |
| TRAINPLAN02 | What baseline is known? | Capture user-provided experience, current routine, constraints, equipment, schedule, injury history if volunteered, and preferences. |
| TRAINPLAN03 | What professional check is needed? | Encourage clinician or qualified trainer review for injury, medical conditions, pregnancy, pain, extreme goals, or major changes. |
| TRAINPLAN04 | What progression should be conservative? | Use gradual workload, rest, warm-up, cooldown, technique focus, and stop signals for pain or concerning symptoms. |
| TRAINPLAN05 | What weekly structure is needed? | Map sessions, rest days, cross-training, skill work, mobility, recovery, and travel or work constraints. |
| TRAINPLAN06 | What tracking metric is useful? | Track attendance, perceived effort, duration, distance, reps, load, skill quality, recovery, mood, or user-selected measures. |
| TRAINPLAN07 | What motivation design helps? | Add cues, social support, accountability, milestone rewards, class booking, environment setup, and low-friction backup sessions. |
| TRAINPLAN08 | What adaptation rule is needed? | Adjust for fatigue, missed sessions, soreness, weather, schedule disruption, equipment gap, or changed goal. |
| TRAINPLAN09 | What overtraining or unsafe signal matters? | Flag pain, dizziness, shortness of breath, severe fatigue, disordered exercise, or compulsive behavior for professional help. |
| TRAINPLAN10 | What training artifact is required? | Produce training calendar, session template, progress tracker, adaptation rule, or trainer-question list. |

| COACHPROG10 | What coaching artifact is required? | Produce practice plan, drill sequence, feedback rubric, cue sheet, or skill progression map. |

| SAFETYREC08 | What communication should be sent? | Draft factual, calm update to participant, guardian, staff, facility, insurer, or league without blame. |
| SAFETYREC09 | What corrective action follows? | Identify hazard fix, retraining, equipment removal, policy update, supervision change, or program pause. |
| SAFETYREC10 | What safety artifact is required? | Produce risk assessment, safety briefing, incident report, safeguarding checklist, or corrective-action log. |
