# Professional Workflow Professional Services Consulting Client Delivery Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle consulting, agencies, advisory work, professional services, client delivery, discovery, proposals, SOWs, workshops, diagnostics, recommendations, implementation support, value realization, account governance, or engagement closeout.

## Professional Services Scope And Engagement Fit

| Code | Question | Prompt/workflow requirement |
|---|---|---|
| PSCSCOPE01 | What client problem is being served? | Define client goal, pain, desired outcome, business context, urgency, and why external help is needed. |
| PSCSCOPE02 | What service type applies? | Classify advisory, strategy, implementation, audit, training, managed service, agency production, research, technical consulting, or fractional leadership. |
| PSCSCOPE03 | Who is the buyer and sponsor? | Identify economic buyer, executive sponsor, day-to-day owner, users, approvers, procurement, legal, finance, and blockers. |
| PSCSCOPE04 | What engagement shape is expected? | Define project, retainer, sprint, assessment, workshop series, implementation phase, support block, or ongoing managed service. |
| PSCSCOPE05 | What outcome will prove value? | Specify decision, savings, revenue, speed, quality, adoption, risk reduction, capability transfer, or concrete deliverable. |
| PSCSCOPE06 | What constraints shape delivery? | Capture budget, timeline, access, data quality, stakeholder availability, internal politics, tools, security, and dependency limits. |
| PSCSCOPE07 | What expertise is required? | Map domain knowledge, technical skills, facilitation, analytics, change management, creative production, legal/compliance, and executive communication. |
| PSCSCOPE08 | What delivery boundary must be set? | Separate advice from execution, strategy from implementation, deliverables from outcomes, and client responsibilities from provider responsibilities. |
| PSCSCOPE09 | What trust or conflict issue exists? | Check conflicts, confidentiality, competitive clients, independence, scope credibility, procurement restrictions, and reputation risk. |
| PSCSCOPE10 | What is out of scope? | Exclude regulated advice, guarantees, unapproved implementation, hidden procurement work, and access to systems or data not authorized by the client. |
