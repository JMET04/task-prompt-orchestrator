# Professional Workflow Household Emergency Preparedness Disaster Readiness Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle household emergency preparedness, disaster readiness, evacuation planning, shelter-in-place preparation, emergency supplies, go-bags, utility shutoff readiness, household communication, pets, important documents, drills, recovery, or recurring preparedness review.

Use this for personal and household readiness, organization, records, communication, and practice. Do not use it to replace emergency services, official evacuation orders, licensed safety inspection, medical treatment, legal advice, structural engineering, hazardous-material response, or public-authority decisions.

## HEMSCP - Household Emergency Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| HEMSCP01 | What household or location is in scope? | Define home, apartment, dorm, elder household, second home, vehicle, workplace-adjacent plan, or temporary lodging. |
| HEMSCP02 | Who must be included? | Identify adults, children, elders, disabled members, visitors, roommates, pets, caregivers, and trusted contacts. |
| HEMSCP03 | What emergency outcome is needed? | Produce readiness audit, supply list, evacuation plan, communication plan, document packet, drill plan, or recovery checklist. |
| HEMSCP04 | What time horizon matters? | Plan immediate response, 24 hours, 72 hours, one week, seasonal risk, travel absence, or long outage. |
| HEMSCP05 | What dependency affects readiness? | Capture medication, power, mobility, oxygen, refrigeration, child care, pet care, transportation, or caregiver access. |
| HEMSCP06 | What housing constraint applies? | Note apartment rules, stairs, elevator, parking, shared utilities, storage limits, landlord rules, or neighborhood access. |
| HEMSCP07 | What official source should guide actions? | Use local emergency management, weather service, fire authority, public health, utility provider, or school notice. |
| HEMSCP08 | What boundary keeps the workflow safe? | Do not override evacuation orders, diagnose injuries, inspect structures, handle hazardous materials, or advise illegal actions. |
| HEMSCP09 | What existing plan or supply exists? | Inventory previous kits, contacts, documents, alerts, insurance records, neighborhood plan, and school plans. |
| HEMSCP10 | What artifact should be produced? | Create household emergency brief, readiness checklist, supply tracker, communication card, or drill schedule. |

## HAZMAP - Household Hazard Map

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| HAZMAP01 | What hazard is most likely? | Identify fire, flood, storm, earthquake, heat, cold, outage, smoke, chemical release, violence, or pandemic-like disruption. |
| HAZMAP02 | What hazard is highest impact? | Prioritize events that threaten life safety, shelter, mobility, medication, communications, money, documents, or pets. |
| HAZMAP03 | What warning signals should be monitored? | Track alerts, weather watches, air quality, utility notices, school alerts, neighborhood messages, and official orders. |
| HAZMAP04 | What room or system is vulnerable? | Review exits, windows, garage, basement, attic, electrical panel, water heater, gas line, roof, and drainage. |
| HAZMAP05 | What seasonal pattern changes risk? | Note wildfire season, hurricane season, freeze season, heat waves, monsoon, school terms, travel, or holidays. |
| HAZMAP06 | What member-specific risk exists? | Capture mobility limits, sensory needs, medications, child age, pregnancy, elder needs, pet species, or language access. |
| HAZMAP07 | What neighborhood risk matters? | Consider evacuation routes, bridges, flood zones, fire zones, crime, road closure, shelter distance, or power reliability. |
| HAZMAP08 | What mitigation can be done now? | Anchor furniture, clear exits, trim vegetation, test alarms, label shutoffs, elevate items, or secure heavy objects. |
| HAZMAP09 | What risk needs professional review? | Route structural, electrical, gas, fire-code, medical-device, mold, asbestos, or hazardous-material issues to experts. |
| HAZMAP10 | What hazard artifact should be produced? | Create hazard map, priority risk list, mitigation queue, alert source list, or seasonal readiness plan. |

## HOMEKIT - Home Emergency Supplies

| HOMEKIT01 | What core supply category is needed? | List water, food, light, radio, batteries, first aid, sanitation, warmth, cooling, tools, and cash. |
| HOMEKIT02 | What quantity target applies? | Estimate by people, pets, days, climate, storage space, medical needs, and official local guidance. |
| HOMEKIT03 | What supply is already available? | Inventory pantry, batteries, flashlights, chargers, first aid, blankets, masks, tools, and hygiene items. |
| HOMEKIT04 | What expiration or rotation rule applies? | Track food, water, medicine, batteries, filters, sanitizer, documents, and seasonal clothing. |
| HOMEKIT05 | What storage location is best? | Choose accessible, dry, cool, labeled, child-safe, pet-safe, and exit-near locations. |
| HOMEKIT06 | What special-needs supply is required? | Include medication support, mobility supplies, communication aids, diapers, formula, glasses, hearing batteries, or comfort items. |
| HOMEKIT07 | What sanitation supply is needed? | Prepare wipes, bags, toilet options, gloves, disinfectant, menstrual supplies, soap, and trash control. |
| HOMEKIT08 | What cooking or heating boundary applies? | Avoid unsafe indoor combustion; route generator, fuel, and heater questions to official safety guidance. |
| HOMEKIT09 | What replenishment trigger should be used? | Set calendar checks, last-one-open rules, seasonal resets, post-use replacement, and expiration alerts. |
| HOMEKIT10 | What kit artifact should be produced? | Create home kit checklist, inventory tracker, restock list, location map, or rotation calendar. |

## GOBAHG - Go Bag And Grab List

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| GOBAHG01 | Who needs a go bag? | Prepare for each adult, child, elder, caregiver, pet, and vehicle where appropriate. |
| GOBAHG02 | What must be grabbed if time is short? | List IDs, medications, keys, wallet, phone, charger, glasses, documents, pet leash, and critical devices. |
| GOBAHG03 | What should be pre-packed? | Include clothes, hygiene, copies, water, snacks, light, first aid, comfort item, power bank, and contact card. |
| GOBAHG04 | What bag weight or mobility limit applies? | Match bag size to age, strength, stairs, transit, wheelchair, stroller, or vehicle plan. |
| GOBAHG05 | What climate or season changes contents? | Rotate coats, rain gear, heat protection, sunscreen, blankets, gloves, and footwear. |
| GOBAHG06 | What document copies belong in the bag? | Include redacted IDs, insurance cards, medication list, contacts, pet records, and emergency plan. |
| GOBAHG07 | What item should not be packed? | Exclude unsafe fuel, expired medicine, excessive cash, sensitive originals unless needed, and heavy low-value items. |
| GOBAHG08 | What location makes the bag useful? | Store near exit, bedroom, vehicle, workplace, school pickup area, or caregiver handoff point. |
| GOBAHG09 | What review cadence applies? | Check after seasonal change, growth in child size, medication changes, battery expiry, and household move. |
| GOBAHG10 | What go-bag artifact should be produced? | Create bag checklist, grab list, location map, packing label, or review reminder. |

## EVACPL - Evacuation Plan

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| EVACPL01 | What evacuation trigger applies? | Define official order, smoke, flood warning, fire, gas smell, structural concern, violence, or self-chosen safety threshold. |
| EVACPL02 | What exits and routes exist? | Map doors, windows, stairs, elevators, garage, vehicle route, walking route, transit route, and backup route. |
| EVACPL03 | Where should the household meet? | Choose near-home meeting point, out-of-area contact, shelter, hotel, family home, school pickup, or pet-friendly option. |
| EVACPL04 | What transportation is available? | Plan car, rideshare, neighbor, public transit, walking, accessible transport, school bus, or caregiver vehicle. |
| EVACPL05 | What member needs assistance leaving? | Assign help for children, elders, disabled members, pets, visitors, heavy doors, stairs, or medical equipment. |
| EVACPL06 | What should be done before leaving if safe? | Grab bag, documents, pets, shut windows, unplug devices, check stove, lock doors, and follow official guidance. |
| EVACPL07 | What should never delay evacuation? | Avoid searching for replaceable items, arguing over packing, unsafe shutoff attempts, or returning after leaving. |
| EVACPL08 | What destination requirement matters? | Check accessibility, pet policy, medication refrigeration, language support, distance, cost, and communications. |
| EVACPL09 | What proof or record is needed later? | Save departure time, photos, alerts, receipts, hotel records, damage photos, and contact attempts. |
| EVACPL10 | What evacuation artifact should be produced? | Create route map, grab list, meet-up card, destination shortlist, or evacuation decision tree. |

## COMMSP - Communication And Contact Plan

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| COMMSP01 | Who needs to be contacted? | List household members, out-of-area contact, school, caregivers, neighbors, work, landlord, and emergency contacts. |
| COMMSP02 | What contact method should be primary? | Use phone, text, group chat, email, radio, app alert, physical note, or out-of-area relay. |
| COMMSP03 | What backup method is needed? | Prepare printed contacts, wallet cards, offline maps, paper notes, radio channel, and meeting points. |
| COMMSP04 | What message template helps? | Draft safe/need help/evacuating/sheltering/where we are/please pick up messages. |
| COMMSP05 | What check-in cadence applies? | Set when leaving, arriving, sheltering, after alert updates, and after official all-clear. |
| COMMSP06 | What school or caregiver communication matters? | Include pickup rules, reunification site, authorized adults, medical info, and notification channels. |
| COMMSP07 | What language or accessibility need exists? | Prepare translated cards, visual instructions, large print, AAC support, caption-friendly channels, or interpreter contacts. |
| COMMSP08 | What privacy boundary applies? | Avoid publicly sharing empty-home status, exact safe location, minors' details, or sensitive medical information. |
| COMMSP09 | What communication failure should be planned for? | Plan no cell service, dead battery, overloaded network, separated household, and lost phone. |
| COMMSP10 | What communication artifact should be produced? | Create contact card, message templates, group plan, call tree, or offline contact sheet. |

## UTLSHF - Utility Shutoff And Home Systems

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| UTLSHF01 | What home system needs readiness? | Identify gas, electricity, water, HVAC, generator, sump pump, alarm, garage door, or medical power equipment. |
| UTLSHF02 | Where is the shutoff or control? | Document panel, valve, breaker, meter, main water, appliance shutoff, alarm panel, and access tool location. |
| UTLSHF03 | Who is allowed and able to operate it? | Assign trained adult, landlord, utility, contractor, building manager, or emergency responder. |
| UTLSHF04 | What official instruction applies? | Follow utility provider, fire department, landlord, product manual, or emergency authority guidance. |
| UTLSHF05 | What tool or label is needed? | Prepare wrench, flashlight, labels, photos, instructions, spare keys, and access notes. |
| UTLSHF06 | What should not be attempted? | Avoid unsafe gas handling, wet electrical panels, structural entry, generator misuse, or turning utilities back on without guidance. |
| UTLSHF07 | What outage workaround is needed? | Plan charging, refrigeration, heat/cooling, water storage, communication, medical devices, and food safety. |
| UTLSHF08 | What routine test or maintenance applies? | Test alarms, replace batteries, inspect extinguishers, check sump pump, label breakers, and review manuals. |
| UTLSHF09 | What service record should be kept? | Store inspection dates, repairs, manuals, utility contacts, warranties, and vendor notes. |
| UTLSHF10 | What utility artifact should be produced? | Create shutoff map, outage plan, label sheet, maintenance calendar, or vendor contact card. |

| DOCEMR09 | What legal or insurance boundary applies? | Do not interpret coverage, benefits, liability, or legal rights; route to insurer, agency, or professional. |

## DRILLH - Household Drills And Practice

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| DRILLH01 | What drill should be practiced? | Choose fire exit, earthquake drop-cover-hold, evacuation loading, shelter-in-place, communication check, or go-bag grab. |
| DRILLH02 | Who should participate? | Include adults, children, elders, pets as feasible, caregivers, roommates, and trusted helpers. |
| DRILLH03 | What scenario should be simulated? | Practice night alarm, blocked exit, no phone, car unavailable, child at school, pet hiding, or power outage. |
| DRILLH04 | What timing target matters? | Measure exit time, contact time, bag grab time, route time, or setup time without creating unsafe pressure. |
| DRILLH05 | What should be explained beforehand? | Teach roles, exits, meeting point, stop signal, child-friendly framing, and no-panic expectations. |
| DRILLH06 | What safety limit applies during practice? | Avoid dangerous shutoffs, road risk, smoke exposure, locked exits, or frightening vulnerable members. |
| DRILLH07 | What observation should be recorded? | Note confusion, missing supplies, blocked path, unclear roles, inaccessible item, and communication gap. |
| DRILLH08 | What improvement follows the drill? | Update labels, routes, supplies, contacts, roles, bag placement, or instructions. |
| DRILLH09 | What cadence is useful? | Schedule seasonal, annual, after move, after household change, after school change, or after new risk alert. |
| DRILLH10 | What drill artifact should be produced? | Create drill script, scorecard, improvement list, role card, or next-practice reminder. |

## SHELTH - Shelter In Place

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SHELTH01 | What shelter-in-place trigger applies? | Define storm, smoke, chemical release, security threat, public health alert, extreme heat/cold, or road closure. |
| SHELTH02 | What room or area is safest? | Choose interior room, basement, upper floor, cool room, warm room, sealed room, or accessible room. |
| SHELTH03 | What supplies should be in reach? | Stage water, food, medications, lights, chargers, radio, sanitation, pet supplies, and comfort items. |
| SHELTH04 | What air or ventilation issue matters? | Plan masks, filters, window closure, HVAC setting, smoke exposure, or official air-quality guidance. |
| SHELTH05 | What communication should continue? | Monitor official alerts, check in with household, school, neighbors, caregivers, and out-of-area contact. |
| SHELTH06 | What household member needs extra support? | Plan for children, elders, disabled members, anxiety, sensory needs, medication, temperature sensitivity, or pets. |
| SHELTH07 | What boredom or stress support helps? | Prepare books, games, headphones, comfort items, routine, quiet space, and child-friendly explanations. |
| SHELTH08 | What should not be done while sheltering? | Avoid unsafe generator use, unnecessary travel, rumor sharing, opening sealed areas, or ignoring official updates. |
| SHELTH09 | What all-clear or exit check is needed? | Confirm official update, home safety, utilities, air quality, road status, and school/work notices. |
| SHELTH10 | What shelter artifact should be produced? | Create shelter room checklist, supply map, official alert list, or household instruction card. |

## RECOVH - Household Recovery

| RECOVH02 | What safety check comes first? | Verify official all-clear, structural safety, utilities, water safety, food safety, injuries, and hazardous conditions. |

| RECOVH04 | What contact sequence is needed? | Contact household, landlord, insurer, utility, school, employer, repair vendors, medical provider, or local agency. |
| RECOVH05 | What expense tracking is required? | Log temporary lodging, food, transport, repairs, supplies, medical costs, pet care, and replacement purchases. |
| RECOVH06 | What cleanup boundary applies? | Avoid mold, sewage, gas, electrical, structural, chemical, or biohazard cleanup without appropriate professionals. |
| RECOVH07 | What document or claim packet is needed? | Organize photos, inventory, receipts, timeline, contacts, estimates, and official notices without interpreting coverage. |
| RECOVH08 | What household routine must be restored? | Rebuild medication, school, work, pet care, meals, sleep, transport, bills, and communication routines. |
| RECOVH09 | What emotional support or escalation may help? | Note stress, grief, child reactions, caregiver strain, and route crisis or clinical needs to appropriate support. |
| RECOVH10 | What recovery artifact should be produced? | Create recovery action plan, damage log, expense tracker, contact tracker, or restoration checklist. |
