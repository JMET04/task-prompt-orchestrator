# Professional Workflow Nonprofit Fundraising Community Programs Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle nonprofit, NGO, charity, foundation, philanthropy, fundraising, donor stewardship, grants, volunteer programs, beneficiary support, community services, advocacy, impact measurement, restricted funds, board governance, or nonprofit operations.

## Nonprofit Mission And Scope

| Code | Question | Prompt/workflow requirement |
|---|---|---|
| NPOSCOPE01 | What nonprofit mission is being served? | Define mission, community need, beneficiary group, geography, values, public benefit, and theory of change. |
| NPOSCOPE02 | What organizational type applies? | Identify charity, NGO, foundation, association, mutual aid group, fiscal sponsor, school program, faith-based group, or social enterprise. |
| NPOSCOPE03 | Who are the primary stakeholders? | Map beneficiaries, donors, funders, volunteers, staff, board, partners, regulators, community leaders, and public audiences. |
| NPOSCOPE04 | What program or campaign is in scope? | Define service, advocacy, fundraising appeal, grant project, volunteer initiative, event, emergency response, or capacity-building effort. |
| NPOSCOPE05 | What community context matters? | Capture lived experience, language, access barriers, trust, cultural fit, local institutions, safety, and historical harm. |
| NPOSCOPE06 | What outcomes must be protected? | Specify impact, dignity, equity, privacy, service continuity, donor trust, compliance, financial stewardship, and community accountability. |
| NPOSCOPE07 | What constraints shape the work? | Record budget, staff capacity, volunteer capacity, grant restrictions, deadlines, eligibility, reporting duties, and partner commitments. |
| NPOSCOPE08 | What ethical boundaries apply? | Avoid exploitative storytelling, coercive fundraising, unsafe services, inflated claims, privacy breaches, and token community engagement. |
| NPOSCOPE09 | What evidence is already available? | Gather needs assessments, prior reports, donor history, grant requirements, program data, testimonials, policies, and financial records. |
| NPOSCOPE10 | What is out of scope? | Exclude legal, tax, clinical, immigration, financial, or emergency advice unless qualified review and escalation are part of the workflow. |

## Impact Measurement Learning And Reporting

| Code | Question | Prompt/workflow requirement |
|---|---|---|
| IMPACTNP01 | What impact question must be answered? | Define outcome, population, timeframe, comparison point, decision use, reporting audience, and uncertainty. |
| IMPACTNP02 | What theory of change applies? | Connect inputs, activities, outputs, short-term outcomes, long-term outcomes, assumptions, and external factors. |
| IMPACTNP03 | What indicators are meaningful? | Select output, outcome, equity, quality, reach, cost, satisfaction, retention, referral, and systems-change indicators. |
| IMPACTNP04 | What data collection method is ethical? | Use surveys, interviews, case records, attendance, partner data, observation, administrative data, or storytelling with consent. |
| IMPACTNP05 | What baseline or target is needed? | Establish current state, target, comparison group, historical trend, benchmark, or realistic directional goal. |
| IMPACTNP06 | What analysis should be performed? | Segment by population, geography, access barrier, service type, dosage, time, partner, and equity dimension. |
| IMPACTNP07 | What qualitative evidence is needed? | Capture participant voice, case examples, community feedback, partner observations, and unintended outcomes responsibly. |
| IMPACTNP08 | What reporting format is required? | Produce dashboard, grant report, annual report, donor impact note, board memo, public infographic, or learning brief. |
| IMPACTNP09 | What limitations must be stated? | Note sample bias, missing data, attribution limits, confounders, privacy suppression, and uncertainty. |
| IMPACTNP10 | How should learning change the program? | Convert findings into program changes, funding asks, staffing decisions, partner actions, and next measurement plan. |
