# Professional Workflow Home Improvement DIY Small Repairs Projects Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle home improvement, DIY projects, small repairs, renter-friendly changes, measuring, materials, tools, safety checks, project planning, permits or landlord boundaries, budgets, execution, cleanup, helper/vendor decisions, documentation, or project review.

Use this for personal planning and low-risk household project coordination. Do not use it to replace licensed electrical, plumbing, gas, structural, roofing, hazardous-material, fire-safety, code-compliance, engineering, landlord/legal, or emergency-service decisions.

## DIYSCP - DIY Project Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| DIYSCP01 | What project is being considered? | Define repair, install, decorate, organize, paint, assemble, mount, patch, replace, or improve. |
| DIYSCP02 | What space is affected? | Identify room, wall, floor, ceiling, cabinet, fixture, outdoor area, rental unit, or shared space. |
| DIYSCP03 | What outcome is desired? | Clarify function, safety, appearance, storage, comfort, accessibility, energy use, or resale/rental readiness. |
| DIYSCP04 | Who is affected? | Include household members, children, pets, roommates, landlord, neighbors, guests, or future users. |
| DIYSCP05 | What property boundary applies? | Note owned home, rental, HOA, dorm, workplace housing, shared building, or temporary lodging. |
| DIYSCP06 | What skill level is realistic? | Match beginner, practiced DIY, helper-assisted, tool-limited, time-limited, or professional-needed. |
| DIYSCP07 | What risk level exists? | Flag electrical, plumbing, gas, structural, ladder, glass, mold, asbestos, lead, fire, or water damage risk. |
| DIYSCP08 | What existing evidence should be gathered? | Collect photos, measurements, manuals, receipts, product links, landlord notes, and damage history. |
| DIYSCP09 | What should stay out of scope? | Exclude unsafe, regulated, permitted, warranty-voiding, or irreversible work unless handled by proper authority. |
| DIYSCP10 | What artifact should be produced? | Create project brief, scope boundary, materials list, safety checklist, or go/no-go note. |

## FIXREQ - Repair Need Definition

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FIXREQ01 | What problem needs fixing? | Describe loose, broken, leaking, squeaking, sticking, stained, cracked, missing, misaligned, or worn item. |
| FIXREQ02 | What symptom is observed? | Record when it happens, where, severity, sound, smell, movement, moisture, heat, or visible damage. |
| FIXREQ03 | What caused or worsened it? | Note wear, impact, moisture, installation error, child/pet use, weather, overload, or unknown cause. |
| FIXREQ04 | What happens if ignored? | Assess inconvenience, safety, further damage, cost growth, access problem, pest risk, or cosmetic-only impact. |
| FIXREQ05 | What quick stabilization is safe? | Use temporary label, stop use, contain water, move items, block access, or protect surface without risky repair. |
| FIXREQ06 | What diagnosis should not be guessed? | Avoid hidden wiring, gas, structural, mold, sewer, electrical, or load-bearing conclusions. |
| FIXREQ07 | What reference can clarify the issue? | Use manual, manufacturer guide, prior repair note, building rule, landlord process, or safe DIY source. |
| FIXREQ08 | What photo or measurement is needed? | Capture closeup, context, dimensions, part number, screw type, wall type, and damage extent. |
| FIXREQ09 | What decision is needed? | Choose DIY, monitor, replace, hire professional, ask landlord, claim warranty, or stop project. |
| FIXREQ10 | What repair-definition artifact should be produced? | Create symptom log, repair brief, triage note, or professional handoff packet. |

## SAFEDY - DIY Safety Boundary

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SAFEDY01 | What safety hazard is present? | Identify electricity, gas, water, height, blade, dust, fumes, heat, heavy object, or trip hazard. |
| SAFEDY02 | What professional boundary applies? | Route electrical, plumbing, gas, structural, roofing, asbestos, lead, mold, and fire-system work to experts. |
| SAFEDY03 | What personal protective equipment is needed? | Select gloves, eye protection, mask, respirator, knee pads, hearing protection, and closed-toe shoes. |
| SAFEDY04 | What ventilation or dust control is required? | Open windows, use fan safely, isolate area, wet-wipe dust, cover vents, and protect HVAC. |
| SAFEDY05 | What ladder or height rule applies? | Check stable surface, helper, three points of contact, no overreach, and no unsafe roof work. |
| SAFEDY06 | What child or pet control is needed? | Set exclusion zone, tool storage, cleanup check, noise warning, and safe temporary access route. |
| SAFEDY07 | What fire or heat risk exists? | Manage rags, solvents, heat guns, overloaded outlets, sparks, batteries, and cooking-adjacent work. |
| SAFEDY08 | What emergency stop condition applies? | Stop for smell of gas, sparks, active leak, structural movement, injury, dizziness, or hidden hazard. |
| SAFEDY09 | What disposal safety applies? | Follow rules for paint, solvents, batteries, blades, dust, broken glass, and chemicals. |
| SAFEDY10 | What safety artifact should be produced? | Create safety checklist, stop-work rule, PPE list, or household warning note. |

| PLANDY07 | What quality checkpoint applies? | Check level, fit, strength, finish, leak-free state, door swing, and user comfort. |

| PLANDY10 | What plan artifact should be produced? | Create step plan, timeline, dependency list, or checkpoint checklist. |

| EXECDY08 | What quality touch matters? | Smooth edge, clean line, tight hardware, matching finish, consistent gap, and stable placement. |
| EXECDY09 | What interim state must be safe? | Cover holes, secure tools, restore access, label wet paint, and block unsafe area. |
| EXECDY10 | What execution artifact should be produced? | Create work log, photo set, issue note, or completion checklist. |

## HELPRD - Helper Vendor Decision

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| HELPRD01 | When is help needed? | Identify heavy lifting, ladder work, unfamiliar tool, deadline, accessibility, or safety concern. |
| HELPRD02 | What type of help fits? | Choose household helper, knowledgeable friend, handyman, licensed trade, landlord, manufacturer, or store service. |
| HELPRD03 | What must be licensed or specialized? | Route electrical, plumbing, gas, HVAC, structural, roof, pest, mold, asbestos, lead, or fire systems. |
| HELPRD04 | What information should be shared? | Provide photos, measurements, symptoms, constraints, materials, access notes, and desired outcome. |
| HELPRD05 | What quote detail matters? | Compare scope, labor, materials, timeline, license, insurance, warranty, cleanup, and disposal. |
| HELPRD06 | What access preparation is needed? | Schedule time, parking, pets, adult presence, keys, clear path, and building rules. |
| HELPRD07 | What communication boundary applies? | Ask clear questions, confirm price, avoid unsafe instructions, and keep written records. |
| HELPRD08 | What acceptance check is needed? | Verify function, finish, cleanup, receipts, warranty, and next maintenance step before final payment. |
| HELPRD09 | What vendor risk should be avoided? | Watch vague scope, pressure, no license when required, cash-only demand, and no written record. |
| HELPRD10 | What helper artifact should be produced? | Create helper brief, quote comparison, appointment checklist, or acceptance log. |
