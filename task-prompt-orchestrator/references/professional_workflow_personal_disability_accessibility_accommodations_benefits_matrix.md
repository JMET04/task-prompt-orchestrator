# Professional Workflow Personal Disability Accessibility Accommodations Benefits Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle personal disability administration, accessibility needs, accommodation requests, disability benefit applications, medical documentation packets, workplace accommodations, school accommodations, housing accessibility, transportation accessibility, assistive technology or mobility aids, caregiver or support services, denial or appeal organization, privacy boundaries, or recurring disability-support review.

Use this for personal organization, forms, deadline tracking, documentation checklists, communication drafts, access planning, support coordination, and professional handoff preparation. Do not use it to diagnose disability, determine eligibility, certify limitations, provide medical/legal/employment/education/benefits advice, appeal as counsel, choose treatment or devices, or replace clinicians, therapists, disability-rights advocates, benefits agencies, schools, employers, housing providers, transportation authorities, attorneys, insurers, or emergency services.

## DISASC - Disability Access Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| DISASC01 | What disability or access-support situation is being organized? | Define accommodation, benefits, school support, work support, housing access, transport access, assistive tech, care services, or review. |
| DISASC02 | Who needs support or authorization? | Identify self, child, student, employee, applicant, tenant, traveler, caregiver, guardian, advocate, or authorized helper. |
| DISASC03 | What functional barriers should be described? | Capture mobility, vision, hearing, speech, cognitive, mental health, chronic illness, fatigue, pain, sensory, or episodic barriers as user-provided. |
| DISASC04 | What setting is affected? | Note home, workplace, school, healthcare, transit, public venue, travel, digital service, benefits office, or community program. |
| DISASC05 | What urgency or risk level exists? | Separate routine planning, expiring benefit, missed service, unsafe access barrier, school/work deadline, or urgent health/safety need. |
| DISASC06 | What official source governs the request? | Track agency, school office, HR, housing provider, transit agency, insurer, clinician, accessibility office, or benefits portal. |
| DISASC07 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect diagnosis, medical records, disability status, benefits IDs, school records, employment files, finances, and caregiver authority. |
| DISASC08 | What support network is involved? | Map clinician, therapist, caseworker, advocate, school coordinator, HR, landlord, caregiver, family, or peer support. |
| DISASC09 | What should not be determined here? | Do not decide eligibility, legal entitlement, medical necessity, work capacity, disability status, or best treatment/device. |
| DISASC10 | What scope artifact should be produced? | Create disability-support brief, barrier map, contact sheet, privacy note, or professional handoff packet. |
