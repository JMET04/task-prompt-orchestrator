# Professional Workflow Personal Insurance Policies Claims Admin Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle personal insurance policy inventory, coverage-summary organization, premiums, billing, renewals, claims intake, evidence packets, adjuster or insurer communication, denials and appeal organization, health insurance EOBs, life insurance beneficiary records, travel insurance, or recurring insurance review.

Use this for personal organization, records, reminders, evidence preparation, communication drafts, and decision-support checklists. Do not use it to provide insurance, legal, tax, financial, medical, actuarial, underwriting, coverage, claims-liability, fraud, or regulatory advice, and do not replace a licensed agent, broker, attorney, tax professional, clinician, insurer, adjuster, regulator, or plan administrator.

## PINSCP - Personal Insurance Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PINSCP01 | What insurance situation is being managed? | Define policy inventory, quote comparison, renewal, premium bill, claim, denial, evidence packet, or review. |
| PINSCP02 | Which policy type is involved? | Identify auto, homeowners, renters, condo, health, dental, vision, life, disability, umbrella, travel, pet, device, or warranty-like coverage. |
| PINSCP03 | Who is covered or affected? | Capture policyholder, dependents, beneficiaries, household members, drivers, property occupants, pets, or travelers. |
| PINSCP04 | What record source is authoritative? | Use insurer portal, policy declarations, ID card, benefits booklet, EOB, claim letter, broker email, or employer plan document. |
| PINSCP05 | What deadline or event matters? | Note premium due date, renewal date, claim filing deadline, proof deadline, appeal window, open enrollment, or travel date. |
| PINSCP06 | What personal boundary applies? | Organize facts and questions without interpreting coverage, liability, medical necessity, law, taxes, or claim value. |
| PINSCP07 | What connected life area is affected? | Link to health care, vehicle, home, travel, finance, documents, emergency prep, or estate records. |
| PINSCP08 | What privacy issue exists? | Protect policy numbers, health details, claim files, identity documents, financial data, and beneficiary information. |
| PINSCP09 | What success criteria matter? | Set complete records, on-time payment, submitted claim packet, clear questions, documented communications, or review decision. |
| PINSCP10 | What scope artifact should be produced? | Create insurance admin brief, policy list, claim tracker, renewal checklist, or evidence plan. |

## POLINV - Policy Inventory

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| POLINV01 | Which policies exist? | List insurer, policy type, policy number, named insured, covered item/person, term dates, and portal link. |
| POLINV02 | What documents should be collected? | Gather declarations page, full policy, endorsements, ID cards, benefits summaries, certificates, riders, and notices. |
| POLINV03 | What accounts and contacts matter? | Record insurer portal, agent/broker, employer benefits contact, claims phone, roadside number, and service email. |
| POLINV04 | What covered assets or people need mapping? | Map vehicles, homes, valuables, dependents, pets, trips, devices, or business-use exclusions to policies. |
| POLINV05 | What duplicate or missing coverage should be flagged? | Identify apparent gaps, overlapping policies, old policies, missing ID cards, or unclear named insureds for professional review. |
| POLINV06 | What expiration or renewal dates need reminders? | Add policy term end, renewal notice window, open enrollment, payment schedule, and card replacement dates. |
| POLINV07 | What proof packets should be ready? | Prepare auto ID card, landlord proof, mortgagee/lienholder proof, travel certificate, or employer proof. |
| POLINV08 | What storage system is needed? | Organize digital vault, paper folder, secure backup, naming convention, and emergency access. |
| POLINV09 | What inventory should not include? | Avoid storing unnecessary sensitive medical, financial, or identity details when a pointer is enough. |
| POLINV10 | What inventory artifact should be produced? | Create policy inventory table, document checklist, contact sheet, or coverage map for review. |

## COVSUM - Coverage Summary Organization

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| COVSUM01 | What policy terms need plain-language extraction? | Summarize limits, deductibles, copays, coinsurance, exclusions, riders, waiting periods, and service areas as source-backed notes. |
| COVSUM02 | What should be quoted or referenced exactly? | Preserve policy names, endorsement numbers, claim requirements, deadlines, and insurer wording where needed. |
| COVSUM03 | What question needs a professional answer? | Turn unclear coverage, liability, tax, legal, or medical-necessity issues into questions for insurer, broker, attorney, or clinician. |
| COVSUM04 | What comparison is being made? | Compare policies by source-stated premium, deductible, limit, network, covered item, exclusions, and service process. |
| COVSUM05 | What scenario needs a checklist? | Create source-backed questions for accident, theft, water damage, medical visit, trip cancellation, disability, or death claim. |
| COVSUM06 | What assumption must be labeled? | Mark inferred, missing, stale, unaudited, or professional-review-needed interpretations. |
| COVSUM07 | What coverage language should not be simplified? | Avoid changing legal policy wording where precision matters; link to the authoritative clause. |
| COVSUM08 | What user-facing summary is useful? | Produce short "what to check first" notes without promising coverage or claim outcome. |
| COVSUM09 | What source refresh is needed? | Re-check latest policy documents after renewal, employer plan year change, endorsement, move, or insurer notice. |
| COVSUM10 | What coverage-summary artifact should be produced? | Create coverage summary, question list, comparison table, or source-cited policy digest. |

| PREPAY02 | What payment source is used? | Note bank account, card, payroll deduction, escrow, employer deduction, or third-party billing. |
| PREPAY03 | What reminders are needed? | Set due-date, autopay verification, renewal-premium change, failed-payment, and document-download reminders. |
| PREPAY04 | What proof should be saved? | Store receipts, confirmation numbers, bank records, payroll deduction statements, and insurer payment history. |
| PREPAY05 | What premium change should be reviewed? | Flag increase, discount removal, address/vehicle/driver change, coverage change, or employer contribution change. |
| PREPAY06 | What billing issue should be escalated? | Prepare facts for missed payment, duplicate charge, canceled policy notice, escrow mismatch, or payroll error. |
| PREPAY07 | What budget planning is needed? | Estimate annual premium calendar, monthly cash flow, deductible reserve, and renewal shock buffer. |
| PREPAY08 | What payment security matters? | Avoid sharing full payment details, use trusted portals, and document suspicious billing messages. |
| PREPAY09 | What should not be decided here? | Do not advise whether to change coverage or stop paying; prepare questions and evidence for qualified review. |
| PREPAY10 | What billing artifact should be produced? | Create premium calendar, receipt folder, billing issue packet, or annual insurance cost summary. |

## RENINS - Renewal And Shopping Admin

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RENINS01 | Which policy is renewing? | Capture insurer, term end, renewal offer, premium change, coverage changes, and response deadline. |
| RENINS02 | What life changes affect renewal review? | Note move, new vehicle, renovation, household member, marriage, child, job, health plan year, travel, pet, or valuables. |
| RENINS03 | What documents should be compared? | Compare current declarations, renewal packet, quote, endorsement, benefits summary, and prior premium record. |
| RENINS04 | What questions should be asked? | Prepare questions about stated changes, discounts, deductibles, covered assets, network, riders, and proof requirements. |
| RENINS05 | What quote comparison fields are needed? | Compare source-stated premium, deductible, limits, exclusions, claim process, service contacts, and effective dates. |
| RENINS06 | What deadline protection is needed? | Avoid lapse by tracking response date, cancellation date, proof deadline, and new-policy effective date. |
| RENINS07 | What cancellation or switch records matter? | Save cancellation confirmation, new binder, refund notice, payment stop, and proof to lender/landlord if needed. |
| RENINS08 | What professional handoff may be needed? | Flag complex coverage, business use, high-value property, life/estate questions, tax issues, or legal exposure. |
| RENINS09 | What should not be optimized blindly? | Do not choose only by premium without source-backed review of deductibles, limits, exclusions, and needs. |
| RENINS10 | What renewal artifact should be produced? | Create renewal checklist, quote comparison, question list, or switch/lapse prevention plan. |

| CLMINT02 | Which policy might be relevant? | Identify possible policy and insurer contact without asserting coverage or liability. |
| CLMINT03 | What immediate safety or mitigation matters? | Note emergency services, medical care, property stabilization, theft report, travel assistance, or insurer hotline. |
| CLMINT04 | What claim deadline or notice rule is known? | Extract source-stated reporting deadlines, proof deadlines, appeal windows, and documentation requirements. |

| CLMINT06 | What claim number and contacts should be tracked? | Record claim number, adjuster, representative, phone, email, portal, and next promised action. |
| CLMINT07 | What statement should be prepared carefully? | Draft factual timeline and question list; avoid speculation, unsupported blame, or coverage conclusions. |
| CLMINT08 | What dependent workflows are triggered? | Link repairs, medical bills, police report, travel rebooking, temporary housing, rental car, or receipts. |
| CLMINT09 | What should not be done? | Do not fabricate facts, destroy evidence, admit liability, ignore safety, or rely on unofficial coverage promises. |
| CLMINT10 | What claim-intake artifact should be produced? | Create claim intake form, incident timeline, contact log, or first-call checklist. |

## EVIPKT - Evidence Packet

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| EVIPKT01 | What evidence is required by the insurer or plan? | Extract requested photos, receipts, estimates, bills, reports, forms, statements, tickets, or medical/provider records. |
| EVIPKT02 | What evidence already exists? | Inventory photos, videos, screenshots, emails, invoices, EOBs, police reports, repair estimates, and travel notices. |
| EVIPKT03 | What evidence is missing? | Create a request list for providers, repair shops, police, airline, landlord, employer, or service vendors. |
| EVIPKT04 | How should evidence be organized? | Use claim number, date, document type, source, status, and submitted/not-submitted naming. |
| EVIPKT05 | What sensitive data should be minimized? | Redact unnecessary account numbers, unrelated medical details, identity numbers, and third-party personal data. |
| EVIPKT06 | What chronology helps? | Build timeline of incident, notice, actions taken, expenses, communications, and insurer responses. |
| EVIPKT07 | What proof of submission matters? | Save upload confirmations, mail tracking, email receipts, fax logs, portal screenshots, and representative names. |
| EVIPKT08 | What evidence quality issue exists? | Flag blurry photos, missing dates, inconsistent amounts, unclear source, duplicates, or unsupported claims. |
| EVIPKT09 | What should not be altered? | Do not edit evidence in a misleading way, backdate records, or remove material context. |
| EVIPKT10 | What evidence artifact should be produced? | Create evidence index, claim packet, missing-doc tracker, or submission log. |

| COMINS08 | What escalation path exists? | Identify supervisor, formal appeal, complaint channel, broker support, employer benefits escalation, or regulator info source. |

| HLTHEB06 | What questions should be prepared? | Draft questions for insurer, provider billing office, employer benefits, or pharmacy benefit manager. |
| HLTHEB07 | What record should be saved? | Store EOBs, bills, receipts, payment plans, HSA/FSA receipts, prior auth letters, and call logs. |
| HLTHEB08 | What should not be concluded? | Do not decide medical necessity, diagnosis accuracy, plan legality, or final liability without qualified review. |
| HLTHEB09 | What recurring pattern should be tracked? | Track deductible progress, repeated denial code, out-of-network provider, medication issue, or billing office delay. |
| HLTHEB10 | What health-insurance artifact should be produced? | Create EOB-bill reconciliation, question list, payment tracker, or appeal packet starter. |
