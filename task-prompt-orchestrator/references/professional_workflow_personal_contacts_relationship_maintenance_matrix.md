# Professional Workflow Personal Contacts Relationship Maintenance Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage personal contacts, address books, phone numbers, email addresses, birthdays, anniversaries, social follow-ups, reconnecting, group lists, introductions, privacy, relationship notes, holiday card lists, moving announcements, or contact cleanup. Use this for personal organization, thoughtful communication, and relationship maintenance, not to replace therapy, legal advice, HR processes, stalking/harassment response, formal mediation, sales CRM, or professional public relations.

## CONTSCP - Contact Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CONTSCP01 | What contact set is in scope? | Define family, friends, neighbors, classmates, parents, hobby group, local services, holiday cards, or whole address book. |
| CONTSCP02 | Why is contact work needed now? | Link to move, event, holiday, phone migration, reconnection, duplicate cleanup, caregiving, or social reset. |
| CONTSCP03 | Who owns the contact list? | Identify self, household, family organizer, caregiver, event host, group admin, or shared address book. |
| CONTSCP04 | What outcome is desired? | Choose clean contacts, birthday reminders, invite list, reconnect plan, address update, or group message list. |
| CONTSCP05 | What sensitivity applies? | Note private numbers, addresses, family conflict, children, ex-partners, medical/care context, or safety concerns. |
| CONTSCP06 | What should be excluded? | Exclude harassment, surveillance, sales prospecting, legal service, HR casework, therapy, or formal mediation. |
| CONTSCP07 | What relationship category matters? | Classify close, family, friend, acquaintance, service provider, school, neighbor, dormant, or do-not-contact. |
| CONTSCP08 | What contact authority exists? | Confirm consent to store, share, message, introduce, or invite people. |
| CONTSCP09 | What record should be kept? | Track preferred name, channel, last contact, birthday, address, boundaries, and source. |
| CONTSCP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce contact brief, cleanup plan, relationship map, or communication list. |

## CNTINV - Contact Inventory

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CNTINV01 | Where are contacts stored? | Inventory phone, email, SIM, cloud, social apps, messaging apps, spreadsheets, paper lists, and old devices. |
| CNTINV02 | What contact source is authoritative? | Choose current phone, cloud account, family list, address book, event spreadsheet, or user-provided source. |
| CNTINV03 | What duplicate contacts exist? | Find duplicate names, old numbers, merged households, multiple emails, and app-created duplicates. |
| CNTINV04 | What contact is incomplete? | Flag missing phone, email, address, birthday, relationship note, last name, or preferred channel. |
| CNTINV05 | What contact is outdated? | Mark old workplace, old address, changed name, inactive number, or outdated relationship context. |
| CNTINV06 | What contact should be preserved carefully? | Preserve deceased loved ones, family history, elder contacts, and sentimental notes. |
| CNTINV07 | What contact should be removed or archived? | Remove spam, obsolete services, unsafe contacts, duplicates, or no-longer-needed entries. |
| CNTINV08 | What import or export is needed? | Use CSV, vCard, cloud sync, phone transfer, or printed backup. |
| CNTINV09 | What backup exists? | Check cloud sync, export file, password manager note, paper list, or family emergency copy. |
| CNTINV10 | What inventory artifact is required? | Produce contact source map, duplicate queue, backup plan, or cleanup checklist. |

| ADDRBK08 | What relationship context helps? | Add how met, family link, shared group, last meaningful interaction, or gift preference. |
| ADDRBK09 | What field should not be stored? | Avoid unnecessary sensitive data, gossip, private medical details, or non-consented information. |

## RECONN - Reconnecting And Dormant Relationships

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RECONN01 | Who might be worth reconnecting with? | Identify old friend, relative, mentor, neighbor, classmate, hobby contact, or former colleague socially. |
| RECONN02 | Why reconnect now? | Link to sincere interest, apology, shared memory, event, move, milestone, or mutual support. |
| RECONN03 | What history should be considered? | Note drift, conflict, boundary, grief, major life changes, or prior unanswered messages. |
| RECONN04 | What first message is appropriate? | Use brief, warm, low-pressure note with context and no demand. |
| RECONN05 | What should not be included? | Avoid guilt, sales pitch, emotional dumping, pressure, or assumptions about closeness. |
| RECONN06 | What channel is least intrusive? | Choose text, email, card, social message, mutual intro, or no contact. |
| RECONN07 | What response options should be allowed? | Make it easy to reply, decline, delay, or not respond. |
| RECONN08 | What boundary means stop? | Stop after no response, explicit decline, safety concern, or prior do-not-contact request. |
| RECONN09 | What next step follows a positive response? | Suggest call, coffee, update exchange, shared memory, or future plan. |
| RECONN10 | What reconnect artifact is required? | Produce reconnect shortlist, message drafts, boundary plan, or response tracker. |

## GRPLST - Groups Lists And Events

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| GRPLST01 | What group list is needed? | Define dinner guests, holiday cards, school parents, neighbors, club, trip group, or family branch. |
| GRPLST02 | Who belongs on the list? | Include based on purpose, relationship, consent, capacity, and current contact details. |

| GRPLST07 | What list update is needed after the event? | Record attendance, new address, preferences, thanks owed, and next invite note. |
| GRPLST08 | What list should be archived? | Archive one-time events, outdated school groups, old trips, or inactive clubs. |
| GRPLST09 | What recurring group cadence exists? | Set monthly dinner, annual cards, reunion, club meeting, or family check-in. |
| GRPLST10 | What group artifact is required? | Produce invite list, RSVP tracker, mailing list, or group privacy checklist. |

## CNTPRI - Contact Privacy And Consent

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CNTPRI01 | What contact data is sensitive? | Identify address, phone, children's details, workplace, medical context, relationship status, or location. |
| CNTPRI02 | Who consented to sharing? | Confirm permission before sharing phone, address, photo, group invite, or introduction. |
| CNTPRI03 | What contact should be protected? | Protect survivors, private people, children, public-facing workers, and people with safety concerns. |
| CNTPRI04 | What contact should not receive a message? | Respect no-contact, do-not-invite, unsubscribed, conflict, or safety boundaries. |
| CNTPRI05 | What note wording is respectful? | Store neutral facts and preferences, not judgments, gossip, or sensitive speculation. |
| CNTPRI06 | What data minimization helps? | Keep only needed fields, remove stale details, and avoid collecting unnecessary private data. |
| CNTPRI07 | What group privacy risk exists? | Avoid exposing emails, phone numbers, addresses, or relationship status in group communications. |
| CNTPRI08 | What child privacy applies? | Limit children's full names, school, address, photos, and parent contact sharing. |
| CNTPRI09 | What deletion request should be honored? | Remove or update details when someone asks or when relationship boundary changes. |
| CNTPRI10 | What privacy artifact is required? | Produce consent checklist, sensitive-contact map, or safe-sharing rule. |

## CNTSHR - Introductions And Contact Sharing

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CNTSHR01 | What introduction is being considered? | Define social intro, neighbor intro, family connection, hobby group, mentor, or local service referral. |
| CNTSHR02 | Does each person consent? | Ask before sharing contact details or making an introduction. |
| CNTSHR03 | What context should be included? | Share why the intro fits, relationship, topic, and easy opt-out. |
| CNTSHR04 | What contact detail should be shared? | Share only approved channel, such as email, phone, handle, or event link. |
| CNTSHR05 | What boundary should be stated? | Clarify no obligation, privacy, timing, and purpose of connection. |
| CNTSHR06 | What referral risk exists? | Avoid overpromising, private details, business claims, or unsafe connections. |
| CNTSHR07 | What group handoff is needed? | Introduce in group chat, email, event, or in person based on comfort. |
| CNTSHR08 | What follow-up is appropriate? | Check only if helpful; avoid monitoring the relationship. |
| CNTSHR09 | What contact sharing should be refused? | Refuse if no consent, safety concern, sensitive context, or unclear purpose. |
| CNTSHR10 | What sharing artifact is required? | Produce intro message, consent note, referral context, or handoff checklist. |

| CNTCLN02 | What merge rule should be used? | Keep most complete, current, trusted, and recently confirmed contact card. |
| CNTCLN03 | What should be backed up first? | Export contacts before bulk edit, phone migration, cloud merge, or dedupe tool. |
| CNTCLN04 | What migration path fits? | Use cloud sync, vCard export, CSV import, phone transfer, or manual rebuild. |
| CNTCLN05 | What conflict should be reviewed manually? | Review same name, family members, old spouses, deceased contacts, and sensitive notes. |
| CNTCLN06 | What stale field should be removed? | Delete old job, old address, duplicate email, dead number, or unneeded note. |
| CNTCLN07 | What contacts should be archived instead of deleted? | Preserve family history, deceased loved ones, old mentors, or sentimental records. |
| CNTCLN08 | What sync risk exists? | Watch duplicate loops, wrong account sync, deleted contacts, or mixed work/personal lists. |
| CNTCLN09 | What verification proves cleanup? | Sample key contacts, search duplicates, test sync, and confirm backup. |
| CNTCLN10 | What cleanup artifact is required? | Produce cleanup log, merge rules, backup file note, or migration checklist. |
