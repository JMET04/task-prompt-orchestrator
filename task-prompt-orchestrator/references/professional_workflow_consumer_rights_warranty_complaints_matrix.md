# Professional Workflow Consumer Rights Warranty Complaints Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage consumer purchases, receipts, product fit, returns, refunds, warranties, repairs, service complaints, payment disputes, recalls, subscription cancellation, marketplace scams, regulator escalation, or consumer recordkeeping.

This supports organization, evidence collection, communication, escalation planning, and consumer decision workflows. It does not replace legal advice, regulator decisions, platform enforcement, product-safety authorities, financial-institution decisions, or licensed repair judgment.

## Consumer Issue Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CONSCOP01 | What consumer issue is being handled? | Classify refund, return, warranty, repair, missing item, service failure, billing dispute, recall, cancellation, scam, or complaint. |
| CONSCOP02 | Who is the consumer and seller? | Identify buyer, account holder, gift recipient, merchant, marketplace, manufacturer, service provider, installer, or reseller. |
| CONSCOP03 | What product or service is involved? | Capture model, SKU, serial, plan, order, contract, delivery, installation, subscription, or service appointment. |
| CONSCOP04 | What purchase channel applies? | Distinguish store, online marketplace, direct brand site, app, door-to-door, phone sale, social commerce, or contractor sale. |
| CONSCOP05 | What timeline matters? | Record purchase date, delivery date, defect discovery, contact attempts, return window, warranty term, and dispute deadline. |
| CONSCOP06 | What consumer goal is realistic? | Define refund, replacement, repair, credit, cancellation, apology, corrected bill, safe recall remedy, or escalation package. |
| CONSCOP07 | What rules or policies govern the case? | Gather return policy, warranty terms, service contract, marketplace rules, card policy, consumer law, or recall notice. |
| CONSCOP08 | What money or safety risk exists? | Note financial loss, recurring charge, unsafe product, spoiled goods, identity risk, inaccessible service, or urgent dependency. |
| CONSCOP09 | What boundary must be respected? | Separate consumer organization from legal advice, chargeback decisions, regulator authority, product-safety orders, and repair diagnosis. |
| CONSCOP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce issue brief, purchase timeline, evidence checklist, desired remedy note, or escalation map. |

| COMPMSG09 | What follow-up should happen? | Log response, update timeline, ask for supervisor, restate remedy, or move to next escalation. |
| COMPMSG10 | What complaint artifact is required? | Produce complaint email, call script, escalation note, or negotiation tracker. |
