# Professional Workflow Personal Voting Elections Civic Participation Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle personal voting, voter registration, election calendars, ballot research, candidate or measure comparison, polling-place planning, absentee or mail ballot administration, election-day logistics, accessible voting, election issue reporting preparation, contacting elected representatives, civic participation records, political donation records, or recurring civic review.

Use this for personal organization, official-source checklists, nonpartisan comparison tables, reminders, document gathering, communication drafts, and recordkeeping. Do not use it to impersonate a voter, suppress voting, provide legal election advice, guarantee eligibility, misrepresent candidates or measures, target political persuasion, automate political advocacy without consent, replace election officials, campaign compliance counsel, accessibility advocates, or emergency services.

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VOTESC01 | What civic participation task is being organized? | Define voter registration, election calendar, ballot research, voting plan, mail ballot, representative contact, donation records, or review. |

| VOTESC03 | What jurisdiction or election applies? | Track country, state/province, county, municipality, district, precinct, campus, union, HOA, association, or organizational election. |
| VOTESC04 | What official source owns the rule? | Capture election office, secretary of state, local clerk, electoral commission, voter portal, ballot guide, or organization administrator. |
| VOTESC05 | What date sensitivity exists? | Separate registration deadline, ballot request deadline, early voting, mail return deadline, election day, certification, or representative session. |
| VOTESC06 | What participation mode is relevant? | Identify in-person voting, early voting, mail/absentee ballot, proxy/assisted voting where allowed, drop box, or civic contact. |
| VOTESC07 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect address, party registration where sensitive, ID numbers, ballot choices, disability status, donation records, and household preferences. |
| VOTESC08 | What accessibility or language need matters? | Note accessible location, curbside voting, sample ballot format, interpreter rules, language materials, transportation, or assistive technology. |
| VOTESC09 | What should not be decided here? | Do not determine legal eligibility, advise how to vote, give election-law advice, or submit political actions without explicit user consent. |
| VOTESC10 | What scope artifact should be produced? | Create civic participation brief, election calendar, source log, voting-plan checklist, or privacy note. |

| MEASUR09 | What should not be concluded? | Do not provide legal advice, endorse a vote, predict outcomes, or simplify beyond official caveats. |
| MEASUR10 | What measure artifact should be produced? | Create measure explainer table, pro/con source map, fiscal note summary, or question packet. |
