# Professional Workflow Product Management Roadmap Feedback Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage product discovery, customer insight, feedback intake, opportunity definition, requirements, PRDs, prioritization, roadmap planning, experiments, product design handoff, build readiness, release coordination, product metrics, lifecycle management, or feature retirement.

## Product Scope And Strategy Fit

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PMSCOPE01 | What product, feature, service, or platform is in scope? | Define product surface, module, feature, API, internal tool, workflow, package, or productized service boundary. |
| PMSCOPE02 | What product objective matters most? | Choose acquisition, activation, retention, expansion, revenue, efficiency, quality, compliance, differentiation, or learning. |
| PMSCOPE03 | What user or customer segment is primary? | Identify persona, account tier, industry, maturity, geography, role, job-to-be-done, and accessibility needs. |
| PMSCOPE04 | What business strategy should the product support? | Link work to company strategy, portfolio theme, revenue model, market position, customer promise, or operational goal. |
| PMSCOPE05 | What product lifecycle stage applies? | Classify concept, MVP, beta, launch, growth, optimization, maturity, maintenance, sunset, or replacement. |
| PMSCOPE06 | What product constraint shapes decisions? | Capture budget, capacity, technical debt, legal, security, data, platform, brand, manufacturing, or support limits. |
| PMSCOPE07 | What product owner or decision maker is accountable? | Assign product manager, founder, product lead, business owner, platform owner, technical owner, or steering group. |
| PMSCOPE08 | What product promise must not be broken? | Preserve core value, reliability, trust, compatibility, compliance, privacy, performance, pricing, and customer commitments. |
| PMSCOPE09 | What product artifact is required? | Produce product brief, opportunity memo, PRD, roadmap, experiment plan, launch brief, metric review, or sunset plan. |
| PMSCOPE10 | What evidence proves product progress? | Use shipped outcomes, metric movement, customer learning, adoption, quality, revenue, retention, support reduction, or risk closure. |

## Customer Insight And Problem Discovery

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CUSTINS01 | What user problem needs discovery? | Frame pain, unmet need, workflow friction, workaround, risk, desire, job-to-be-done, or buying trigger. |
| CUSTINS02 | What discovery source should be used? | Use interviews, observation, support tickets, analytics, sales calls, win/loss notes, reviews, surveys, or community posts. |
| CUSTINS03 | What user context must be understood? | Capture environment, role, frequency, stakes, tools, constraints, motivation, alternatives, and decision pressure. |
| CUSTINS04 | What discovery question avoids leading the user? | Ask about recent behavior, concrete examples, tradeoffs, current solutions, cost of pain, and success criteria. |
| CUSTINS05 | What segment difference may change the problem? | Compare needs by persona, account size, maturity, geography, language, platform, accessibility, or regulation. |
| CUSTINS06 | What problem severity evidence is needed? | Measure frequency, intensity, cost, urgency, workaround effort, revenue impact, compliance risk, or churn risk. |
| CUSTINS07 | What alternative solution is currently used? | Map spreadsheet, manual process, competitor, internal tool, outsourced service, no-solution, or behavior workaround. |
| CUSTINS08 | What discovery bias should be controlled? | Check sample bias, loud-customer bias, sales pressure, recency, confirmation bias, and overfitting to one account. |
| CUSTINS09 | What insight should become reusable? | Store problem statement, quote, evidence, segment tag, source, confidence, and follow-up question. |
| CUSTINS10 | What discovery artifact is required? | Produce research plan, interview guide, insight synthesis, problem brief, journey note, or evidence pack. |

## Feedback Intake And Triage

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FEEDBK01 | What feedback channel is being processed? | Handle support, sales, customer success, community, app store, NPS, survey, analytics, bug report, or internal request. |
| FEEDBK02 | What feedback type is it? | Classify bug, feature request, usability issue, performance issue, pricing concern, integration request, content gap, or praise. |
| FEEDBK03 | What customer or user context should be attached? | Add segment, plan, ARR, product area, workflow, severity, frequency, role, account health, and source reliability. |
| FEEDBK04 | What duplicate or pattern exists? | Merge duplicates, group similar requests, identify trend, detect noisy outlier, and preserve source references. |
| FEEDBK05 | What urgency or severity applies? | Route by blocker, revenue risk, safety, compliance, outage, churn risk, launch dependency, or low-priority enhancement. |
| FEEDBK06 | What feedback should be rejected or parked? | Park misfit, unsupported segment, custom one-off, unclear value, policy conflict, or already-solved request with rationale. |
| FEEDBK07 | What feedback should trigger immediate action? | Escalate high-severity bugs, security/privacy issues, major account risks, regulatory concerns, or launch blockers. |
| FEEDBK08 | What feedback response should be sent? | Acknowledge, clarify, set expectation, share workaround, avoid false promises, and record customer communication. |
| FEEDBK09 | What feedback signal updates prioritization? | Feed volume, value, severity, strategic fit, customer tier, sales impact, and support cost into opportunity scoring. |
| FEEDBK10 | What feedback artifact is required? | Produce feedback log, triage board, pattern report, escalation note, response template, or product input digest. |

## Opportunity Definition And Framing

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| OPPDEF01 | What product opportunity is being framed? | Define the user problem, target segment, expected outcome, evidence, constraints, and owner. |
| OPPDEF02 | What outcome should the opportunity improve? | Link opportunity to activation, retention, conversion, expansion, efficiency, risk, quality, satisfaction, or revenue. |
| OPPDEF03 | What assumption makes the opportunity uncertain? | Name demand, usability, feasibility, viability, adoption, pricing, channel, data, or risk assumptions. |
| OPPDEF04 | What solution options should be compared? | Compare no-build, process change, content, configuration, small feature, full feature, platform change, or partner solution. |
| OPPDEF05 | What customer value proposition applies? | Explain who benefits, what improves, why now, proof, expected frequency, and why alternatives are insufficient. |
| OPPDEF06 | What business case supports the opportunity? | Estimate revenue, cost reduction, retention, market access, differentiation, compliance, support savings, or strategic learning. |
| OPPDEF07 | What risks could make this the wrong opportunity? | Check small market, weak evidence, high complexity, low adoption, cannibalization, support burden, or policy conflict. |
| OPPDEF08 | What success and failure criteria should be set? | Define metric target, learning milestone, quality threshold, adoption floor, stop condition, and review date. |
| OPPDEF09 | What stakeholder alignment is needed? | Align product, design, engineering, sales, success, support, finance, legal, security, and leadership expectations. |
| OPPDEF10 | What opportunity artifact is required? | Produce opportunity brief, product bet memo, assumption map, option comparison, or problem-solution framing note. |

## Requirements PRD And Acceptance

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PRDREQ01 | What requirement must be specified? | Define functional, nonfunctional, UX, data, integration, performance, security, compliance, analytics, or support requirement. |
| PRDREQ02 | What user story or job should guide the requirement? | Write user, need, context, trigger, desired outcome, and acceptance evidence without over-prescribing implementation. |
| PRDREQ03 | What scope is in and out? | Specify must-have, should-have, could-have, out-of-scope, future-phase, and no-build decisions. |
| PRDREQ04 | What acceptance criteria prove completion? | Define observable behavior, edge cases, data handling, permissions, error states, analytics, and testable outcomes. |
| PRDREQ05 | What nonfunctional requirement matters? | Include latency, reliability, scalability, accessibility, localization, compatibility, privacy, security, observability, and maintainability. |
| PRDREQ06 | What dependency affects the requirement? | Capture API, data, design, legal, vendor, migration, platform, analytics, release, and operational dependencies. |
| PRDREQ07 | What edge case or exception must be handled? | Include empty states, errors, permission denial, abuse, offline, migration, rollback, partial completion, and conflicting inputs. |
| PRDREQ08 | What analytics or instrumentation is required? | Define event names, properties, funnels, cohorts, dashboards, alerting, privacy boundaries, and metric ownership. |
| PRDREQ09 | What review gate should approve requirements? | Route through design, engineering, QA, data, security, legal, support, sales, success, and leadership as needed. |
| PRDREQ10 | What PRD artifact is required? | Produce PRD, user story map, acceptance checklist, analytics spec, API contract, or requirements traceability table. |

## Prioritization And Tradeoff

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PRDPRIO01 | What items are being prioritized? | List opportunities, features, bugs, tech debt, experiments, customer asks, compliance work, or operational improvements. |
| PRDPRIO02 | What prioritization criteria should be used? | Choose impact, reach, confidence, effort, risk, revenue, retention, strategy fit, urgency, dependencies, and learning value. |
| PRDPRIO03 | What scoring model fits the decision? | Use RICE, ICE, WSJF, MoSCoW, Kano, cost-of-delay, opportunity scoring, or custom weighted criteria. |
| PRDPRIO04 | What capacity constraint shapes priority? | Account for engineering, design, data, QA, support, legal, budget, platform, vendor, and release capacity. |
| PRDPRIO05 | What must interrupt the planned priority? | Allow security, outage, compliance, contractual, executive, major customer, or critical quality escalations with evidence. |
| PRDPRIO06 | What tradeoff should be explicit? | Compare speed versus quality, breadth versus depth, new feature versus debt, customer-specific versus scalable, or revenue versus trust. |
| PRDPRIO07 | What dependency ordering changes priority? | Sequence prerequisites, migration, platform work, data readiness, design system work, and go-to-market timing. |
| PRDPRIO08 | What stakeholder disagreement must be resolved? | Facilitate decision owner, evidence, criteria, options, objections, risk, decision, and communication plan. |
| PRDPRIO09 | What deprioritization message is needed? | Explain why an item is parked, what would change priority, and when it will be reviewed again. |
| PRDPRIO10 | What prioritization artifact is required? | Produce ranked backlog, decision memo, scoring sheet, tradeoff log, roadmap rationale, or escalation decision. |

## Product Roadmap Planning

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PMROAD01 | What roadmap horizon is needed? | Choose now-next-later, quarterly, release train, theme-based, milestone, experiment, platform, or customer-commitment roadmap. |
| PMROAD02 | What roadmap audience is being served? | Tailor view for executives, customers, sales, success, support, engineering, board, partners, or public users. |
| PMROAD03 | What roadmap item is a commitment versus intent? | Separate committed dates, target windows, discovery items, bets, experiments, dependencies, and aspirational themes. |
| PMROAD04 | What theme organizes roadmap work? | Group by customer outcome, strategic objective, product area, lifecycle stage, technical foundation, or market segment. |
| PMROAD05 | What release or milestone should anchor the roadmap? | Link roadmap items to launches, betas, pilots, migrations, compliance deadlines, sales events, or customer deadlines. |
| PMROAD06 | What roadmap dependency must be visible? | Show design, engineering, data, platform, legal, vendor, support, launch, and adoption dependencies. |
| PMROAD07 | What roadmap risk or uncertainty should be labeled? | Mark low-confidence items, discovery-needed items, capacity risks, unknown feasibility, and external dependencies. |
| PMROAD08 | What roadmap change process applies? | Require reason, impact, stakeholder communication, versioning, approval, and archive when roadmap materially changes. |
| PMROAD09 | What roadmap communication should be sent? | Share what changed, why, impact, commitments, caveats, next review, and audience-specific implications. |
| PMROAD10 | What roadmap artifact is required? | Produce roadmap view, theme map, release plan, dependency map, public roadmap note, or roadmap change log. |

## Experimentation And Validation

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PMEXPER01 | What product hypothesis needs validation? | State user, problem, solution, behavior, outcome, metric, assumption, and confidence level. |
| PMEXPER02 | What validation method is appropriate? | Choose interview, prototype test, fake door, concierge, A/B test, beta, pilot, smoke test, survey, or data analysis. |
| PMEXPER03 | What minimum evidence is needed before build? | Define sample, success threshold, failure threshold, qualitative evidence, quantitative signal, and decision rule. |
| PMEXPER04 | What experiment population should be used? | Select segment, cohort, traffic split, beta customers, internal users, geography, plan tier, and exclusion rules. |
| PMEXPER05 | What ethical or trust boundary applies? | Control consent, deception, user harm, privacy, fairness, vulnerable users, data use, and rollback plan. |
| PMEXPER06 | What instrumentation must be ready? | Define events, logs, survey prompts, cohort tags, exposure tracking, dashboards, and data quality checks. |
| PMEXPER07 | What experiment duration is enough? | Set time window, traffic volume, sample size, seasonality, stopping rule, and monitoring cadence. |
| PMEXPER08 | What decision follows experiment results? | Decide ship, iterate, pivot, stop, expand sample, run deeper research, or update roadmap priority. |
| PMEXPER09 | What learning should be preserved? | Store hypothesis, setup, result, confidence, caveats, customer quotes, metric impact, and next action. |
| PMEXPER10 | What experiment artifact is required? | Produce experiment plan, test script, beta plan, results memo, learning log, or validation dashboard. |

| PRDDES08 | What design-engineering handoff is needed? | Provide specs, states, assets, tokens, acceptance criteria, analytics hooks, edge cases, and review points. |
| PRDDES09 | What design decision needs documentation? | Record alternatives, rationale, user evidence, constraints, tradeoffs, unresolved questions, and follow-up test. |
| PRDDES10 | What design artifact is required? | Produce flow map, wireframe, prototype, UX spec, content spec, usability report, or design handoff packet. |

|---|---|---|

| BLDREADY03 | What engineering breakdown is needed? | Split work into epics, stories, tasks, spikes, migrations, QA tasks, analytics tasks, and release tasks. |
| BLDREADY04 | What dependency or blocker must be cleared? | Resolve vendor, platform, design, data, legal, security, customer, staffing, and environment dependencies. |
| BLDREADY05 | What sequencing should reduce delivery risk? | Order spikes, prototypes, platform work, high-risk integration, migration, testing, and rollout preparation. |

| BLDREADY07 | What product decision may arise during build? | Prepare decision owner, criteria, escalation path, tradeoff boundary, and communication rule for scope questions. |
| BLDREADY08 | What scope change control is needed? | Define how new asks, bug discoveries, design changes, and deadline pressure affect scope and release readiness. |
| BLDREADY09 | What delivery status should product monitor? | Track progress, risk, blockers, scope, quality, metrics readiness, launch readiness, and customer commitments. |
| BLDREADY10 | What build-readiness artifact is required? | Produce build brief, story map, delivery checklist, dependency log, QA plan, or scope-change log. |

## Product Metrics And Learning Loop

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PRDMET01 | What product metric should drive the decision? | Choose activation, adoption, retention, engagement, conversion, revenue, quality, performance, satisfaction, or risk metric. |
| PRDMET02 | What metric definition is precise? | Define numerator, denominator, cohort, time window, event source, exclusions, owner, and refresh cadence. |
| PRDMET03 | What funnel or journey should be measured? | Map discovery, signup, onboarding, activation, usage, value, support, renewal, expansion, or exit funnel. |
| PRDMET04 | What baseline and target are needed? | Set current value, target, confidence interval, seasonality, benchmark, and acceptable movement threshold. |
| PRDMET05 | What segmentation should be reviewed? | Compare by persona, plan, region, device, channel, cohort, account tier, use case, or product area. |
| PRDMET06 | What qualitative signal explains the metric? | Use interviews, session replay, support themes, survey comments, sales notes, success notes, and user quotes. |
| PRDMET07 | What metric instrumentation issue may mislead? | Check missing events, duplicate events, bot traffic, tracking changes, privacy filters, sampling, and dashboard drift. |
| PRDMET08 | What learning should update the roadmap? | Feed metric movement, customer evidence, experiment results, support trends, and revenue impact into priority decisions. |
| PRDMET09 | What product review cadence is needed? | Run weekly, sprint, monthly, quarterly, launch, incident, experiment, or portfolio metric reviews. |
| PRDMET10 | What metrics artifact is required? | Produce metric dictionary, funnel dashboard, product review memo, insight digest, or learning backlog. |

## Product Lifecycle Retirement And Migration

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PRDRET01 | What product, feature, or workflow may be retired? | Identify low-use feature, legacy plan, old API, unsupported integration, deprecated content, or obsolete process. |
| PRDRET02 | What retirement rationale is valid? | Use low value, high maintenance cost, security risk, compliance issue, replacement available, strategy shift, or quality burden. |
| PRDRET03 | Who is affected by retirement? | Identify active users, paying customers, internal teams, partners, developers, integrations, reports, and support workflows. |
| PRDRET04 | What usage and dependency evidence is required? | Measure active use, revenue, support tickets, integrations, contractual commitments, data dependencies, and customer impact. |
| PRDRET05 | What migration path should be offered? | Provide replacement feature, export, API migration, workflow guide, support package, timeline, and exception process. |
| PRDRET06 | What communication plan is required? | Notify affected parties, explain rationale, timeline, action required, support path, and consequences of no action. |
| PRDRET07 | What contractual or compliance check applies? | Review terms, SLAs, retention rules, accessibility, data export, legal obligations, and regulated customer commitments. |
| PRDRET08 | What shutdown gate must pass? | Verify migration, backup/export, monitoring, support readiness, rollback exception, documentation, and owner approval. |
| PRDRET09 | What post-retirement cleanup is needed? | Remove code, docs, pricing, analytics, permissions, support macros, sales materials, and stale roadmap references. |
| PRDRET10 | What retirement artifact is required? | Produce retirement brief, impact analysis, migration plan, communication pack, shutdown checklist, or cleanup report. |
