# Professional Workflow Neighborhood Property Community Relations Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle neighbors, shared housing, apartment or HOA coordination, property manager communication, noise, shared spaces, parking, pets, safety concerns, community notices, local groups, neighborly conflict, building rules, informal mediation, neighborhood events, or community follow-up. This matrix supports coordination and documentation; it does not replace emergency services, law enforcement, legal advice, licensed property management, HOA/board authority, professional mediation, or regulated building/safety inspections.

## Neighborhood Property Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| NEIGHSCP01 | What community or property context applies? | Define apartment, condo, HOA, dorm, shared house, block, rural neighbors, mixed-use building, or online neighborhood group. |
| NEIGHSCP02 | What issue or opportunity is in scope? | Identify noise, parking, shared space, pets, maintenance, safety, event, announcement, conflict, or mutual aid. |
| NEIGHSCP03 | Who are the stakeholders? | Map neighbor, tenant, owner, landlord, property manager, HOA board, building staff, local business, or city office. |
| NEIGHSCP04 | What authority structure exists? | Clarify lease, house rules, HOA bylaws, property policy, municipal rule, informal norm, or no clear authority. |
| NEIGHSCP05 | What urgency or safety boundary applies? | Route violence, fire, gas, medical emergency, active threat, break-in, or dangerous condition to emergency services. |
| NEIGHSCP06 | What privacy or dignity concern exists? | Protect addresses, children, health issues, disabilities, finances, immigration status, and personal disputes. |
| NEIGHSCP07 | What outcome is realistic? | Choose awareness, repair request, rule reminder, compromise, schedule change, documentation, escalation, or relationship repair. |
| NEIGHSCP08 | What history affects the issue? | Note prior conversations, complaints, repairs, meetings, agreements, conflicts, or repeated incidents. |
| NEIGHSCP09 | What tone should guide the process? | Use neighborly, factual, non-accusatory, safety-aware, and de-escalating communication. |
| NEIGHSCP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce issue brief, stakeholder map, authority note, safety boundary, or community plan. |

## Neighbor Communication And Relationship Repair

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| NEIGHMSG01 | What message needs to be sent? | Draft introduction, request, apology, notice, clarification, boundary, invitation, or follow-up. |
| NEIGHMSG02 | What tone fits the relationship? | Choose warm, brief, formal, neutral, appreciative, firm, or mediated tone. |
| NEIGHMSG03 | What facts should be separated from assumptions? | State observed behavior, dates, impact, and request without labeling motives. |
| NEIGHMSG04 | What channel is best? | Choose note, text, email, in-person, building app, manager relay, or group post. |
| NEIGHMSG05 | What should not be included? | Avoid threats, insults, legal conclusions, gossip, personal data, photos of people, or public shaming. |
| NEIGHMSG06 | What repair or apology is needed? | Acknowledge impact, accept responsibility where appropriate, propose fix, and invite practical next step. |
| NEIGHMSG07 | What language or accessibility need exists? | Use plain language, translation, large text, phone alternative, or visual notice. |
| NEIGHMSG08 | What boundary is necessary? | State communication hours, no direct contact, written-only channel, or manager-mediated contact when needed. |
| NEIGHMSG09 | What response should be expected? | Plan for agreement, no reply, defensiveness, escalation, compromise, or need for manager involvement. |
| NEIGHMSG10 | What communication artifact is required? | Produce message draft, apology note, boundary script, channel plan, or follow-up summary. |

## Documentation Evidence And Incident Logs

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| NEIGHDOC01 | What should be documented? | Record incident, communication, repair request, rule notice, meeting, payment, witness, or safety concern. |
| NEIGHDOC02 | What facts are needed? | Capture date, time, place, people, observed behavior, impact, photos, messages, and action taken. |
| NEIGHDOC03 | What evidence should be avoided? | Avoid illegal recording, trespass, surveillance, harassment, doxxing, or photographing minors/private spaces. |
| NEIGHDOC04 | What log format works? | Use spreadsheet, notes app, email folder, ticket export, photo album, or evidence index. |
| NEIGHDOC05 | What privacy redaction is needed? | Redact addresses, phone numbers, children, health details, financial info, and unrelated neighbors. |
| NEIGHDOC06 | What timeline should be built? | Order incidents, communication, management responses, repairs, payments, and escalation attempts. |
| NEIGHDOC07 | What official record exists? | Include tickets, notices, board minutes, violation letters, inspection reports, police/fire reports, or city case numbers. |
| NEIGHDOC08 | What missing information should be requested? | Ask for policy, rule text, ticket status, repair scope, meeting minutes, or decision rationale. |
| NEIGHDOC09 | What sharing boundary applies? | Share only with manager, board, mediator, attorney, official agency, or directly involved parties as appropriate. |
| NEIGHDOC10 | What documentation artifact is required? | Produce incident log, timeline, evidence index, redacted packet, or records request. |
