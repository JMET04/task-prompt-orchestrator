# Professional Workflow Pet Animal Care Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle companion animals, pet adoption, daily care routines, veterinary visit preparation, pet health records, medication administration support as instructed by a veterinarian, nutrition organization, grooming, training and behavior, boarding, travel with pets, emergency preparedness, end-of-life planning, or pet service providers. This matrix supports organization and care coordination; it does not replace veterinary diagnosis, treatment, emergency care, animal behavior specialist judgment, or local animal-control rules.

## Pet And Animal Care Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PETSCOPE01 | What animal or pet context is in scope? | Define dog, cat, bird, fish, reptile, small mammal, horse, service animal, foster animal, shelter animal, or multiple-pet household. |
| PETSCOPE02 | Who is responsible for care? | Identify owner, guardian, family member, sitter, walker, trainer, groomer, veterinarian, rescue, shelter, or foster coordinator. |
| PETSCOPE03 | What care outcome is needed? | State routine stability, health visit prep, safety, behavior improvement, travel readiness, adoption, boarding, or emergency preparedness. |
| PETSCOPE04 | What professional boundary applies? | Route diagnosis, medication changes, emergencies, pain, injury, poisoning, pregnancy, aggression, or complex behavior to qualified professionals. |
| PETSCOPE05 | What animal-specific factors matter? | Capture species, breed/type, age, size, sex/neuter status, temperament, mobility, known conditions, allergies, and environment. |
| PETSCOPE06 | What household and environment matters? | Note children, other animals, housing, yard, climate, noise, stairs, landlord rules, local laws, and household schedule. |
| PETSCOPE07 | What records are available? | Gather vaccination records, microchip, license, vet notes, medications, diet, insurance, adoption papers, and behavior history. |
| PETSCOPE08 | What safety or welfare concern exists? | Flag injury, illness, escape risk, heat/cold, aggression, neglect, abuse, toxicity, unsafe housing, or emergency access. |
| PETSCOPE09 | What privacy or consent applies? | Protect owner contact details, addresses, payment data, vet records, adoption records, and third-party information. |
| PETSCOPE10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce pet profile, care-scope note, professional-escalation note, record checklist, or household care map. |

| PETVET09 | What second opinion or referral path is needed? | Prepare records, referral letter, specialist questions, urgency, and owner goals for veterinarian coordination. |
| PETVET10 | What veterinary artifact is required? | Produce vet visit brief, symptom timeline, question list, post-visit checklist, or claim packet. |
