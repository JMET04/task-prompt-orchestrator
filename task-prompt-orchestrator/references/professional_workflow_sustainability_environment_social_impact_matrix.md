# Professional Workflow Sustainability Environment Social Impact Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow may affect environmental footprint, resource use, emissions, waste, communities, labor conditions, supply chains, green claims, ESG reporting, climate resilience, or broader social value. Combine it with commercial/financial/ROI, vendor/procurement, governance/policy, risk/issue/dependency, source/evidence, quality/audit evidence, and domain adaptation when sustainability impact matters.

## Sustainability Scope

| Code | Sustainability question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SUSCOPE01 | What sustainability decision does this workflow affect? | Separate design choice, procurement, production, operations, logistics, reporting, investment, or public claim decisions. |
| SUSCOPE02 | Who is affected by the sustainability outcome? | Identify customers, workers, suppliers, communities, future users, regulators, investors, and ecosystems. |
| SUSCOPE03 | What impact boundary applies? | Define product, service, project, site, market, supply chain, lifecycle stage, or organizational boundary. |
| SUSCOPE04 | What sustainability dimensions matter? | Select carbon, energy, water, materials, waste, biodiversity, labor, community, equity, resilience, or governance dimensions. |
| SUSCOPE05 | What time horizon matters? | Consider immediate, launch, operational, lifecycle, end-of-life, multi-year, or intergenerational impacts. |
| SUSCOPE06 | What baseline should be compared? | Define current practice, alternative design, no-action case, prior period, benchmark, or regulatory threshold. |
| SUSCOPE07 | What materiality level applies? | Classify impact by magnitude, likelihood, stakeholder concern, legal exposure, reversibility, and strategic relevance. |
| SUSCOPE08 | What positive impact is intended? | Identify emissions reduction, inclusion, safety, resilience, access, circularity, local value, or waste reduction. |
| SUSCOPE09 | What sustainability assumption is uncertain? | Record weak data, estimated factors, supplier opacity, rebound effects, and unmeasured externalities. |
| SUSCOPE10 | What sustainability-scope artifact is needed? | Produce impact brief, materiality note, boundary map, or sustainability decision log. |

## Environmental Footprint

| Code | Sustainability question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ENVFOOT01 | What environmental inputs are consumed? | Map energy, water, materials, land, chemicals, compute, packaging, transport, and consumables. |
| ENVFOOT02 | What environmental outputs are produced? | Track emissions, wastewater, solid waste, heat, noise, pollution, scrap, returns, and hazardous byproducts. |
| ENVFOOT03 | What lifecycle stage creates impact? | Compare extraction, manufacturing, packaging, transport, use, maintenance, reuse, recycling, and disposal. |

| ENVFOOT06 | What measurement method is appropriate? | Use LCA, bill of materials, spend factors, activity data, utility data, supplier data, or engineering estimate. |
| ENVFOOT07 | What uncertainty should be disclosed? | State factor source, data age, allocation method, estimate range, and excluded categories. |
| ENVFOOT08 | What environmental tradeoff exists? | Compare carbon versus water, durability versus material use, cost versus impact, speed versus transport footprint. |
| ENVFOOT09 | What environmental validation is needed? | Review source data, assumptions, methods, benchmarks, and domain expert checks. |
| ENVFOOT10 | What footprint artifact is needed? | Produce footprint table, hotspot map, method note, or environmental impact summary. |

## Claims And Communication

| Code | Sustainability question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SUSCLAIM01 | What sustainability claim is being made? | Identify green, ethical, inclusive, low-carbon, recycled, sustainable, net-zero, local, or impact claims. |
| SUSCLAIM02 | What evidence supports the claim? | Require specific data, methodology, scope, certificate, supplier proof, audit evidence, and date. |
| SUSCLAIM03 | What claim could be misleading? | Avoid vague, absolute, unqualified, cherry-picked, irrelevant, future-only, or offset-only claims. |
| SUSCLAIM04 | What comparison claim needs context? | Define baseline, period, geography, product version, calculation method, and material exclusions. |
| SUSCLAIM05 | What visual or packaging claim risk exists? | Review icons, colors, seals, imagery, labels, QR codes, and implied environmental superiority. |
| SUSCLAIM06 | What legal or platform rule applies? | Check advertising rules, green-claims guidance, consumer protection, securities disclosure, and marketplace policies. |
| SUSCLAIM07 | What audience understanding is needed? | Ensure claims are clear, plain-language, localized, accessible, and not overbroad. |
| SUSCLAIM08 | What correction path exists? | Define claim withdrawal, updated evidence, public correction, customer notice, and archive of prior claim. |
| SUSCLAIM09 | What communication review is required? | Route through sustainability, legal, brand, product, data, and executive review as appropriate. |
| SUSCLAIM10 | What claim artifact is needed? | Produce claim substantiation file, disclosure note, label review, or communications approval log. |

## Mitigation And Improvement

| Code | Sustainability question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SUSMITIG01 | What impact should be avoided first? | Prioritize elimination, reduction, substitution, reuse, repair, recycling, compensation, and last-resort offsets. |
| SUSMITIG02 | What low-regret action exists? | Identify actions that reduce cost, risk, waste, emissions, or burden without reducing quality. |
| SUSMITIG03 | What design change reduces impact? | Simplify, right-size, modularize, localize, improve efficiency, reduce variants, or extend lifespan. |
| SUSMITIG04 | What operational change reduces impact? | Improve scheduling, batching, routing, procurement, maintenance, training, and monitoring. |
| SUSMITIG05 | What behavioral or adoption change helps? | Guide users, teams, suppliers, or customers toward lower-impact use and disposal. |
| SUSMITIG06 | What offset or compensation claim is risky? | Treat offsets as secondary, verify quality, avoid overclaiming, and disclose limitations. |
| SUSMITIG07 | What impact tradeoff should be decided? | Compare cost, quality, time, safety, equity, carbon, water, waste, and resilience. |
| SUSMITIG08 | What mitigation owner is needed? | Assign action owner, due date, budget, metric, evidence, and escalation path. |
| SUSMITIG09 | What improvement validation is required? | Compare before/after impact, normalize for volume, check unintended effects, and confirm persistence. |
| SUSMITIG10 | What mitigation artifact is needed? | Produce mitigation backlog, impact reduction plan, tradeoff table, or improvement tracker. |
