# Professional Workflow Household Cleaning Chores Routines Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage household cleaning, chores, tidying, decluttering, supplies, cleaning cadence, room zones, household task sharing, guest readiness, seasonal deep cleaning, or cleaning review. Use this for organization, routines, safety, and coordination, not to replace professional remediation, pest control, hazardous-material handling, medical infection-control advice, construction cleanup, or landlord/legal decisions.

## CLNSCP - Cleaning And Chore Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CLNSCP01 | What cleaning or chore area is in scope? | Define room, surface, appliance, closet, entry, bathroom, kitchen, shared space, outdoor area, or whole home. |
| CLNSCP02 | Why is cleaning needed now? | Link to routine, guest visit, move, illness recovery, spill, seasonal reset, inspection, stress, or safety. |
| CLNSCP03 | Who uses the space? | Identify adults, children, elders, pets, guests, roommates, caregivers, or home workers. |
| CLNSCP04 | What level of clean is enough? | Choose quick tidy, functional reset, sanitary clean, deep clean, guest-ready, move-out, or maintenance level. |
| CLNSCP05 | What constraints apply? | Note time, energy, mobility, allergies if user-provided, supplies, noise, pets, budget, or shared housing. |
| CLNSCP06 | What should be excluded? | Exclude hazardous waste, mold remediation, biohazards, pest infestation, structural repair, and medical disinfection protocols. |
| CLNSCP07 | What cleaning priority comes first? | Prioritize safety, food areas, bathroom, clutter paths, trash, odor, laundry, or visible guest zones. |
| CLNSCP08 | What standard should guide the work? | Use household preference, lease expectation, guest comfort, child safety, pet safety, or maintenance requirement. |
| CLNSCP09 | What record should be kept? | Track schedule, assignments, supply list, recurring issues, before-after photos, or maintenance notes. |
| CLNSCP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce cleaning brief, room list, chore map, or reset checklist. |

## TASKCL - Task Breakdown And Sequencing

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|

| TASKCL03 | What can be done in parallel? | Run laundry, dishwasher, soak, air out, robot vacuum, or drying while doing another task. |
| TASKCL04 | What task needs a timer? | Use timer for soaking, laundry cycles, focused sprint, break, or recurring reminder. |
| TASKCL05 | What task requires preparation? | Gather supplies, clear surface, move furniture, protect fabric, open windows, or secure pets. |
| TASKCL06 | What task has a stop condition? | Stop when trash removed, surface clear, floor safe, supplies restocked, or time box ends. |
| TASKCL07 | What task should be split smaller? | Break overwhelming room into trash, laundry, dishes, papers, surfaces, floor, and final reset. |
| TASKCL08 | What task should wait? | Delay noisy, wet, chemical, outdoor, or shared-space work until appropriate time. |
| TASKCL09 | What task should be delegated or hired out? | Consider heavy lifting, high ladder, carpet cleaning, pest work, or specialized appliance cleaning. |
| TASKCL10 | What task artifact is required? | Produce task sequence, room checklist, sprint plan, or done-definition card. |

## SAFCL - Cleaning Safety And Health Boundaries

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SAFCL01 | What safety hazard is present? | Identify slips, sharp objects, broken glass, chemicals, fumes, mold-like growth, pests, heavy items, or electricity. |
| SAFCL02 | What protective step is needed? | Use gloves, ventilation, mask if appropriate, closed shoes, eye protection, or keep pets/children away. |
| SAFCL03 | What product should not be mixed? | Avoid combining bleach, ammonia, acids, drain cleaners, and unknown products. |
| SAFCL04 | What label instruction matters? | Follow dilution, contact time, surface warning, ventilation, storage, and disposal instructions. |
| SAFCL05 | What allergy or sensitivity should be respected? | Use fragrance-free, dust control, pet-safe, child-safe, or user-tolerated products when needed. |
| SAFCL06 | What should be escalated to professionals? | Escalate sewage, widespread mold, infestation, asbestos, chemical spill, fire damage, or hoarding crisis. |
| SAFCL07 | What emergency boundary applies? | Stop and seek emergency help for poisoning, severe reaction, fire, gas smell, or injury. |
| SAFCL08 | What safe storage is required? | Lock chemicals, label containers, separate food, secure sharp tools, and keep instructions. |
| SAFCL09 | What safety evidence should be kept? | Save product labels, incident photos, landlord notice, repair ticket, or professional report when relevant. |
| SAFCL10 | What safety artifact is required? | Produce safety checklist, escalation note, product warning list, or safe-storage plan. |
