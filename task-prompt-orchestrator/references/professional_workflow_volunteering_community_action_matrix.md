# Professional Workflow Volunteering Community Action Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage volunteering, community action, mutual aid, service projects, cause selection, opportunity search, volunteer applications, scheduling, onsite participation, safety, donations, advocacy, community programs, impact records, board or committee participation, or volunteer review.

This supports personal and small-team planning, coordination, documentation, and reflection. It does not replace nonprofit management, legal advice, medical care, emergency services, licensed social work, safeguarding authority, public-sector authority, or crisis-response command.

## Volunteer Community Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VOLSCP01 | What community action is being considered? | Classify volunteering, mutual aid, donation, advocacy, mentoring, board service, cleanup, event support, or neighborhood help. |
| VOLSCP02 | Who is participating? | Identify individual, family, student, employee group, faith group, club, class, neighbor group, or remote volunteer. |
| VOLSCP03 | Who is served or affected? | Define beneficiaries, community members, animals, environment, school, neighborhood, nonprofit, or public space. |
| VOLSCP04 | What motivation matters? | Clarify service, learning, solidarity, skill-building, civic duty, faith, social connection, or required hours. |
| VOLSCP05 | What time and capacity are available? | Capture one-time, recurring, seasonal, remote, physical, emotional, financial, and skill capacity. |
| VOLSCP06 | What boundaries are required? | Separate help from professional care, legal advocacy, emergency response, therapy, and official casework. |
| VOLSCP07 | What risk should be checked? | Note safety, safeguarding, scams, burnout, political sensitivity, privacy, transport, and liability. |
| VOLSCP08 | What organization or organizer is involved? | Identify nonprofit, school, mutual aid group, city agency, faith community, informal group, or campaign. |
| VOLSCP09 | What record should be kept? | Track hours, contacts, role, training, waivers, receipts, impact, and reflection. |
| VOLSCP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce volunteer brief, cause map, capacity note, or participation plan. |

## Cause And Fit Selection

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CAUSEP01 | What cause area is relevant? | Choose food, housing, education, environment, animals, health support, arts, eldercare, youth, civic, or disaster recovery. |
| CAUSEP02 | What community need is evidenced? | Use local organizations, public data, community requests, lived experience, and direct asks. |
| CAUSEP03 | What values and boundaries fit? | Align with ethics, faith, politics, skills, accessibility, time, and emotional capacity. |
| CAUSEP04 | What skills can be offered? | Identify tutoring, driving, translation, design, admin, cooking, fundraising, repair, mentoring, or technical skills. |
| CAUSEP05 | What learning is needed first? | Learn history, community leadership, harm reduction, cultural context, and organizer guidance. |
| CAUSEP06 | What help is not appropriate? | Avoid saviorism, unsafe advice, untrained clinical/legal help, unsolicited aid, and privacy intrusion. |
| CAUSEP07 | What local versus remote option fits? | Compare neighborhood action, online support, donation, phone banking, or professional pro bono path. |
| CAUSEP08 | What commitment level is realistic? | Choose one-off, monthly, weekly, seasonal, emergency-only, or project-based support. |
| CAUSEP09 | What organization credibility should be checked? | Review mission, transparency, safety, volunteer support, community trust, and financial signals. |
| CAUSEP10 | What cause artifact is required? | Produce cause shortlist, fit scorecard, learning list, or boundary statement. |

## Opportunity Search And Vetting

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| OPPFIND01 | What volunteer opportunity is needed? | Search event, ongoing role, remote task, board seat, mutual aid request, school support, or cleanup. |
| OPPFIND02 | What source channels are useful? | Use nonprofit websites, volunteer centers, schools, libraries, local groups, city pages, and trusted referrals. |
| OPPFIND03 | What role details must be confirmed? | Confirm tasks, location, schedule, training, physical demands, age limits, supervision, and contact. |
| OPPFIND04 | What eligibility requirements apply? | Check age, background check, language, license, vaccination, training, certifications, and availability. |
| OPPFIND05 | What costs should be understood? | Consider transport, supplies, parking, uniform, background check, donation pressure, and lost wages. |
| OPPFIND06 | What safety or safeguarding policy exists? | Confirm child/vulnerable-adult policy, buddy system, incident reporting, and emergency contacts. |
| OPPFIND07 | What red flags suggest avoiding it? | Flag unclear organizer, unsafe tasks, high-pressure money asks, no supervision, discrimination, or privacy misuse. |
| OPPFIND08 | What accessibility fit should be checked? | Review physical access, remote option, sensory load, language, transportation, and task flexibility. |
| OPPFIND09 | What comparison method helps? | Compare impact, fit, safety, schedule, learning, support, and cost. |
| OPPFIND10 | What opportunity artifact is required? | Produce opportunity tracker, vetting checklist, question list, or shortlist. |

## Volunteer Application And Onboarding

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VOLAPPL01 | What application or signup is required? | Complete form, interview, orientation, waiver, background check, references, or membership. |
| VOLAPPL02 | What personal data is requested? | Identify address, ID, emergency contact, background, health/safety info, and availability. |
| VOLAPPL03 | What should be shared minimally? | Provide required data only and avoid unnecessary sensitive details. |
| VOLAPPL04 | What training must be completed? | Track orientation, safeguarding, confidentiality, safety, role training, and tool instructions. |
| VOLAPPL05 | What agreement or waiver is involved? | Review volunteer agreement, liability waiver, media release, confidentiality, and code of conduct. |
| VOLAPPL06 | What role expectations need clarification? | Ask about tasks, reporting line, schedule, dress, supplies, breaks, and boundaries. |
| VOLAPPL07 | What background check timeline applies? | Track submission, consent, clearance, expiration, and privacy concerns. |
| VOLAPPL08 | What onboarding materials should be saved? | Store handbook, contact list, schedule, policies, training certificate, and login info. |
| VOLAPPL09 | What decision follows onboarding? | Accept role, ask questions, choose different role, pause, or decline. |
| VOLAPPL10 | What onboarding artifact is required? | Produce application packet, onboarding checklist, policy summary, or role acceptance note. |

| SCHEDV09 | What hours must be logged? | Track date, role, supervisor, service hours, required forms, and verification. |

## Onsite Volunteer Execution

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ONSITEV01 | What should happen on arrival? | Check in, meet supervisor, confirm role, sign waiver, get badge, and review safety instructions. |
| ONSITEV02 | What supplies or clothing are needed? | Bring ID, water, gloves, closed shoes, weather gear, phone, charger, snacks, and role tools. |
| ONSITEV03 | What task instructions must be followed? | Confirm process, quality expectations, boundaries, reporting, and escalation. |
| ONSITEV04 | What communication line exists? | Know supervisor, team lead, emergency contact, group chat, and incident channel. |
| ONSITEV05 | What beneficiary privacy applies? | Avoid photos, names, stories, documents, and social posts without permission. |
| ONSITEV06 | What should be documented during work? | Record hours, tasks, issues, supplies, outcomes, and follow-up needs. |
| ONSITEV07 | What if the task feels unsafe or inappropriate? | Pause, ask supervisor, leave unsafe area, document concern, and escalate. |
| ONSITEV08 | What debrief is needed before leaving? | Report completion, issues, lost items, next steps, and sign-out. |
| ONSITEV09 | What post-shift care helps? | Hydrate, wash up, decompress, note emotions, and check physical strain. |
| ONSITEV10 | What onsite artifact is required? | Produce shift checklist, incident note, hours record, or debrief summary. |

## Volunteer Safety And Safeguarding

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SAFEVOL01 | What safety risk exists? | Identify physical, transport, crowd, weather, disease exposure, emotional, harassment, or conflict risk. |
| SAFEVOL02 | What safeguarding context applies? | Check work with children, elders, vulnerable adults, animals, homes, or sensitive records. |
| SAFEVOL03 | What training or supervision is required? | Require orientation, buddy system, PPE, emergency plan, and role-specific supervision. |
| SAFEVOL04 | What personal boundary is needed? | Limit personal contact, gifts, money, social media, rides, home visits, and private meetings. |
| SAFEVOL05 | What incident reporting path exists? | Know who to call, what to document, when to contact emergency services, and how to escalate. |
| SAFEVOL06 | What health precaution applies? | Plan masks, gloves, handwashing, vaccination policy, heat/cold safety, and food handling. |
| SAFEVOL07 | What emotional safety support is needed? | Prepare debrief, peer support, role change, break, or referral after difficult service. |
| SAFEVOL08 | What legal or professional boundary applies? | Avoid giving legal, medical, financial, or counseling advice unless qualified and authorized. |
| SAFEVOL09 | What unsafe request should be refused? | Refuse secrecy, illegal acts, unsupervised vulnerable contact, unsafe driving, or personal financial transfers. |
| SAFEVOL10 | What safety artifact is required? | Produce safety checklist, safeguarding note, incident path, or boundary script. |

| DONATEV07 | What delivery logistics are needed? | Plan drop-off, pickup, shipping, storage, perishables, and volunteer unloading. |
| DONATEV08 | What donor privacy should be set? | Choose anonymous, public, recurring, restricted, or memorial donation. |
| DONATEV09 | What follow-up should be tracked? | Save receipt, thank-you, impact update, recurring charge, and renewal/cancel date. |
| DONATEV10 | What donation artifact is required? | Produce donation plan, in-kind checklist, receipt folder, or giving tracker. |

## Advocacy And Civic Action

| ADVOCV07 | What safety or retaliation risk exists? | Consider privacy, employer/school risk, public exposure, harassment, and legal boundaries. |
| ADVOCV08 | What respectful engagement is needed? | Avoid misinformation, harassment, doxxing, tokenizing, and speaking over affected communities. |
| ADVOCV09 | What follow-up tracks progress? | Record contacts, commitments, votes, meetings, responses, and next action. |
| ADVOCV10 | What advocacy artifact is required? | Produce action brief, message script, evidence packet, or follow-up tracker. |

## Impact And Hours Documentation

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| IMPACTV01 | What impact should be recorded? | Track hours, people served, items delivered, money raised, area cleaned, calls made, or outcomes. |
| IMPACTV02 | What proof is needed? | Save supervisor signature, portal entry, certificate, photos with permission, receipts, and event records. |
| IMPACTV03 | What required-hours program applies? | Support school service, court/community service, scholarship, workplace, faith, or certification hours. |
| IMPACTV04 | What privacy limits apply to impact stories? | Avoid names, faces, sensitive details, and identifiable stories without consent. |
| IMPACTV05 | What reflection is useful? | Record what happened, what was learned, community feedback, challenges, and next step. |
| IMPACTV06 | What resume or application proof helps? | Summarize role, hours, skills, outcomes, and supervisor contact where appropriate. |
| IMPACTV07 | What donor or organizer report is needed? | Provide counts, receipts, photos with consent, budget, and lessons. |
| IMPACTV08 | What data quality risk exists? | Avoid inflated counts, double counting, vague outcomes, and unverified claims. |
| IMPACTV09 | What archive should be kept? | Store hours log, role descriptions, training, certificates, and impact summaries. |
| IMPACTV10 | What impact artifact is required? | Produce hours log, impact summary, reflection note, or verification packet. |

## Volunteer Review And Sustainability

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VOLREV01 | What volunteer period should be reviewed? | Review first shift, month, season, campaign, school term, board term, or annual service. |
| VOLREV02 | What worked well? | Note fit, impact, community connection, learning, schedule, supervision, and safety. |
| VOLREV03 | What caused friction? | Identify overload, unclear role, poor communication, unsafe setting, transport issue, or values mismatch. |
| VOLREV04 | What boundaries need adjustment? | Change hours, role, contact rules, emotional exposure, donation level, or public visibility. |
| VOLREV05 | What skills or training are needed next? | Seek training, language support, first aid, facilitation, data skills, or role-specific learning. |
| VOLREV06 | What relationship should be maintained? | Follow up with organizer, community member, mentor, teammate, or beneficiary through appropriate channels. |
| VOLREV07 | What should be stopped or paused? | Stop unsustainable, unsafe, performative, ineffective, or misaligned commitments. |
| VOLREV08 | What recognition or thanks is due? | Thank volunteers, organizers, donors, hosts, and community partners. |
| VOLREV09 | What next commitment is realistic? | Choose continue, reduce, shift cause, take break, recruit others, or donate instead. |
| VOLREV10 | What review artifact is required? | Produce volunteer retrospective, next-commitment plan, boundary update, or impact archive. |
