# Professional Workflow School Child Activities Family Education Admin Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle child school administration, family education logistics, school calendars, permission forms, school supplies, homework routines, teacher communication, extracurricular activities, transportation, meals, accommodations, school records, or recurring school-year review.

Use this for family organization, communication, records, and school-life coordination. Do not use it to replace school policy, teacher judgment, medical advice, therapy, special-education/legal advocacy, safeguarding authority, emergency services, or official credentialing decisions.

## KIDSCP - School Family Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| KIDSCP01 | Which child or student context is in scope? | Capture grade, school, program, classroom, activity, age, household roles, and relevant caregiver context. |
| KIDSCP02 | What school-life outcome is needed? | Produce calendar, form packet, supply list, homework routine, activity plan, communication draft, or record archive. |
| KIDSCP03 | Who must coordinate? | Identify parent, guardian, child, teacher, coach, counselor, school office, caregiver, driver, and emergency contact. |
| KIDSCP04 | What time period applies? | Scope school year, semester, term, week, day, activity season, exam period, trip, or transition. |
| KIDSCP05 | What official source should be followed? | Use school portal, handbook, teacher message, district calendar, activity rules, or official form. |
| KIDSCP06 | What child-specific need matters? | Include allergies, medication, mobility, language, sensory needs, custody rules, learning support, or transportation limits. |
| KIDSCP07 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect student identity, grades, health, discipline, custody details, photos, addresses, and school location routines. |
| KIDSCP08 | What decision is outside this workflow? | Route legal, medical, therapy, special education disputes, discipline appeals, or safeguarding concerns to proper professionals. |
| KIDSCP09 | What recurring friction exists? | Detect missed forms, late arrivals, homework conflict, supply shortages, activity overload, or communication gaps. |
| KIDSCP10 | What artifact should be produced? | Create school admin brief, weekly dashboard, family checklist, communication plan, or record packet. |
