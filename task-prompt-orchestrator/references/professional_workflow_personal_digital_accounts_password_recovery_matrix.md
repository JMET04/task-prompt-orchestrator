# Professional Workflow Personal Digital Accounts Password Recovery Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage personal digital accounts, passwords, passkeys, MFA, recovery email or phone, account inventory, privacy settings, logged-in devices, account breach response, family access, account closure, emergency access, or periodic account review. Use this for personal organization, access recovery, security hygiene, and privacy coordination, not to replace professional cybersecurity incident response, platform support decisions, legal advice, identity-theft remediation services, or regulated enterprise IAM.

## ACCSCP - Account Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ACCSCP01 | What account is in scope? | Define email, phone, bank app, cloud, social, shopping, subscription, school, work-adjacent, device, or household account. |
| ACCSCP02 | Why is account work needed now? | Link to lockout, new device, password reuse, breach alert, family change, subscription review, or privacy cleanup. |
| ACCSCP03 | Who uses or depends on the account? | Identify self, partner, child, elder, roommate, family organizer, caregiver, or trusted contact. |
| ACCSCP04 | What outcome is required? | Choose regain access, secure account, inventory accounts, close account, update recovery, or document handoff. |
| ACCSCP05 | What sensitivity applies? | Flag finance, identity, health, children, private messages, photos, location, work, or legal-adjacent data. |
| ACCSCP06 | What should be excluded? | Exclude hacking, bypassing access controls, enterprise IAM, legal disputes, and professional incident response. |
| ACCSCP07 | What authority exists? | Confirm account owner, guardian, executor, family organizer, authorized helper, or no-authority boundary. |
| ACCSCP08 | What time pressure exists? | Note active lockout, suspicious login, travel, bill due, device sale, or account deletion deadline. |
| ACCSCP09 | What record should be kept? | Track account owner, recovery method, MFA status, last review, closure proof, and support case number. |
| ACCSCP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce account brief, recovery plan, access map, or security checklist. |

## ACCINV - Account Inventory

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ACCINV01 | What accounts exist? | Inventory email, password manager, browser saves, phone apps, bank charges, receipts, and old devices. |
| ACCINV02 | What account is most critical? | Prioritize primary email, phone number, password manager, bank, cloud, identity, and device accounts. |
| ACCINV03 | What account is forgotten? | Find old subscriptions, forums, stores, school accounts, social profiles, cloud drives, and app logins. |
| ACCINV04 | What login identifier is used? | Capture email, username, phone, social login, app store, SSO, or passkey provider. |
| ACCINV05 | What account owns other accounts? | Map primary email, phone number, app store, Google/Apple/Microsoft login, and family organizer roles. |
| ACCINV06 | What recovery channel is attached? | Record recovery email, phone, authenticator, backup code, trusted device, and security questions. |
| ACCINV07 | What payment or subscription is linked? | Note cards, app store billing, recurring charges, wallets, and family payment methods. |
| ACCINV08 | What data is stored there? | Identify files, photos, contacts, messages, purchase history, credentials, location, or identity docs. |
| ACCINV09 | What account can be retired? | Flag unused, duplicate, risky, unsupported, or no-longer-needed accounts. |
| ACCINV10 | What inventory artifact is required? | Produce account register, critical-account map, or retirement queue. |

## PWDHYG - Password And Passkey Hygiene

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PWDHYG01 | What credential protects the account? | Identify password, passkey, PIN, device unlock, recovery key, or social login. |
| PWDHYG02 | Is the password unique? | Flag reused, weak, old, shared, guessed, or exposed credentials. |
| PWDHYG03 | What password manager is used? | Identify password manager, browser vault, paper list, device keychain, or no manager. |
| PWDHYG04 | What password should be changed first? | Prioritize primary email, banking, password manager, cloud, phone carrier, and breached accounts. |
| PWDHYG05 | What passkey setup is appropriate? | Consider passkey for high-value accounts when device access, backup, and recovery are understood. |
| PWDHYG06 | What credential should not be shared? | Avoid sharing master passwords, MFA codes, recovery keys, and private account credentials. |
| PWDHYG07 | What shared account needs safer access? | Use family plan, delegated access, separate profiles, shared vault item, or account-specific permission. |
| PWDHYG08 | What backup credential is needed? | Store recovery codes, emergency kit, recovery key, or printed backup in a secure place. |
| PWDHYG09 | What password change proof matters? | Record completion, date, account, MFA status, and remaining follow-up without storing plaintext passwords. |
| PWDHYG10 | What password artifact is required? | Produce password hygiene checklist, priority change list, or credential backup plan. |

## MFAACC - MFA And Verification Setup

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MFAACC01 | What MFA method is active? | Identify authenticator app, SMS, email code, hardware key, passkey, push prompt, or none. |
| MFAACC02 | What MFA method is safest enough? | Prefer stronger methods for high-value accounts while considering usability and recovery. |
| MFAACC03 | What device holds MFA? | Map phone, tablet, hardware key, password manager, authenticator app, or trusted computer. |
| MFAACC04 | What backup MFA exists? | Check backup codes, second device, recovery key, alternate method, or trusted contact. |
| MFAACC05 | What MFA lockout risk exists? | Flag lost phone, old number, broken device, travel, app deletion, or inaccessible email. |
| MFAACC06 | What verification prompts are risky? | Beware unexpected MFA prompts, social engineering, fake support calls, and code requests. |
| MFAACC07 | What account needs MFA enabled? | Prioritize email, banking, cloud storage, social media, password manager, and phone carrier. |
| MFAACC08 | What method should be removed? | Remove old phones, old numbers, unused authenticators, or untrusted devices. |
| MFAACC09 | What test confirms MFA works? | Sign in safely, verify backup codes, confirm recovery path, and document non-secret status. |
| MFAACC10 | What MFA artifact is required? | Produce MFA setup checklist, backup-code plan, or verification map. |

## RECACC - Recovery Channels And Lockout

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RECACC01 | What recovery channel is available? | Identify email, phone, trusted device, backup codes, recovery key, support form, or identity check. |
| RECACC02 | What recovery channel is outdated? | Flag old phone, inactive email, lost device, forgotten security question, or deceased owner. |
| RECACC03 | What account recovery path should be tried? | Use official recovery page, device prompt, app store account, support form, or verified help channel. |
| RECACC04 | What information is needed for recovery? | Gather username, prior password if known, device, billing details, creation date, or support case. |
| RECACC05 | What should not be done during recovery? | Avoid fake support numbers, paying unofficial unlockers, sharing codes, or bypassing ownership checks. |
| RECACC06 | What if recovery fails? | Record attempt, wait period, alternate channel, escalation, replacement account, or data-loss plan. |
| RECACC07 | What access should be restored first? | Recover primary email, phone number, password manager, cloud, bank, and device accounts in dependency order. |
| RECACC08 | What recovery update follows success? | Update password, MFA, recovery email, phone, backup codes, and logged-in devices. |
| RECACC09 | What lockout evidence should be kept? | Save support case number, dates, official messages, screenshots, and outcome. |
| RECACC10 | What recovery artifact is required? | Produce recovery plan, lockout log, dependency map, or post-recovery checklist. |

## ACCPRI - Privacy Settings And Data Exposure

| ACCPRI06 | What audience should see content? | Set private, friends, family, custom group, public, or no-sharing defaults. |
| ACCPRI07 | What child or family privacy applies? | Review parental controls, child profiles, family location, shared photos, and purchase permissions. |
| ACCPRI08 | What ad or tracking setting should change? | Adjust personalization, data sharing, activity history, cross-app tracking, and email marketing. |
| ACCPRI09 | What privacy proof is needed? | Save settings screenshot, export confirmation, app revocation, or deletion request receipt. |
| ACCPRI10 | What privacy artifact is required? | Produce privacy checklist, app access audit, or exposure cleanup plan. |

## ACCBRC - Breach Alert And Suspicious Activity

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ACCBRC01 | What alert or suspicious activity occurred? | Classify breach notice, unknown login, password reset email, charge, message, or account change. |
| ACCBRC02 | What account is affected? | Identify account, linked email, payment method, devices, stored data, and connected apps. |
| ACCBRC03 | What immediate containment is needed? | Change password, revoke sessions, enable MFA, remove unknown devices, and secure primary email. |
| ACCBRC04 | What evidence should be preserved? | Keep alert, timestamp, IP/device if visible, emails, charges, support cases, and screenshots. |
| ACCBRC05 | What linked accounts are at risk? | Check reused passwords, social login, payment accounts, cloud storage, and password manager. |
| ACCBRC06 | What official source should be used? | Use account security page, official app, verified support, and known URL rather than message links. |
| ACCBRC07 | What payment or identity risk exists? | Route suspicious transactions, identity theft, or legal issues to the appropriate institution or professional. |
| ACCBRC08 | What communication is needed? | Warn contacts if spam was sent, notify family users, or document support interaction. |
| ACCBRC09 | What prevention update follows? | Update password reuse, MFA, recovery methods, app access, and monitoring reminders. |
| ACCBRC10 | What breach artifact is required? | Produce suspicious-activity log, containment checklist, or follow-up plan. |

| ACCCLS08 | What account should not be closed yet? | Keep if needed for recovery, warranties, tax records, device activation, or family access. |
| ACCCLS09 | What follow-up should be scheduled? | Check deletion completion, billing stop, data export, and search result cleanup. |
| ACCCLS10 | What closure artifact is required? | Produce closure checklist, export log, proof packet, or deletion follow-up tracker. |
