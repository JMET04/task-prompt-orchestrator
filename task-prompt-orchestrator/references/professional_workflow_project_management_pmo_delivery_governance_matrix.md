# Professional Workflow Project Management PMO Delivery Governance Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must initiate, plan, govern, execute, track, report, recover, or close a project, program, PMO initiative, milestone plan, cross-functional delivery effort, client implementation, transformation workstream, or complex deliverable with scope, schedule, resources, dependencies, risks, decisions, stakeholders, and acceptance gates.

## Project Scope And Charter

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PROJSCOPE01 | What project or program is being managed? | Define project, program, workstream, client implementation, migration, launch, transformation, or internal initiative. |
| PROJSCOPE02 | What outcome justifies the project? | Link business goal, customer promise, compliance need, operational improvement, risk reduction, or strategic objective. |
| PROJSCOPE03 | What is in and out of scope? | Define included deliverables, excluded work, assumptions, constraints, dependencies, and boundary decisions. |
| PROJSCOPE04 | What success criteria apply? | Set measurable delivery, quality, adoption, cost, schedule, risk, stakeholder, and business outcome criteria. |
| PROJSCOPE05 | What project governance model applies? | Choose lightweight owner-led, agile team, formal PMO, steering committee, vendor-managed, or regulated governance. |
| PROJSCOPE06 | Who sponsors and owns the project? | Identify executive sponsor, accountable owner, project manager, workstream leads, approvers, and key stakeholders. |
| PROJSCOPE07 | What project assumptions must be tested? | Capture funding, capacity, technology readiness, supplier commitment, user availability, and timeline assumptions. |
| PROJSCOPE08 | What constraints shape execution? | Account for deadline, budget, staffing, procurement, regulatory, security, system, calendar, and change-freeze constraints. |
| PROJSCOPE09 | What charter artifact is required? | Produce project charter, one-page brief, kickoff pack, governance plan, or project initiation document. |
| PROJSCOPE10 | What evidence proves the project is authorized? | Require sponsor approval, funding, owner assignment, scope signoff, acceptance criteria, and launch readiness. |

## Work Breakdown And Deliverable Structure

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| WBSMGMT01 | What deliverables must be produced? | List final outputs, interim artifacts, decisions, approvals, training, migration, launch assets, and closeout records. |
| WBSMGMT02 | What work packages are needed? | Break project into phases, workstreams, tasks, milestones, dependencies, owners, and acceptance gates. |
| WBSMGMT03 | What task granularity is useful? | Make tasks small enough to estimate, assign, track, verify, and hand off without micromanaging. |
| WBSMGMT04 | What deliverable owner is accountable? | Assign DRI, contributors, reviewers, approvers, and backup owner per work package. |
| WBSMGMT05 | What acceptance evidence is required per deliverable? | Define test result, signoff, artifact, demo, metric, audit trail, or customer confirmation. |
| WBSMGMT06 | What reusable template or playbook exists? | Reuse prior project plan, migration checklist, launch checklist, RACI, risk log, or delivery playbook. |
| WBSMGMT07 | What work should be parallelized? | Split independent lanes and define merge points, conflicts, shared resources, and integration gates. |
| WBSMGMT08 | What work must remain sequential? | Capture prerequisite, dependency, approval, data readiness, procurement, build, testing, and rollout order. |
| WBSMGMT09 | What WBS validation is needed? | Check missing tasks, hidden approvals, unrealistic sequence, orphan deliverables, and unowned dependencies. |
| WBSMGMT10 | What WBS artifact is required? | Produce WBS, task list, workstream map, deliverable register, acceptance matrix, or execution backlog. |

## Schedule Planning And Critical Path

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SCHEDULE01 | What timeline horizon must be planned? | Plan sprint, week, month, quarter, launch window, migration weekend, seasonal peak, or multi-year program. |
| SCHEDULE02 | What milestone anchors the schedule? | Use contract date, launch, audit, event, release, migration, executive review, customer date, or regulatory deadline. |
| SCHEDULE03 | What task duration and effort estimates are needed? | Estimate labor, elapsed time, review time, procurement time, test time, wait time, and contingency. |
| SCHEDULE04 | What calendar constraint applies? | Account for holidays, blackout windows, fiscal close, release freeze, staff PTO, vendor hours, and customer availability. |
| SCHEDULE05 | What critical path determines delivery date? | Identify longest dependency chain, constrained resource, approval gate, procurement lead time, or testing bottleneck. |
| SCHEDULE06 | What buffer or contingency should be added? | Add time buffer, scope reserve, resource reserve, decision buffer, and cutover rollback window. |
| SCHEDULE07 | What schedule compression option exists? | Use fast tracking, crashing, scope reduction, extra resources, parallel reviews, automation, or staged release. |
| SCHEDULE08 | What schedule risk needs monitoring? | Track slipped start, blocked dependency, late approval, underestimated effort, rework, vendor delay, and queue backlog. |
| SCHEDULE09 | What schedule baseline should be preserved? | Record approved dates, changes, variance, rationale, and rebaseline authority. |
| SCHEDULE10 | What schedule artifact is required? | Produce Gantt, roadmap, sprint plan, milestone calendar, critical path note, or baseline schedule. |

## Milestone Gates And Stage Reviews

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MILEGATE01 | What milestone must be reached? | Define initiation, design complete, build complete, test ready, pilot, launch, adoption, handoff, or closeout milestone. |
| MILEGATE02 | What entry criteria start the phase? | Require scope, funding, resources, inputs, dependencies, approvals, data, designs, or vendor readiness. |
| MILEGATE03 | What exit criteria close the phase? | Require deliverable acceptance, tests, decision log, signoff, risk review, docs, and next-phase readiness. |
| MILEGATE04 | What gate decision is needed? | Decide proceed, hold, rework, pivot, split scope, escalate, pause, cancel, or rebaseline. |
| MILEGATE05 | Who approves the gate? | Identify sponsor, steering committee, product owner, compliance, customer, PMO, or operations owner. |
| MILEGATE06 | What evidence must be reviewed at the gate? | Include status, deliverables, risks, issues, dependencies, budget, quality, acceptance, and change requests. |
| MILEGATE07 | What conditional approval is acceptable? | Allow proceed with actions, exceptions, risk acceptance, limited scope, manual workaround, or remediation deadline. |
| MILEGATE08 | What milestone communication is required? | Send gate decision, outcome, date, risks, owner actions, next phase, and stakeholder impact. |
| MILEGATE09 | What missed milestone recovery is needed? | Analyze cause, reset date, revise scope, add resources, escalate blocker, and communicate impact. |
| MILEGATE10 | What milestone artifact is required? | Produce milestone map, gate checklist, stage review pack, decision record, or milestone variance report. |

## Dependency And Interface Management

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| DEPENDPM01 | What upstream dependency exists? | Identify inputs, approvals, data, environment, design, vendor, legal, finance, security, or customer dependency. |
| DEPENDPM02 | What downstream dependency is affected? | Map teams, releases, campaigns, operations, training, support, customers, vendors, and financial reporting. |
| DEPENDPM03 | What dependency owner is accountable? | Assign owner, due date, acceptance evidence, escalation path, and backup. |
| DEPENDPM04 | What interface contract must be defined? | Specify input, output, schema, format, location, timing, quality, permissions, and change process. |
| DEPENDPM05 | What dependency health signal should be tracked? | Monitor status, confidence, date risk, quality, owner response, and blocker age. |
| DEPENDPM06 | What cross-team handoff is needed? | Prepare handoff packet, receiving criteria, meeting, support period, and acknowledgement. |
| DEPENDPM07 | What dependency conflict must be resolved? | Handle competing priorities, resource conflict, incompatible dates, scope mismatch, or ownership ambiguity. |
| DEPENDPM08 | What external dependency needs extra control? | Manage vendor, client, regulator, platform, procurement, shipping, legal review, or third-party API dependency. |
| DEPENDPM09 | What dependency escalation threshold applies? | Escalate when due date, confidence, quality, SLA, or critical-path impact crosses threshold. |
| DEPENDPM10 | What dependency artifact is required? | Produce dependency map, interface contract, handoff checklist, blocker log, or dependency health report. |

## Resource Budget And Capacity Management

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RESPM01 | What resources are required? | List people, roles, skills, budget, tools, environments, vendors, facilities, data, and subject matter experts. |
| RESPM02 | What capacity is actually available? | Check allocation, competing work, PTO, queue load, vendor capacity, budget timing, and tool limits. |
| RESPM03 | What resource gap threatens delivery? | Identify skill gap, staffing shortage, budget gap, tool missing, environment unavailable, or SME bottleneck. |
| RESPM04 | What budget baseline applies? | Track labor, vendor, software, travel, cloud, procurement, contingency, and run-rate costs. |
| RESPM05 | What funding or approval is required? | Obtain budget approval, purchase order, hiring approval, contractor approval, or change request. |
| RESPM06 | What resource tradeoff should be decided? | Compare scope, speed, quality, cost, risk, automation, outsourcing, and sequencing options. |
| RESPM07 | What utilization risk exists? | Avoid overload, single point of failure, context switching, underused budget, and hidden unpaid work. |
| RESPM08 | What resource reallocation is needed? | Shift staff, add specialist, defer lower-priority work, use vendor, automate task, or reduce scope. |
| RESPM09 | What capacity metric should be monitored? | Track planned versus actual effort, burn rate, team load, vendor utilization, and forecast-to-complete. |
| RESPM10 | What resource artifact is required? | Produce staffing plan, RACI, budget tracker, capacity plan, resource ask, or tradeoff memo. |

## RAID Risk Assumption Issue Decision Management

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RAIDPM01 | What risk could affect the project? | Capture delivery, quality, budget, resource, stakeholder, technical, legal, vendor, adoption, and schedule risks. |
| RAIDPM02 | What assumption needs validation? | Track assumptions about users, data, scope, effort, vendor, technology, approval, and business value. |
| RAIDPM03 | What active issue is blocking progress? | Record blocker, owner, impact, severity, next action, due date, and escalation. |
| RAIDPM04 | What decision is needed? | Capture decision options, criteria, owner, deadline, recommendation, rationale, and downstream impact. |
| RAIDPM05 | What risk score should be used? | Rate probability, impact, proximity, detectability, owner confidence, and residual risk. |
| RAIDPM06 | What mitigation or contingency is required? | Plan avoid, reduce, transfer, accept, monitor, fallback, workaround, or reserve action. |
| RAIDPM07 | What issue aging rule applies? | Escalate unresolved issues by age, severity, SLA, milestone impact, or owner nonresponse. |
| RAIDPM08 | What assumption invalidation changes the plan? | Replan scope, schedule, budget, resources, acceptance, or stakeholder commitment when assumption fails. |
| RAIDPM09 | What decision log evidence is needed? | Preserve date, attendees, options considered, decision, owner, rationale, and follow-up actions. |
| RAIDPM10 | What RAID artifact is required? | Produce RAID log, decision log, issue tracker, risk heatmap, assumption register, or mitigation plan. |

## Change Control And Scope Management

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CHGPM01 | What change request is being proposed? | Define scope change, schedule change, budget change, requirement change, design change, or acceptance change. |
| CHGPM02 | Why is the change needed? | Link to new requirement, risk, issue, stakeholder request, defect, compliance, value opportunity, or constraint. |
| CHGPM03 | What impact should be assessed? | Evaluate scope, time, cost, quality, risk, resources, dependencies, contracts, training, support, and users. |
| CHGPM04 | What options exist besides accepting the change? | Consider defer, reject, split, phase, workaround, substitute, reduce scope, or pilot. |
| CHGPM05 | Who approves the change? | Route to sponsor, product owner, change board, customer, finance, legal, compliance, or PMO. |
| CHGPM06 | What baseline must be updated? | Update scope, schedule, budget, roadmap, acceptance criteria, contract, RAID log, and communication plan. |
| CHGPM07 | What scope creep signal should be watched? | Detect unapproved asks, hidden tasks, repeated exceptions, gold plating, and ambiguous acceptance. |
| CHGPM08 | What change communication is required? | Notify affected stakeholders, owners, users, vendors, support, training, and governance forums. |
| CHGPM09 | What change traceability must be kept? | Link request, analysis, decision, approvals, implementation, test, and closeout evidence. |
| CHGPM10 | What change-control artifact is required? | Produce change request, impact analysis, approval record, updated baseline, or change log. |

## Meeting Cadence And Project Rituals

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RITUALPM01 | What project meeting is needed? | Choose kickoff, standup, workstream sync, risk review, steering committee, design review, gate review, or retro. |
| RITUALPM02 | What cadence should the ritual follow? | Set daily, weekly, biweekly, milestone-based, exception-only, or phase-gate cadence. |
| RITUALPM03 | What agenda keeps the meeting useful? | Cover progress, blockers, decisions, risks, dependencies, owner actions, changes, and next milestone. |
| RITUALPM04 | Who must attend and why? | Include decision makers, accountable owners, workstream leads, SMEs, vendors, and optional observers. |
| RITUALPM05 | What pre-read or artifact is required? | Send status report, decision options, risk log, dashboard, deliverable, or escalation packet before meeting. |
| RITUALPM06 | What decision must be made in the ritual? | Identify approve, prioritize, unblock, escalate, rebaseline, accept risk, or close action. |
| RITUALPM07 | What action tracking is required? | Capture owner, action, due date, dependency, status, and follow-up evidence. |
| RITUALPM08 | What meeting should be canceled or replaced? | Replace low-value meetings with async update, dashboard, office hours, or targeted decision meeting. |
| RITUALPM09 | What meeting quality signal matters? | Track decisions made, blocker age, action closure, attendance fit, and recurring agenda drift. |
| RITUALPM10 | What ritual artifact is required? | Produce agenda, pre-read, decision log, action register, steering deck, or meeting notes. |

## Closeout Lessons And Benefits Realization

| CLOSEPM04 | What open actions remain after closeout? | Track defects, adoption actions, training gaps, support issues, deferred scope, and benefits follow-up. |
| CLOSEPM05 | What artifact should be archived? | Archive charter, plan, decisions, RAID, deliverables, approvals, reports, contracts, and closeout summary. |
| CLOSEPM06 | What vendor or contract closeout is needed? | Confirm acceptance, payment, performance review, data return, access removal, and support obligations. |
| CLOSEPM07 | What team recognition or transition is needed? | Recognize contributors, release capacity, update role assignments, and communicate next opportunities. |
| CLOSEPM08 | What benefits owner takes over? | Assign metric owner, review date, reporting cadence, and action owner for unrealized benefits. |
| CLOSEPM09 | What PMO learning should update standards? | Improve templates, estimation, governance, risk checks, vendor rules, and training based on evidence. |
| CLOSEPM10 | What closeout artifact is required? | Produce closeout report, lessons learned, benefits tracker, archive manifest, or PMO improvement note. |

| PMOGOV09 | What compliance or audit requirement applies? | Ensure required approvals, change logs, signoffs, evidence retention, and segregation of duties where needed. |
| PMOGOV10 | What PMO artifact is required? | Produce portfolio review, intake rubric, governance calendar, project health dashboard, or PMO operating model. |
