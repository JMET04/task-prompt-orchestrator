# Professional Workflow Personal Wellbeing Sleep Stress Recovery Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle non-clinical personal wellbeing routines, sleep planning, stress organization, mood or energy logs, recovery time, mindfulness practice, social support, breaks, environment adjustments, habits, escalation boundaries, reflection, or recurring wellbeing review.

Use this for self-organization, reflection, habit support, and preparation for professional conversations. Do not use it to diagnose, treat, or replace medical care, mental-health therapy, crisis support, medication guidance, workplace accommodation/legal advice, or emergency services.

## WELSCP - Wellbeing Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| WELSCP01 | What wellbeing area is in scope? | Define sleep, stress, mood, energy, recovery, focus, social support, breaks, environment, or routine. |
| WELSCP02 | What outcome is desired? | Produce routine reset, reflection log, sleep plan, stress map, support plan, or professional-prep summary. |
| WELSCP03 | What time horizon applies? | Scope today, week, month, season, transition, exam period, work sprint, caregiving phase, or recovery window. |
| WELSCP04 | What context affects wellbeing? | Note work, school, caregiving, health, travel, relationships, finances, environment, workload, or life event. |
| WELSCP05 | What is user-controlled? | Separate habits, schedule, environment, communication, support requests, and professional or external constraints. |
| WELSCP06 | What should be observed first? | Track sleep, energy, mood, stressors, routines, triggers, supports, and recovery without overinterpreting. |
| WELSCP07 | What boundary keeps this non-clinical? | Avoid diagnosis, treatment, medication changes, crisis handling, and claims about medical causes. |
| WELSCP08 | What risk needs escalation? | Route self-harm, danger, severe symptoms, abuse, panic, substance crisis, or medical symptoms to appropriate help. |
| WELSCP09 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect journals, health notes, workplace issues, relationship details, and sensitive family context. |
| WELSCP10 | What artifact should be produced? | Create wellbeing brief, routine map, tracker, support script, or review plan. |

## SLEEPR - Sleep Routine

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SLEEPR01 | What sleep schedule is desired? | Capture bedtime, wake time, total opportunity, nap pattern, shift work, and weekend difference. |
| SLEEPR02 | What sleep disruption appears? | Note late screens, caffeine, stress, noise, light, temperature, pain, caregiving, pets, or racing thoughts. |
| SLEEPR03 | What wind-down routine helps? | Plan dim lights, device boundary, hygiene, reading, stretching, journaling, prep for tomorrow, or calm cue. |
| SLEEPR04 | What morning anchor matters? | Set wake time, light exposure, water, movement, medication reminder if user-provided, and first task. |
| SLEEPR05 | What sleep environment needs adjustment? | Address noise, light, bedding, temperature, clutter, device charging, and partner or pet disruption. |
| SLEEPR06 | What schedule conflict exists? | Map work, school, commute, childcare, social plans, deadlines, and late meals. |
| SLEEPR07 | What sleep record is useful? | Track bedtime, wake time, awakenings, perceived quality, naps, caffeine, alcohol if relevant, and stress. |
| SLEEPR08 | What should not be advised here? | Avoid diagnosing insomnia, apnea, medication effects, or medical sleep disorders. |
| SLEEPR09 | What professional conversation may be needed? | Prepare notes for clinician when sleep problems are severe, persistent, unsafe, or linked to health symptoms. |
| SLEEPR10 | What sleep artifact should be produced? | Create sleep routine, wind-down checklist, sleep log, or clinician-prep summary. |

## STRESSR - Stress Map

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| STRESSR01 | What stressor is active? | Identify workload, conflict, uncertainty, money, caregiving, health, deadlines, environment, or identity pressure. |
| STRESSR02 | What is the stress pattern? | Note time, place, people, task type, body signal, thoughts, and aftermath. |
| STRESSR03 | What can be reduced directly? | Remove small friction, renegotiate deadline, simplify plan, delegate, pause input, or clarify expectation. |
| STRESSR04 | What cannot be controlled? | Label external decision, other people's behavior, illness, market, weather, or institutional process. |
| STRESSR05 | What coping action is safe and realistic? | Choose walk, breathing, journaling, tidy one area, ask for help, rest, or tiny next step. |
| STRESSR06 | What communication is needed? | Draft boundary, status update, request for help, expectation reset, or delay notice. |
| STRESSR07 | What stress pileup exists? | Detect too many deadlines, sleep loss, conflict stack, caregiving load, and decision fatigue. |
| STRESSR08 | What body signal should be respected? | Notice tension, fatigue, headache, appetite change, panic-like feelings, or shutdown without diagnosing. |
| STRESSR09 | What escalation boundary applies? | Route severe distress, panic, unsafe thoughts, abuse, or medical symptoms to appropriate support. |
| STRESSR10 | What stress artifact should be produced? | Create stress map, controllable/uncontrollable list, support request, or next-step plan. |

## MOODLG - Mood Energy Log

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MOODLG01 | What mood or energy signal should be tracked? | Choose mood label, energy level, focus, irritability, calm, motivation, or overwhelm. |
| MOODLG02 | What scale is simple enough? | Use 1-5 rating, color, words, emoji-free label, yes/no, or short note. |
| MOODLG03 | What context should be logged? | Add sleep, meals, movement, social contact, workload, cycle if user-relevant, weather, or major event. |
| MOODLG04 | What trigger might be linked? | Note conflict, deadline, noise, caffeine, screen time, isolation, clutter, or uncertainty. |
| MOODLG05 | What support helped? | Record rest, walk, music, talk, food, therapy homework if user-provided, boundary, or task progress. |
| MOODLG06 | What pattern should not be overread? | Treat logs as clues, not diagnosis, prediction, or proof of a medical condition. |
| MOODLG07 | What privacy control is needed? | Store private notes safely, redact before sharing, and separate workplace-safe summary. |
| MOODLG08 | What review cadence fits? | Review daily, weekly, monthly, before appointment, or after stressful period. |
| MOODLG09 | What concerning pattern needs help? | Escalate persistent severe distress, unsafe thoughts, inability to function, or major behavior change. |
| MOODLG10 | What log artifact should be produced? | Create mood tracker, energy map, pattern summary, or appointment-prep note. |

## RCVWEL - Recovery Time

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RCVWEL01 | What recovery is needed from? | Identify work sprint, social load, caregiving, illness recovery, travel, conflict, sensory overload, or poor sleep. |
| RCVWEL02 | What kind of rest fits? | Choose sleep, quiet, movement, nature, play, solitude, connection, creative time, or low-demand chores. |
| RCVWEL03 | What time block is realistic? | Plan five minutes, lunch break, evening, weekend, recovery day, or gradual reset. |
| RCVWEL04 | What obligation must be protected? | Maintain safety, caregiving, medication routine if user-provided, essential work, and urgent responsibilities. |
| RCVWEL05 | What can be paused or reduced? | Delay nonurgent chores, messages, errands, social plans, perfection tasks, or optional commitments. |
| RCVWEL06 | What recovery environment helps? | Prepare quiet, light, temperature, comfortable clothes, reduced notifications, water, and simple food. |
| RCVWEL07 | What guilt or pressure appears? | Name unrealistic expectations, social comparison, productivity pressure, and permission to recover. |
| RCVWEL08 | What signal shows enough recovery? | Note steadier mood, clearer thinking, lower tension, better sleepiness, or ability to choose next step. |
| RCVWEL09 | What recovery need may require professional help? | Escalate prolonged exhaustion, unsafe symptoms, severe impairment, or medical concern. |
| RCVWEL10 | What recovery artifact should be produced? | Create recovery plan, rest menu, reduced-commitment list, or reset checklist. |

## MINDFL - Mindfulness Grounding

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MINDFL01 | What practice style fits? | Choose breathing, grounding, body scan, prayer/ritual if user-led, journaling, gratitude, or mindful walk. |
| MINDFL02 | What duration is realistic? | Set one minute, three minutes, ten minutes, commute moment, bedtime, or transition cue. |
| MINDFL03 | What cue should trigger practice? | Use waking, before meeting, after conflict, bedtime, meal, notification, or stress signal. |
| MINDFL04 | What environment supports practice? | Pick quiet spot, headphones, chair, outdoor route, low light, or private corner. |
| MINDFL05 | What wording should be gentle? | Use nonjudgmental observation, permission, short prompts, and no forced positivity. |
| MINDFL06 | What practice should be avoided? | Avoid trauma-triggering exercises, breath retention, spiritual mismatch, or practices that worsen distress. |
| MINDFL07 | What accessibility adaptation helps? | Offer seated, eyes-open, silent, movement-based, low-sensory, or language-specific version. |
| MINDFL08 | What reflection should follow? | Note before/after state, helpful cue, difficulty, and whether to repeat. |
| MINDFL09 | What boundary applies? | Do not claim mindfulness treats medical or mental-health conditions; route clinical needs to professionals. |
| MINDFL10 | What mindfulness artifact should be produced? | Create grounding script, practice menu, cue plan, or reflection card. |

## SOCSUP - Social Support

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SOCSUP01 | What kind of support is needed? | Identify listening, practical help, advice, company, accountability, childcare, meal, or professional referral. |
| SOCSUP02 | Who is safe to ask? | List trusted friend, family, peer, mentor, community group, coworker, clinician, or support service. |
| SOCSUP03 | What request should be specific? | Ask for time, task, presence, check-in, ride, meal, review, or boundary support. |
| SOCSUP04 | What level of disclosure is appropriate? | Share enough context while protecting privacy, dignity, workplace safety, and family boundaries. |
| SOCSUP05 | What support should not be expected from one person? | Avoid overloading friend, unqualified advice, crisis-only reliance, or unsafe contact. |
| SOCSUP06 | What reciprocal or consent boundary applies? | Ask availability, accept no, set time limit, and thank or reciprocate when appropriate. |
| SOCSUP07 | What group or community support exists? | Consider class, faith group, parent group, peer community, hobby group, or moderated support resource. |
| SOCSUP08 | What isolation pattern appears? | Notice withdrawal, missed messages, shame, conflict, or lack of practical help. |
| SOCSUP09 | What escalation is needed? | Use crisis, emergency, medical, domestic violence, or professional support when risk exceeds informal help. |
| SOCSUP10 | What support artifact should be produced? | Create support map, request script, check-in plan, or resource list. |

## BREAKR - Breaks Boundaries

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| BREAKR01 | What break is needed? | Choose microbreak, meal break, screen break, movement, social pause, quiet time, or full day off. |
| BREAKR02 | What cue shows break timing? | Use fatigue, irritability, mistakes, body tension, hunger, attention drift, or calendar interval. |
| BREAKR03 | What break activity restores? | Stretch, walk, water, snack, sunlight, breathing, tidying one area, or short connection. |
| BREAKR04 | What boundary must be communicated? | Set unavailable time, response delay, work stop, meeting limit, or family quiet period. |
| BREAKR05 | What interruption risk exists? | Plan notifications, children, coworkers, chores, guilt, and urgent exceptions. |
| BREAKR06 | What transition ritual helps? | Close tabs, write next step, set timer, stand up, change room, or prepare return point. |
| BREAKR07 | What break should not become avoidance? | Distinguish recovery from procrastination by naming next tiny step and return time. |
| BREAKR08 | What accessibility need applies? | Use seated movement, sensory break, medication/food timing if user-provided, or low-stimulation space. |
| BREAKR09 | What overwork pattern should be escalated? | Note chronic missed breaks, unsafe fatigue, labor-rights issues, or health impact requiring appropriate help. |
| BREAKR10 | What break artifact should be produced? | Create break schedule, boundary script, recovery menu, or return-to-task note. |

| ENVWEL08 | What safety or housing issue is beyond self-help? | Escalate hazards, violence, harassment, mold, unsafe heat/cold, or legal housing issues appropriately. |
| ENVWEL09 | What setup should be tested? | Try one change for a week and observe sleep, focus, stress, and comfort. |
| ENVWEL10 | What environment artifact should be produced? | Create environment audit, reset checklist, shared-space request, or experiment plan. |

| HABWEL08 | What tracking should stay lightweight? | Use checkmark, tally, short note, calendar dot, or weekly reflection. |
| HABWEL09 | What habit may be unsuitable? | Avoid rigid tracking, food/exercise obsession, unsafe restriction, or medicalized advice. |
| HABWEL10 | What habit artifact should be produced? | Create habit plan, cue map, tracker, or restart script. |

## ESCALW - Escalation Safety Boundary

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ESCALW01 | What sign needs immediate help? | Flag self-harm, danger to others, abuse, severe medical symptoms, psychosis-like symptoms, or unsafe environment. |
| ESCALW02 | What professional support may fit? | Consider primary care, therapist, counselor, crisis line, employee assistance, school counselor, or emergency service. |
| ESCALW03 | What symptom should be documented for a professional? | Track duration, severity, sleep, appetite, functioning, triggers, substances if relevant, and safety concerns. |
| ESCALW04 | What should not be handled by routine advice? | Avoid treating trauma, addiction crisis, eating disorder, severe depression, panic crisis, or medication effects. |
| ESCALW05 | What support person should be contacted? | Identify trusted contact, caregiver, family member, clinician, campus resource, or local emergency contact. |
| ESCALW06 | What crisis-safe wording is needed? | Encourage immediate local emergency or crisis support when safety may be at risk. |
| ESCALW07 | What workplace or school boundary applies? | Do not give legal accommodation advice; prepare facts for HR, school, clinician, or advisor. |
| ESCALW08 | What privacy and consent matter? | Share sensitive information only with appropriate consent unless safety requires urgent help. |
| ESCALW09 | What follow-up after escalation is needed? | Plan safe check-in, appointment notes, support logistics, and reduced commitments. |
| ESCALW10 | What escalation artifact should be produced? | Create professional-prep summary, support contact list, safety note, or appointment question list. |

## REFLCT - Reflection Meaning

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| REFLCT01 | What reflection prompt fits? | Ask what happened, what mattered, what helped, what drained, what is needed, or what to release. |
| REFLCT02 | What format is easiest? | Use bullets, voice memo, journal, table, drawing, prayer note, letter, or one-line log. |
| REFLCT03 | What value or priority is involved? | Connect to health, family, learning, work, creativity, service, independence, or peace. |
| REFLCT04 | What emotion needs naming? | Name anger, sadness, fear, relief, shame, joy, grief, uncertainty, or numbness without judgment. |
| REFLCT05 | What story should be softened? | Reframe all-or-nothing, self-blame, perfection, comparison, or hopeless language into workable facts. |
| REFLCT06 | What gratitude or appreciation is authentic? | Note real support, effort, beauty, progress, comfort, or survival without forced positivity. |
| REFLCT07 | What boundary or need is clearer now? | Identify rest, help, space, conversation, decision, food, movement, or professional support. |
| REFLCT08 | What action should follow reflection? | Choose message, break, plan, appointment, cleanup, sleep routine, or no-action rest. |
| REFLCT09 | What privacy rule applies? | Decide keep private, share excerpt, redact names, or archive securely. |
| REFLCT10 | What reflection artifact should be produced? | Create journal prompt, reflection summary, values note, or next-step card. |

| WELREV07 | What boundary should be revised? | Adjust work hours, screen time, sleep protection, social load, chores, or communication expectations. |
| WELREV08 | What evidence helps a professional conversation? | Summarize patterns, duration, severity, functioning impact, and questions without self-diagnosis. |
| WELREV09 | What risk remains? | Flag crisis signs, medical symptoms, unsafe environment, or severe impairment for escalation. |
| WELREV10 | What review artifact should be produced? | Create wellbeing review, routine reset, support plan, or next-month experiment. |
