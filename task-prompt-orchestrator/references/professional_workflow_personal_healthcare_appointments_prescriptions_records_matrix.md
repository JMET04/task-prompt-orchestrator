# Professional Workflow Personal Healthcare Appointments Prescriptions Records Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle personal healthcare administration, medical appointments, patient profiles, prescription and refill coordination, pharmacy pickups, lab or imaging result tracking, referrals, patient portals, medical bills and EOB coordination, medical record requests, care-team contact maps, visit preparation, or post-visit follow-up.

Use this for personal organization, scheduling, records, reminders, communication drafts, care navigation, and professional handoff preparation. Do not use it to provide diagnosis, treatment, medication changes, dosing advice, medical triage, emergency judgment, insurance coverage determinations, legal advice, or billing adjudication, and do not replace clinicians, pharmacists, emergency services, insurers, billing offices, patient advocates, or official medical records systems.

## MEDADM - Healthcare Admin Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MEDADM01 | What healthcare admin situation is being managed? | Define routine visit, specialist care, prescription refill, test follow-up, referral, bill question, records request, or chronic-care admin. |
| MEDADM02 | Who is the patient or authorized helper? | Identify self, child, elder, dependent, caregiver, advocate, guardian, or proxy role and consent boundary. |
| MEDADM03 | What urgency level is present? | Separate routine admin, time-sensitive scheduling, concerning symptom follow-up, medication access issue, or emergency escalation. |
| MEDADM04 | What care setting is involved? | Note primary care, specialist, urgent care, hospital, telehealth, therapy, lab, imaging, pharmacy, dental, vision, or home health. |
| MEDADM05 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect PHI, birth dates, member IDs, portals, diagnoses, medications, lab results, bills, and caregiver authority details. |
| MEDADM06 | What insurance or payment context matters? | Capture plan name, card, referral requirement, copay, deductible, authorization, self-pay, or assistance program without deciding coverage. |
| MEDADM07 | What language or accessibility needs exist? | Track interpreter, captioning, mobility access, caregiver presence, low-vision needs, transportation, or portal access support. |
| MEDADM08 | What records are already available? | Inventory visit notes, medication list, allergies, test results, imaging, referrals, bills, EOBs, and portal messages. |
| MEDADM09 | What should not be decided here? | Do not diagnose, choose treatment, adjust medication, delay urgent care, interpret coverage, or resolve disputes as authority. |
| MEDADM10 | What scope artifact should be produced? | Create healthcare admin brief, task queue, privacy note, urgency flag, or care-navigation plan. |

## PROFIL - Patient Profile

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|

| PROFIL02 | What conditions or history should be listed as user-provided? | Record active conditions, surgeries, hospitalizations, pregnancies, family history, or major events without clinical interpretation. |
| PROFIL03 | What allergy or reaction information exists? | Capture medication, food, latex, contrast, environmental allergies, reaction description, severity as recorded, and source. |

| PROFIL05 | What care preferences or constraints matter? | Track communication preferences, cultural needs, cost concerns, transportation limits, work/school limits, and caregiver availability. |
| PROFIL06 | What devices or home measurements exist? | Record glucometer, blood pressure cuff, pulse oximeter, scale, wearable, logs, and clinician-requested tracking. |
| PROFIL07 | What insurance and ID cards are needed? | Store medical, dental, vision, prescription, government ID, assistance card, and emergency card locations. |
| PROFIL08 | What profile source should be trusted? | Mark portal, clinician note, medication label, discharge paper, user memory, caregiver note, or imported record. |
| PROFIL09 | What should not be inferred? | Do not infer diagnosis, risk level, allergy severity, contraindications, or clinical meaning from profile fields. |

## PORTAL - Patient Portal Messaging

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PORTAL01 | What portal or communication channel is being used? | Identify patient portal, secure message, phone, email, app, text, fax, or in-person desk route. |
| PORTAL02 | What message purpose is needed? | Classify scheduling, refill, result question, symptom update, record request, billing question, referral, or admin correction. |
| PORTAL03 | What concise context should be included? | Include patient identifiers as appropriate, visit date, medication/test name, timeline, request, urgency, and callback preference. |

| PORTAL07 | What privacy or proxy issue exists? | Confirm proxy access, caregiver authority, minor account limits, shared email risk, and message visibility. |
| PORTAL08 | What message log should be kept? | Record date sent, channel, recipient, request, attachments, response, next step, and unresolved items. |
| PORTAL09 | What should not be sent this way? | Do not use routine portal messages for emergencies, severe symptoms, unsafe disclosures, or unsupported medical decisions. |
| PORTAL10 | What portal artifact should be produced? | Create portal message draft, call script, communication log, attachment checklist, or escalation note. |
