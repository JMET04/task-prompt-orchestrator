# Professional Workflow Casual Hosting Home Guest Social Visit Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage informal home hosting, social visits, guest invitations, casual meals, drinks, seating, house preparation, accessibility, overnight guests, activities, children or pets, house rules, cleanup, thank-yous, photos, or hosting review. Use this for personal social coordination, comfort, and logistics, not to replace professional event planning, catering, venue safety, medical dietary care, legal advice, religious authority, or cultural authority.

## HOSTSC - Hosting Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| HOSTSC01 | What kind of visit is being hosted? | Define coffee, meal, game night, family visit, neighbor drop-in, celebration, overnight stay, or informal gathering. |
| HOSTSC02 | Why is the visit happening? | Link to friendship, family, holiday, support, celebration, apology, welcome, childcare, or shared activity. |
| HOSTSC03 | Who is hosting? | Identify host, co-host, household members, roommate, partner, child, or helper responsibilities. |
| HOSTSC04 | Who is invited? | Define individuals, couples, family groups, children, elders, pets, or mixed social circles. |
| HOSTSC05 | What level of formality fits? | Choose drop-in casual, tidy casual, dinner-ready, overnight-ready, celebration, or sensitive visit. |
| HOSTSC06 | What should be excluded? | Exclude professional event operations, paid catering, venue security, medical dietary care, legal advice, or ritual authority. |
| HOSTSC07 | What constraints apply? | Note budget, space, time, noise, accessibility, dietary notes if user-provided, pets, children, neighbors, or shared housing. |
| HOSTSC08 | What comfort goal matters most? | Prioritize welcome, privacy, easy conversation, rest, food, accessibility, kid-friendliness, or low effort. |
| HOSTSC09 | What boundaries should be explicit? | Clarify shoes, smoking, alcohol, pets, rooms off-limits, photos, timing, or overnight expectations. |
| HOSTSC10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce hosting brief, guest list, visit plan, or readiness checklist. |

## INVITEH - Invitations And Guest Count

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| INVITEH01 | Who should be invited? | Select guests based on purpose, relationship, space, energy, budget, and social dynamics. |
| INVITEH02 | Who should not be mixed? | Avoid tense pairings, privacy conflicts, incompatible expectations, or unresolved disputes when possible. |
| INVITEH03 | What invitation channel fits? | Use text, call, email, group chat, calendar invite, paper note, or informal verbal invite. |
| INVITEH04 | What invitation details are needed? | Include date, time, location, food plan, what to bring, parking, kids/pets, and RSVP ask. |
| INVITEH05 | What tone should the invite use? | Choose warm, casual, practical, celebratory, discreet, supportive, or low-pressure tone. |
| INVITEH06 | What RSVP deadline helps? | Set deadline based on food, seating, overnight prep, transportation, or host capacity. |
| INVITEH07 | What plus-one or family rule applies? | Clarify whether partners, children, roommates, or extra friends are welcome. |
| INVITEH08 | What guest information is needed? | Ask about timing, accessibility, food constraints, parking needs, overnight needs, or allergies if they volunteer. |
| INVITEH09 | What reminder should be sent? | Send parking, entry, timing, bring-list, weather, or confirmation note close to the visit. |
| INVITEH10 | What invitation artifact is required? | Produce invite message, RSVP tracker, guest count, or reminder template. |

## SCHEDH - Timing Arrival And Departure

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SCHEDH01 | What date and time work best? | Match host energy, guest availability, meal timing, transit, children's routines, and quiet hours. |
| SCHEDH02 | What arrival window should be set? | Use exact time, flexible window, drop-in range, meal start, or overnight arrival. |
| SCHEDH03 | What departure expectation is fair? | Clarify end time, last transit, bedtime, cleanup timing, or overnight checkout. |
| SCHEDH04 | What prep time is needed? | Allocate cleaning, cooking, setup, errands, rest, shower, child/pet prep, and final reset. |
| SCHEDH05 | What schedule risk exists? | Flag late guests, early arrivals, overlapping appointments, noise limits, parking limits, or host fatigue. |
| SCHEDH06 | What should happen first? | Plan greeting, coats/shoes, drinks, introductions, tour, food timing, or activity start. |
| SCHEDH07 | What should be flexible? | Allow conversation flow, food timing, games, outdoor time, or rest breaks. |
| SCHEDH08 | What transition needs a cue? | Cue dinner, dessert, activity, cleanup, bedtime, departure, or overnight wind-down. |
| SCHEDH09 | What if guests arrive late or cancel? | Adjust food hold, seating, activity order, group message, or reschedule. |
| SCHEDH10 | What timing artifact is required? | Produce hosting timeline, prep countdown, arrival note, or gentle end-time script. |

## SPACHP - Space Prep And Seating

|---|---|---|

| SPACHP04 | What surfaces need clearing? | Prepare table, counters, coffee table, coat area, bathroom sink, entry bench, and serving zone. |
| SPACHP05 | What guest flow works? | Plan entry, coat drop, shoes, drinks, bathroom path, food line, trash, and conversation zones. |
| SPACHP06 | What lighting or temperature helps? | Adjust lamps, ventilation, heat, AC, fans, shade, or outdoor heaters. |
| SPACHP07 | What privacy prep is needed? | Put away papers, mail, devices, medications, valuables, personal photos, or sensitive items. |
| SPACHP08 | What accessibility prep is needed? | Clear paths, reduce trip hazards, reserve seating, prepare bathroom access, and note stairs. |
| SPACHP09 | What space should stay easy to clean? | Use trays, mats, coasters, washable covers, bins, or spill kit. |
| SPACHP10 | What space artifact is required? | Produce room readiness checklist, seating map, guest flow plan, or off-limits note. |

## FOODH - Food Drinks And Dietary Logistics

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FOODH01 | What food or drink level fits? | Choose tea, snacks, potluck, simple meal, takeout, dessert, drinks, brunch, or no-food visit. |
| FOODH02 | What guest preferences matter? | Capture likes, dislikes, vegetarian, religious/cultural preferences, allergies if volunteered, and alcohol preferences. |
| FOODH03 | What is safe to prepare? | Use appropriate storage, temperature, reheating, separation, clean surfaces, and labeled ingredients. |
| FOODH04 | What should be bought versus made? | Balance time, skill, budget, guest count, dietary constraints, and cleanup effort. |
| FOODH05 | What should guests bring? | Assign drinks, dessert, side, ice, games, serving item, or nothing depending on social fit. |
| FOODH06 | What serving style fits? | Use seated meal, buffet, snack board, self-serve drinks, family style, outdoor grill, or takeout setup. |
| FOODH07 | What backup food is useful? | Prepare extra snacks, simple drinks, shelf-stable options, child-friendly food, or late-arrival plate. |
| FOODH08 | What medical boundary applies? | Route allergy severity, clinical nutrition, medication-food concerns, and eating disorder concerns to qualified professionals. |
| FOODH09 | What leftover plan is needed? | Store, label, share, discard unsafe food, pack guest portions, or plan next meal. |
| FOODH10 | What food artifact is required? | Produce menu, grocery list, serving plan, dietary note, or leftover checklist. |

## ACTVHT - Activities Conversation And Flow

|---|---|---|

| ACTVHT03 | What introduction helps? | Prepare names, relationship context, shared interests, and gentle group entry. |

| ACTVHT06 | What role should the host play? | Facilitate introductions, refill food, notice discomfort, protect boundaries, and let flow happen. |
| ACTVHT07 | What group size issue exists? | Split into smaller zones, seat people strategically, or provide parallel activities. |
| ACTVHT08 | What child-friendly flow is needed? | Include play area, simple game, snacks, breaks, and adult conversation zone. |
| ACTVHT09 | What wind-down cue helps? | Use tea, dessert, cleanup, last game, music change, or ride reminder to close gently. |
| ACTVHT10 | What activity artifact is required? | Produce activity plan, conversation guardrails, introduction notes, or backup list. |

| PETKID09 | What should be off-limits? | Mark dangerous rooms, pet area, office, balcony, kitchen tools, medications, and private drawers. |

| SAFHST09 | What legal or medical boundary applies? | Do not give legal, medical, clinical, or safety-authority advice beyond practical host logistics. |

## CLEANH - Cleaning Before And After

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CLEANH01 | What cleaning level is enough before guests? | Choose quick tidy, bathroom refresh, kitchen reset, guest-ready, overnight-ready, or deep clean only if needed. |
| CLEANH02 | What areas must be cleaned first? | Prioritize bathroom, entry, seating, food surfaces, trash, floors, pet areas, and guest room. |
| CLEANH03 | What can stay imperfect? | Accept closets, private rooms, deep storage, paperwork piles, or projects in off-limits spaces. |
| CLEANH04 | What supplies are needed? | Prepare towels, soap, trash bags, wipes, vacuum, dish supplies, coasters, and spill kit. |
| CLEANH05 | What cleanup should happen during the visit? | Clear plates, manage trash, wipe spills, refresh bathroom, and reset serving area lightly. |
| CLEANH06 | What post-visit cleanup is needed? | Handle dishes, trash, laundry, floors, leftovers, borrowed items, and furniture reset. |
| CLEANH07 | What should wait until later? | Delay heavy cleaning, noisy vacuuming, laundry, recycling run, or delicate item sorting if tired. |
| CLEANH08 | What helper task can be accepted? | Let guests carry dishes, pack leftovers, fold chairs, or gather trash if socially comfortable. |
| CLEANH09 | What maintenance issue surfaced? | Note broken chair, clogged sink, stain, low supplies, pet damage, or safety issue. |
| CLEANH10 | What cleaning artifact is required? | Produce before/after checklist, reset plan, supply restock list, or issue note. |

## THANKH - Follow-Up Thanks And Photos

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| THANKH01 | Who should be thanked? | Thank guests, co-host, helper, person who brought food, driver, sitter, or neighbor. |
| THANKH02 | What tone should follow-up use? | Choose warm, brief, playful, formal, condolence-aware, supportive, or low-pressure. |
| THANKH03 | What should be shared after? | Send photos with consent, recipe, link, forgotten item note, next invite, or gratitude message. |
| THANKH04 | What should not be shared? | Avoid unconsented photos, private home details, children's images, sensitive conversations, or embarrassing moments. |
| THANKH05 | What borrowed or forgotten item needs return? | Track containers, coats, toys, chargers, books, serving dishes, or gifts. |
| THANKH06 | What reimbursement or contribution remains? | Settle shared food, rides, supplies, or group purchase without awkwardness. |
| THANKH07 | What relationship follow-up helps? | Suggest next visit, check on guest, repair awkward moment, or send supportive message. |
| THANKH08 | What boundary should be restated later? | Clarify future timing, guest count, pet rule, alcohol rule, or overnight expectations kindly. |
| THANKH09 | What memory should be saved? | Store photos, recipe notes, guest preferences, successful activity, or tradition idea. |
| THANKH10 | What follow-up artifact is required? | Produce thank-you message, photo consent note, item-return tracker, or next-invite note. |

| HOSTRV05 | What supplies should be replenished? | Restock towels, toilet paper, drinks, snacks, candles, games, bedding, or cleaning items. |
| HOSTRV06 | What reusable template helps? | Save invite, menu, grocery list, prep countdown, house note, or overnight checklist. |
| HOSTRV07 | What relationship signal matters? | Note who felt included, who needs follow-up, who should not be mixed, or who hosted next. |
| HOSTRV08 | What boundary should become routine? | Set default rules for photos, shoes, pets, alcohol, off-limits rooms, or end time. |
| HOSTRV09 | What tradition or cadence fits? | Decide recurring dinner, monthly game night, seasonal visit, rotating host, or no recurring plan. |
| HOSTRV10 | What review artifact is required? | Produce hosting retrospective, preference log, next-time checklist, or reusable hosting template. |
