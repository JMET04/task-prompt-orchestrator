# Professional Workflow Relationship Dating Partnership Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle dating, relationship intentions, partner communication, boundaries, compatibility, date planning, online dating, mutual expectations, conflict repair, shared life planning, family or friend integration, intimacy conversations, breakups, privacy, or relationship review. This matrix structures communication and planning; it does not replace therapy, crisis support, domestic-violence services, legal marriage/divorce advice, sexual-health care, or emergency services.

## Relationship Dating Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RELATSCP01 | What relationship context is in scope? | Define dating, early connection, committed partnership, long-distance, cohabiting, engagement, marriage admin, breakup, or reconciliation. |
| RELATSCP02 | Who is involved? | Identify self, date, partner, spouse, ex-partner, co-parent, family, friend group, counselor, or mediator. |
| RELATSCP03 | What outcome is desired? | Choose clarity, safer date, boundary, conversation, shared plan, conflict repair, transition, closure, or review. |
| RELATSCP04 | What high-risk boundary applies? | Route abuse, coercion, stalking, threats, self-harm, violence, or unsafe exit to qualified support and emergency services. |
| RELATSCP05 | What legal or health boundary applies? | Route marriage/divorce, custody, immigration, sexual health, pregnancy, STI, or financial/legal obligations to professionals. |
| RELATSCP06 | What relationship stage matters? | Capture first message, first date, exclusivity, moving in, family meeting, long-term planning, strain, or ending. |
| RELATSCP07 | What personal values should guide the workflow? | Note respect, honesty, autonomy, care, privacy, faith, family, ambition, safety, or playfulness. |
| RELATSCP08 | What privacy should be protected? | Protect screenshots, intimate details, location, contact info, photos, family matters, and sensitive history. |
| RELATSCP09 | What support or accountability is useful? | Identify trusted friend, therapist, advocate, mentor, clergy, legal professional, or health professional when appropriate. |
| RELATSCP10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce relationship brief, decision map, safety boundary, conversation goal, or support list. |

| PARTCOMM08 | What emotional support is appropriate? | Offer presence, reassurance, practical help, affection, or space while respecting autonomy. |
| PARTCOMM09 | What professional support may be useful? | Consider therapist, counselor, coach, clergy, or mediator for repeating patterns or distress. |
| PARTCOMM10 | What communication artifact is required? | Produce conversation plan, script, check-in agenda, agreement summary, or repair note. |
