# Professional Workflow Personal Correspondence Message Admin Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage personal email, chat, letters, inboxes, reply queues, message drafting, follow-ups, attachments, contact context, polite decline, updates, reminders, or personal communication records. Use this for organization, tone, clarity, privacy, and follow-through, not to replace legal notices, HR investigations, therapy, emergency communication, regulated customer support, marketing automation, or professional PR representation.

## CORMSC - Correspondence Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CORMSC01 | What communication situation is in scope? | Define email, chat, SMS, letter, group thread, announcement, invitation, update, request, apology, or follow-up. |
| CORMSC02 | Why does the message matter now? | Link to deadline, relationship maintenance, decision, commitment, event, unresolved question, or recordkeeping need. |
| CORMSC03 | What outcome should the message create? | Choose answer, confirmation, decision, meeting, boundary, thanks, apology, reminder, handoff, or closure. |
| CORMSC04 | What stakes or sensitivity apply? | Note privacy, emotion, money, health, school, work, family, safety, legal-adjacent, or reputation sensitivity. |
| CORMSC05 | What communication history matters? | Summarize prior thread, promises, dates, attachments, unresolved points, tone, and relationship context. |
| CORMSC06 | What channel constraints exist? | Consider character limits, formality, platform norms, screenshot risk, delay, read receipts, or attachment limits. |
| CORMSC07 | What response time is appropriate? | Set urgent, same day, weekly, after decision, after evidence gathered, or no reply needed. |
| CORMSC08 | What should be excluded? | Avoid oversharing, blame spirals, legal claims, medical advice, gossip, confidential data, or speculative accusations. |
| CORMSC09 | What record should be kept? | Save thread, final message, timestamp, confirmation, attachment, call note, or decision summary. |
| CORMSC10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce message brief, correspondence queue item, reply plan, or communication record. |

## RECIPM - Recipient And Relationship Map

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RECIPM01 | Who is the primary recipient? | Identify person, family member, friend, classmate, neighbor, vendor, provider, teacher, organizer, or group. |
| RECIPM02 | Who else may read it? | Account for forwarded readers, group members, household members, staff, moderators, or future reviewers. |
| RECIPM03 | What relationship context matters? | Note closeness, formality, conflict history, authority, trust, age, culture, frequency, and shared commitments. |
| RECIPM04 | What does the recipient already know? | Separate known facts, missing context, assumptions, and items that need a reminder. |
| RECIPM05 | What does the recipient need to do? | Specify answer, choose option, send file, confirm time, pay, attend, stop action, or acknowledge. |
| RECIPM06 | What tone fits the relationship? | Choose warm, concise, formal, appreciative, firm, neutral, apologetic, celebratory, or logistical. |
| RECIPM07 | What accessibility or language need exists? | Adjust for language, reading level, disability, device, timezone, translation, or preferred channel. |
| RECIPM08 | What power imbalance exists? | Handle landlord, teacher, provider, elder, employer-adjacent, group leader, or dependent relationship carefully. |
| RECIPM09 | What contact detail must be verified? | Check address, handle, phone, email, group membership, preferred name, pronouns, or mailing address. |
| RECIPM10 | What recipient artifact is required? | Produce contact note, recipient map, stakeholder list, or send-to checklist. |

## CHANMS - Channel And Format Selection

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CHANMS01 | Which channel should be used? | Choose email, text, chat, phone summary, letter, card, form, shared doc, social DM, or in-person follow-up note. |
| CHANMS02 | What channel should be avoided? | Avoid public posts, group chats, disappearing messages, work channels, or informal channels for sensitive items. |
| CHANMS03 | What format best fits the ask? | Use short reply, bullet list, template, formal letter, agenda, checklist, note, invitation, or confirmation. |
| CHANMS04 | What subject or opener is needed? | Create clear subject line, greeting, context line, reference number, or thread title. |
| CHANMS05 | What length is appropriate? | Keep short for quick asks, structured for complex issues, and separate attachments for detail-heavy context. |
| CHANMS06 | What attachment or link format helps? | Include PDF, screenshot, photo, calendar link, map, form, receipt, shared file, or no attachment. |
| CHANMS07 | What channel etiquette applies? | Respect time windows, response norms, emojis, salutations, group mentions, read receipts, and reply-all rules. |
| CHANMS08 | What confirmation mechanism is needed? | Request reply, receipt, RSVP, signature, click, form submit, calendar accept, or written acknowledgement. |
| CHANMS09 | What fallback channel exists? | Plan second email, call, mailed letter, official portal, shared contact, or in-person note if no response. |
| CHANMS10 | What channel artifact is required? | Produce channel plan, message shell, subject list, or send checklist. |

## INBOXM - Inbox And Message Triage

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| INBOXM01 | What messages require action? | Identify replies, bills, invitations, school notes, provider messages, family logistics, deadlines, or decisions. |
| INBOXM02 | What can be archived without action? | Clear receipts, FYIs, newsletters, completed confirmations, spam, duplicates, and stale threads. |
| INBOXM03 | What needs a quick reply? | Handle simple confirmations, thanks, missing details, short answers, and deadline acknowledgements. |
| INBOXM04 | What needs deeper drafting? | Mark emotional, high-stakes, multi-party, evidence-based, formal, or negotiation-like messages. |
| INBOXM05 | What message needs outside input? | Ask family, professional, teacher, provider, legal counsel, translator, or trusted friend before replying. |
| INBOXM06 | What deadline should be extracted? | Capture due date, RSVP, appointment, payment, pickup window, school deadline, or renewal date. |
| INBOXM07 | What message belongs in another workflow? | Route to finance, documents, health, school, housing, travel, consumer complaint, or conflict workflow. |
| INBOXM08 | What filter or label is useful? | Apply labels for waiting, urgent, family, money, school, health, travel, receipts, or archive. |
| INBOXM09 | What recurring inbox problem exists? | Detect overload, missed deadlines, unclear senders, too many groups, spam, or scattered channels. |
| INBOXM10 | What triage artifact is required? | Produce inbox action list, message queue, deadline extraction, or archive rule. |

## OUTMS - Outgoing Message Drafting

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| OUTMS01 | What is the one-line purpose? | State the request, answer, update, reminder, apology, thanks, decline, invitation, or confirmation. |
| OUTMS02 | What context is necessary? | Include only facts, dates, names, prior agreement, evidence, and relevant constraints needed for action. |
| OUTMS03 | What specific ask should be made? | Request yes/no answer, document, time choice, payment, confirmation, decision, correction, or next step. |
| OUTMS04 | What options can reduce friction? | Offer time slots, acceptable formats, alternatives, deadline choices, or easy reply wording. |
| OUTMS05 | What evidence should support the message? | Reference receipt, screenshot, prior thread, calendar, agreement, policy, photo, or document. |
| OUTMS06 | What emotion should be acknowledged? | Add appreciation, regret, concern, excitement, disappointment, empathy, or respect without overexplaining. |
| OUTMS07 | What boundary or limit is needed? | State availability, deadline, budget, privacy, response channel, scope, or what cannot be done. |
| OUTMS08 | What closing action is clear? | End with reply request, thanks, next step, deadline, calendar invite, or "no action needed." |
| OUTMS09 | What final check is required? | Review names, dates, attachments, tone, facts, privacy, recipients, and whether sending is wise. |
| OUTMS10 | What outgoing artifact is required? | Produce draft message, alternate versions, final send checklist, or message packet. |

## TONEM - Tone And Sensitivity Control

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| TONEM01 | What tone should be perceived? | Aim for kind, firm, brief, professional, warm, neutral, apologetic, celebratory, or serious. |
| TONEM02 | What tone risk exists? | Watch for passive aggression, defensiveness, guilt, coldness, pressure, sarcasm, blame, or overfamiliarity. |
| TONEM03 | What emotional state should not drive send? | Delay if angry, panicked, ashamed, exhausted, intoxicated, or trying to win rather than communicate. |
| TONEM04 | What phrase should be softened? | Replace accusations, absolutes, vague criticism, and demands with observations, needs, options, and requests. |
| TONEM05 | What phrase should be firmer? | Make boundaries, deadlines, declines, safety limits, payment requests, and decisions unambiguous. |
| TONEM06 | What appreciation belongs? | Add thanks, recognition, shared goal, effort noticed, or goodwill when genuine. |
| TONEM07 | What apology or ownership is appropriate? | Own delay, mistake, confusion, missed commitment, or tone without accepting false blame. |
| TONEM08 | What cultural or relationship nuance matters? | Adjust honorifics, directness, formality, humor, family roles, age norms, or group expectations. |
| TONEM09 | What sensitive content should be separated? | Move complex emotion, private details, or evidence into a call, private note, or separate document. |
| TONEM10 | What tone artifact is required? | Produce tone rewrite, sensitivity check, firm version, soft version, or delay-before-send note. |

## FOLLOWM - Follow-Up And Waiting

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FOLLOWM01 | What response is expected? | Define answer, confirmation, file, payment, date choice, review, approval, or apology. |
| FOLLOWM02 | What follow-up timing is fair? | Set same day, two days, one week, after deadline, after event, or according to stated SLA. |
| FOLLOWM03 | What should the follow-up include? | Reference prior message, restate ask, add deadline, offer easy response, and keep tone calm. |
| FOLLOWM04 | What should not be repeated? | Avoid escalating too soon, flooding channels, restating every detail, or adding emotional pressure. |
| FOLLOWM05 | What status should be tracked? | Mark sent, waiting, answered, bounced, declined, escalated, closed, or no-response accepted. |
| FOLLOWM06 | What escalation path exists? | Use phone, official portal, alternate contact, manager, mediator, provider office, or formal letter when appropriate. |
| FOLLOWM07 | What auto-reminder helps? | Add calendar reminder, task, email snooze, note, tickler file, or recurring follow-up. |
| FOLLOWM08 | What no-response boundary applies? | Decide when to stop, archive, change plans, choose another provider, or protect energy. |
| FOLLOWM09 | What response should trigger closure? | Archive after confirmation, thanks, calendar update, payment, document receipt, or decision captured. |
| FOLLOWM10 | What follow-up artifact is required? | Produce follow-up tracker, reminder note, escalation plan, or closure summary. |

## THREADM - Thread And Group Coordination

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| THREADM01 | What thread or group is active? | Identify family chat, school group, event thread, roommate chat, volunteer group, or shared planning channel. |
| THREADM02 | What topic should stay in thread? | Keep logistics, updates, decisions, documents, and shared reminders visible to the right group. |
| THREADM03 | What should move private? | Move sensitive conflict, personal data, money details, health, criticism, or one-person issue to direct channel. |
| THREADM04 | What decision needs summarizing? | Capture agreed time, owner, amount, plan, deadline, location, or next check-in. |
| THREADM05 | What group message should be pinned? | Pin schedule, link, address, packing list, agenda, policy, emergency contact, or final decision. |
| THREADM06 | What reply-all rule applies? | Reply to all only when shared context, decision, or coordination requires it. |
| THREADM07 | What participant is missing? | Add or separately inform person affected by decision, provider, caregiver, organizer, or household member. |
| THREADM08 | What conflict or noise should be contained? | Suggest thread reset, summary, separate topic, moderator, quiet hours, or decision owner. |
| THREADM09 | What group archive matters? | Save final plan, attendance, payment, photos permission, receipts, or decision log. |
| THREADM10 | What thread artifact is required? | Produce group summary, decision log, pinned message, or coordination note. |

## ATTCHM - Attachments Links And Evidence

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| ATTCHM01 | What file or link is needed? | Attach receipt, form, photo, scan, ticket, map, calendar invite, document, spreadsheet, or reference link. |
| ATTCHM02 | What attachment is missing? | Identify absent photo, signed form, address, confirmation number, screenshot, or proof. |
| ATTCHM03 | What file format is safest? | Choose PDF, image, text, shared link, compressed file, or printed letter depending on recipient and device. |
| ATTCHM04 | What file name helps retrieval? | Use date, topic, person, document type, and version in a clear name. |
| ATTCHM05 | What link permission should be checked? | Confirm viewer access, expiration, edit rights, download limits, and recipient identity. |
| ATTCHM06 | What privacy redaction is needed? | Remove account numbers, IDs, addresses, faces, health data, grades, or unrelated message content. |
| ATTCHM07 | What evidence should be quoted instead? | Use short excerpt or summary when full attachment is too private, large, or distracting. |
| ATTCHM08 | What attachment order helps? | Put primary form, proof, supporting screenshots, and optional details in a logical order. |
| ATTCHM09 | What confirmation should mention attachments? | Ask recipient to confirm receipt, ability to open, or whether another format is needed. |
| ATTCHM10 | What attachment artifact is required? | Produce attachment checklist, redaction note, file bundle, or proof index. |

## MSGPRV - Privacy Safety And Consent

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MSGPRV01 | What private data appears in the message? | Detect address, phone, ID, account, medical, school, financial, location, child, or relationship details. |
| MSGPRV02 | Who has consented to sharing? | Check permission for photos, stories, screenshots, group news, contact details, and third-party records. |
| MSGPRV03 | What should be redacted before sending? | Remove unrelated names, account numbers, images, metadata, message history, or sensitive attachments. |
| MSGPRV04 | What channel is unsafe for this content? | Avoid public comments, shared devices, insecure links, group chats, or workplace systems for private matters. |
| MSGPRV05 | What safety risk exists? | Watch harassment, stalking, coercion, retaliation, doxxing, scams, phishing, or escalating conflict. |
| MSGPRV06 | What proof should be preserved before responding? | Save screenshots, headers, timestamps, links, receipts, and call notes when records matter. |
| MSGPRV07 | What message should not be sent by the agent? | Block impersonation, threats, fraud, harassment, spam, sensitive disclosure, or unauthorized commitments. |
| MSGPRV08 | What identity check is needed? | Verify sender, domain, phone number, official portal, payment request, and suspicious links. |
| MSGPRV09 | What professional handoff is needed? | Route legal, emergency, HR, medical, law enforcement, or crisis matters to appropriate support. |
| MSGPRV10 | What privacy artifact is required? | Produce redaction checklist, safe-channel note, scam check, or privacy-safe message. |

## DECIDM - Declines Decisions And Boundaries

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| DECIDM01 | What decision must be communicated? | Accept, decline, postpone, choose option, stop participation, set limit, or close request. |
| DECIDM02 | What reason should be shared? | Share concise reason, constraint, priority, policy, budget, schedule, or no detailed reason when appropriate. |
| DECIDM03 | What alternative can be offered? | Suggest another time, person, resource, smaller commitment, different format, or future check-in. |
| DECIDM04 | What boundary should be explicit? | Clarify availability, topic limit, money limit, contact method, deadline, or repeated-request rule. |
| DECIDM05 | What wording avoids overexplaining? | Use short, kind, firm sentences without excessive apology or debate invitation. |
| DECIDM06 | What decision needs documentation? | Preserve final choice, consent, cancellation, refusal, agreement, or changed responsibility. |
| DECIDM07 | What pushback response is needed? | Prepare repeat boundary, restate decision, offer final option, or stop replying. |
| DECIDM08 | What relationship repair is needed? | Add appreciation, regret, future openness, or acknowledgement when a decline affects someone. |
| DECIDM09 | What risky decision should be delayed? | Pause before emotional, legal-adjacent, financial, unsafe, or irreversible messages. |
| DECIDM10 | What decision artifact is required? | Produce decline draft, boundary script, decision record, or pushback response. |

## MSGREC - Records Archive And Retrieval

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MSGREC01 | What correspondence should be saved? | Save confirmations, agreements, receipts, notices, provider instructions, complaints, decisions, and important updates. |
| MSGREC02 | What can be deleted? | Remove spam, duplicates, outdated drafts, irrelevant newsletters, and sensitive copies no longer needed. |
| MSGREC03 | What naming or tagging helps retrieval? | Tag by person, topic, date, obligation, event, money, school, health, housing, or travel. |
| MSGREC04 | What thread summary should be created? | Summarize dates, decisions, commitments, open questions, attachments, and next action. |
| MSGREC05 | What record retention boundary applies? | Follow user preference, legal/tax/admin need, school policy, platform terms, and privacy minimization. |
| MSGREC06 | What backup is needed? | Export important emails, download attachments, screenshot chats, print letters, or sync to document vault. |
| MSGREC07 | What retrieval question should the archive answer? | Enable finding who said what, when, with what attachment, and what next action followed. |
| MSGREC08 | What records should be separated? | Keep financial, medical, school, legal-adjacent, family, and conflict records in appropriate folders. |
| MSGREC09 | What archive risk exists? | Watch lost accounts, deleted chats, broken links, screenshots without context, or oversaved private data. |
| MSGREC10 | What record artifact is required? | Produce correspondence archive index, thread summary, retention note, or evidence packet. |

## MSGREV - Correspondence Review And Improvement

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MSGREV01 | What communication period should be reviewed? | Review day, week, event, project, household cycle, inbox cleanup, or relationship thread. |
| MSGREV02 | What messages worked well? | Note clear asks, fast confirmations, reduced confusion, good tone, saved records, and closed loops. |
| MSGREV03 | What messages caused friction? | Identify unclear asks, late replies, overlong drafts, missing attachments, tone issues, or wrong channel. |
| MSGREV04 | What recurring template is needed? | Create templates for thanks, scheduling, reminders, declines, follow-ups, requests, and confirmations. |
| MSGREV05 | What channel habit should change? | Adjust notification settings, labels, quiet hours, group rules, snooze, or weekly inbox review. |
| MSGREV06 | What relationship signal emerged? | Observe responsiveness, trust, boundaries, care, reliability, overload, or need for direct conversation. |
| MSGREV07 | What privacy lesson was learned? | Improve redaction, sharing permissions, screenshot handling, safe channels, and archive minimization. |
| MSGREV08 | What unanswered item remains? | Carry forward waiting replies, unresolved decisions, missing documents, and follow-up dates. |
| MSGREV09 | What automation or tool could help? | Use calendar, reminders, labels, snippets, contact notes, shared docs, or connector if available. |
| MSGREV10 | What review artifact is required? | Produce message retrospective, template set, inbox rule update, or correspondence improvement plan. |
