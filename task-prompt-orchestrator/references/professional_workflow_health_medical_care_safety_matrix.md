# Professional Workflow Health Medical Care Safety Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle health information, medical content, patient/caregiver support, symptom organization, clinician visit preparation, care navigation, medication-safety questions, mental-health support, nutrition/fitness/wellness content, health records, public-health education, medical product claims, or healthcare privacy and escalation boundaries. This matrix supports safe information organization and care navigation; it does not replace clinician diagnosis, treatment, emergency care, or licensed professional judgment.

## Health Scope And Safety Boundary

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| HTHSCOPE01 | What health topic or care context is in scope? | Define symptom question, condition education, visit prep, records summary, medication safety, wellness, mental health, caregiving, or public-health content. |
| HTHSCOPE02 | Who is affected by the health workflow? | Identify adult, child, older adult, pregnant person, caregiver, clinician, patient advocate, employee, public audience, or vulnerable group. |
| HTHSCOPE03 | What role should the output play? | Keep to education, organization, questions for clinician, safety reminders, navigation, or supportive coaching rather than diagnosis or treatment. |
| HTHSCOPE04 | What professional boundary must be stated? | Clarify that medical decisions, diagnosis, medication changes, and emergency judgment require qualified healthcare professionals. |
| HTHSCOPE05 | What urgency or risk level is possible? | Classify routine information, concerning symptom, urgent care question, emergency red flag, crisis, medication risk, or vulnerable patient concern. |
| HTHSCOPE06 | What personal health data is present? | Identify PHI, identifiers, dates, test results, images, medications, clinician notes, insurance, or sensitive personal details. |
| HTHSCOPE07 | What consent or authority is needed? | Confirm user is the patient, caregiver, authorized helper, clinician, or working from de-identified/public material. |
| HTHSCOPE08 | What claims must not be made? | Avoid cure, guaranteed outcome, dosage directive, diagnosis, prognosis certainty, or product treatment claim without professional review. |
| HTHSCOPE09 | What compassionate tone is needed? | Use calm, non-alarming, nonjudgmental, inclusive, privacy-aware, and supportive language. |
| HTHSCOPE10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce health-scope note, safety boundary, privacy note, urgency classification, or clinician-review requirement. |

## Symptom Organization And Care-Seeking Triage

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SYMPTOM01 | What symptoms or concerns should be organized? | Capture symptom description, onset, duration, severity, pattern, location, triggers, relieving factors, and functional impact. |
| SYMPTOM02 | What red-flag escalation should be included? | Encourage urgent local emergency care for severe, sudden, worsening, life-threatening, or crisis symptoms. |
| SYMPTOM03 | What timeline helps the clinician? | Build chronology of symptoms, exposures, injuries, medications, tests, visits, and changes. |
| SYMPTOM04 | What context should be attached? | Include age range, pregnancy status when relevant, conditions, allergies, medications, travel, exposures, and recent procedures. |
| SYMPTOM05 | What should not be inferred? | Do not diagnose, rank diseases as certainty, dismiss symptoms, or tell the user to delay professional care. |
| SYMPTOM06 | What questions should be asked next? | Prepare clarifying questions about severity, timing, associated symptoms, risk factors, and care already received. |
| SYMPTOM07 | What visit-prep summary is helpful? | Summarize concerns, timeline, questions, records to bring, and what the user wants from the appointment. |
| SYMPTOM08 | What caregiver observation matters? | Record behavior changes, eating/drinking, sleep, mobility, pain signs, confusion, mood, and safety concerns. |
| SYMPTOM09 | What uncertainty caveat is required? | State that similar symptoms can have many causes and require clinician evaluation when concerning. |
| SYMPTOM10 | What symptom artifact is required? | Produce symptom timeline, visit-prep note, question list, care-seeking checklist, or escalation note. |

## Health Information And Source-Backed Education

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| HTHINFO01 | What health question should be answered with sources? | Frame condition overview, prevention, test meaning, procedure prep, wellness guidance, or care-navigation question. |
| HTHINFO02 | What source standard is required? | Prefer official public-health bodies, clinical institutions, peer-reviewed reviews, drug labels, guidelines, or clinician-provided material. |
| HTHINFO03 | What freshness matters? | Refresh current guidance for infectious disease, medication safety, screening, recalls, public health, and changing recommendations. |
| HTHINFO04 | What population limit applies? | State whether information applies to adults, children, pregnancy, older adults, immunocompromised people, or specific conditions. |
| HTHINFO05 | What reading level is needed? | Adapt explanation for patient, caregiver, student, clinician, public audience, or low-health-literacy reader. |
| HTHINFO06 | What uncertainty should be visible? | Distinguish strong evidence, emerging evidence, mixed evidence, expert opinion, and unknowns. |
| HTHINFO07 | What action should the reader take? | Encourage clinician discussion, urgent care when needed, record gathering, lifestyle question prep, or official resource review. |
| HTHINFO08 | What misinformation risk exists? | Avoid unsupported remedies, conspiracy claims, exaggerated supplements, fake cures, or misleading product claims. |
| HTHINFO09 | What citation or provenance is needed? | Attach source title, organization, date, URL/path, guideline version, or label reference when available. |
| HTHINFO10 | What education artifact is required? | Produce health info brief, FAQ, patient-friendly explainer, source table, or clinician-question sheet. |

## Clinician Visit Preparation And Shared Decision Support

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VISITPREP01 | What upcoming care interaction is being prepared? | Identify primary care, specialist, urgent care, therapy, pharmacy, lab, imaging, telehealth, or second-opinion visit. |
| VISITPREP02 | What goal should the visit accomplish? | Clarify diagnosis discussion, treatment options, test interpretation, medication review, referral, follow-up, or care plan. |
| VISITPREP03 | What records should be gathered? | List symptoms, medications, allergies, test results, images, prior notes, vitals, questions, and insurance/referral documents. |
| VISITPREP04 | What questions should the patient ask? | Prepare questions about options, benefits, risks, alternatives, warning signs, next steps, costs, and follow-up. |
| VISITPREP05 | What preference or constraint should be shared? | Include goals, values, side-effect concerns, access barriers, caregiving needs, work/school limits, and cultural needs. |
| VISITPREP06 | What communication aid is needed? | Create concise summary, one-page timeline, medication list, concern ranking, or post-visit note template. |
| VISITPREP07 | What shared-decision boundary applies? | Support comparing clinician-provided options; do not choose treatment without professional evaluation. |
| VISITPREP08 | What post-visit capture should happen? | Record diagnosis discussed, plan, medication changes, warning signs, follow-up, tests, referrals, and unanswered questions. |
| VISITPREP09 | What advocate or interpreter support may be needed? | Plan companion, interpreter, accessibility accommodation, care coordinator, or patient portal message. |
| VISITPREP10 | What visit artifact is required? | Produce visit agenda, one-page health summary, question list, post-visit checklist, or portal message draft. |

## Medication Pharmacy And Treatment Safety

| MEDSAFE02 | What must be referred to a professional? | Route dosing, stopping, switching, interactions, pregnancy, child dosing, adverse effects, overdose, and contraindications to clinician/pharmacist. |
| MEDSAFE03 | What medication list should be organized? | Build current meds, OTC, supplements, allergies, prior reactions, missed doses, and questions for review. |
| MEDSAFE04 | What safety signal needs urgent escalation? | Encourage urgent care or poison control/local emergency resources for overdose, severe reaction, breathing trouble, or alarming symptoms. |
| MEDSAFE05 | What label or official source should be checked? | Use prescription label, medication guide, official drug label, pharmacist instruction, or clinician note. |
| MEDSAFE06 | What adherence support is non-clinical? | Suggest reminders, routine pairing, pill organizer question, refill tracking, and clinician-approved plan review. |
| MEDSAFE07 | What side-effect tracking is useful? | Record symptom, timing, severity, dose context as user-provided, other factors, and when to contact clinician. |
| MEDSAFE08 | What treatment claim should be avoided? | Do not promise effectiveness, recommend unapproved use, or infer personal suitability. |
| MEDSAFE09 | What privacy caution applies? | Avoid exposing medication details, diagnoses, pharmacy IDs, or prescriber names unnecessarily. |
| MEDSAFE10 | What medication artifact is required? | Produce medication list, pharmacist question sheet, side-effect log, refill checklist, or safety escalation note. |

## Mental Health Crisis And Supportive Guidance

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MHSUPPORT01 | What mental-health or wellbeing concern is present? | Identify stress, anxiety, low mood, grief, burnout, relationship strain, trauma, loneliness, or crisis indicators. |
| MHSUPPORT02 | What crisis boundary must be checked? | Encourage immediate local emergency/crisis support for self-harm, harm to others, abuse, psychosis, or inability to stay safe. |
| MHSUPPORT03 | What supportive response is appropriate? | Use validation, grounding, practical next step, social support, professional help suggestion, and nonjudgmental language. |
| MHSUPPORT04 | What should not be done? | Do not diagnose, minimize risk, encourage isolation, give coercive advice, or replace licensed mental-health care. |
| MHSUPPORT05 | What coping step is safe and general? | Offer breathing, grounding, short walk, journaling, sleep routine, reaching out, or reducing immediate stressor when appropriate. |
| MHSUPPORT06 | What support network can be engaged? | Identify trusted person, clinician, counselor, crisis line, workplace/school support, caregiver, or community service. |
| MHSUPPORT07 | What sensitive context applies? | Consider minors, abuse, substance use, eating disorder, domestic violence, grief, trauma, disability, or cultural stigma. |
| MHSUPPORT08 | What follow-up encouragement is needed? | Suggest scheduling care, sharing concerns, documenting patterns, and checking in while avoiding pressure. |
| MHSUPPORT09 | What privacy boundary matters? | Protect disclosures, crisis details, therapy records, and third-party identities. |
| MHSUPPORT10 | What support artifact is required? | Produce supportive message, crisis-aware response, coping plan draft, help-seeking script, or check-in note. |

## Health Records Labs And Patient Data Summaries

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RECORDS01 | What health record material is being summarized? | Identify visit notes, labs, imaging reports, discharge papers, medication lists, wearable data, insurance letters, or portal messages. |
| RECORDS02 | What summary purpose applies? | Prepare for clinician visit, second opinion, personal tracking, caregiver handoff, insurance appeal, or patient education. |
| RECORDS03 | What facts should be extracted without interpretation? | Capture dates, diagnoses as written, tests, results, medications, procedures, recommendations, follow-up, and clinician names if needed. |
| RECORDS04 | What lab or report boundary applies? | Do not interpret abnormal results as diagnosis; encourage clinician discussion for meaning and next steps. |
| RECORDS05 | What timeline should be built? | Sequence symptoms, visits, tests, treatments, medication changes, hospitalizations, and follow-up instructions. |
| RECORDS06 | What sensitive data should be redacted? | Remove direct identifiers, record numbers, addresses, contact details, insurance IDs, and unrelated third-party data. |
| RECORDS07 | What discrepancy should be flagged? | Mark conflicting medication lists, missing results, unclear follow-up, inconsistent dates, or unanswered patient questions. |
| RECORDS08 | What patient-friendly translation is needed? | Explain terms in plain language while preserving uncertainty and professional review boundary. |
| RECORDS09 | What provenance should be kept? | Link each fact to document name, date, page/section, portal message, or user-provided source. |
| RECORDS10 | What records artifact is required? | Produce medical timeline, record summary, question list, medication reconciliation prep, or de-identified packet. |

## Healthcare Privacy Consent And Data Governance

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| HTHPRIV01 | What health data is being collected or processed? | Identify PHI, images, records, symptoms, medications, mental-health notes, genetic data, wearable data, or payment/insurance data. |
| HTHPRIV02 | What minimum necessary data is needed? | Collect only facts required for summary, visit prep, education, support, or de-identified analysis. |
| HTHPRIV03 | What identifiers should be removed? | Redact names, exact birthdates, addresses, phone/email, record IDs, insurance IDs, faces, and clinician details when unnecessary. |
| HTHPRIV04 | What permission basis applies? | Confirm patient consent, caregiver authority, clinician role, de-identification, public data, or institutional policy. |
| HTHPRIV05 | What sharing boundary applies? | Limit posting, publishing, emailing, uploading, connector use, model input, and third-party access. |
| HTHPRIV06 | What retention or deletion rule is needed? | Define how long records, summaries, logs, and generated outputs should be kept or deleted. |
| HTHPRIV07 | What secure handling is required? | Use controlled files, access restriction, encryption where available, no secrets, and careful local paths. |
| HTHPRIV08 | What vulnerable-person protection applies? | Add extra care for minors, elders, disabled people, crisis situations, domestic violence, and dependent patients. |
| HTHPRIV09 | What audit trail is appropriate? | Record source, consent basis, redaction, recipient, version, date, and review gate without overexposing data. |
| HTHPRIV10 | What privacy artifact is required? | Produce redaction checklist, consent note, minimum-data plan, sharing warning, or privacy review record. |
