# Professional Workflow Manufacturing Physical Production Quality Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must turn a design, CAD file, packaging concept, prototype, product spec, or physical deliverable into manufacturable, inspectable, traceable, shippable production work.

## Manufacturing Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MANUSCOPE01 | What physical item is being produced? | Define product, component, packaging, label, fixture, sample, tooling, or finished good boundary. |
| MANUSCOPE02 | What production stage is current? | Classify concept, engineering sample, prototype, pilot, tooling, first article, ramp, mass production, or sustaining production. |
| MANUSCOPE03 | What production quantity and cadence apply? | Capture one-off, sample batch, pilot lot, seasonal run, continuous production, or high-volume batch. |
| MANUSCOPE04 | What manufacturing method is expected? | Identify printing, cutting, folding, molding, machining, casting, forming, assembly, coating, electronics assembly, or packing. |
| MANUSCOPE05 | What production environment is needed? | Define lab, workshop, print shop, packaging plant, cleanroom, food-grade site, electronics line, or contract manufacturer. |
| MANUSCOPE06 | What physical constraints dominate? | Track dimensions, weight, tolerance, material, durability, shelf life, ergonomics, environment, and handling limits. |
| MANUSCOPE07 | What safety or compliance boundary applies? | Link product safety, labeling, flammability, food contact, child safety, electrical safety, transport, or workplace safety. |
| MANUSCOPE08 | What production owner is accountable? | Assign design, engineering, sourcing, manufacturing, quality, packaging, logistics, supplier, and release owners. |
| MANUSCOPE09 | What production deliverables are required? | Require drawings, CAD, BOM, spec sheet, process plan, QC plan, sample report, packing spec, and release record. |
| MANUSCOPE10 | What production decision is needed now? | Decide feasibility, quote readiness, prototype build, tooling release, supplier award, pilot approval, or mass production release. |

## DFM DFA Readiness

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| DFMREAD01 | Is the design manufacturable with the intended process? | Check geometry, feature size, draft, bend radius, print limits, tolerances, material behavior, and assembly access. |
| DFMREAD02 | Is the design assemblable at expected speed and quality? | Review part count, orientation, fasteners, adhesives, fixtures, access, error-proofing, and operator steps. |
| DFMREAD03 | What features drive cost or complexity? | Identify tight tolerances, special finishes, rare materials, manual steps, low yield features, and custom tooling. |
| DFMREAD04 | What feature should be simplified before release? | Propose geometry, material, fastening, finishing, folding, nesting, or labeling simplifications. |
| DFMREAD05 | What design rule must be checked? | Apply supplier DFM rules, print bleed, dieline clearance, mold rules, machining allowances, and assembly standards. |
| DFMREAD06 | What production error is likely? | Anticipate misorientation, mismatch, misprint, fold error, tolerance stack, cosmetic defect, or missing component. |
| DFMREAD07 | What design change improves yield? | Add locating features, poka-yoke, larger radii, looser tolerances, clearer marks, process windows, or standard parts. |
| DFMREAD08 | What DFM evidence should be captured? | Keep supplier feedback, annotated drawings, feasibility notes, risk list, and approved design changes. |
| DFMREAD09 | What unresolved manufacturability risk remains? | Mark open items, test needs, supplier assumptions, and signoff owner. |
| DFMREAD10 | What DFM gate must pass before tooling? | Require engineering, manufacturing, quality, supplier, and cost approval before tool commitment. |

## Materials And BOM

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MATBOM01 | What material or component list is required? | Build BOM with part numbers, descriptions, quantities, revision, supplier, unit, and substitute rules. |
| MATBOM02 | What material specification controls selection? | Define grade, thickness, finish, color, coating, strength, barrier, recyclability, food contact, or electrical properties. |
| MATBOM03 | What material source must be qualified? | Identify approved supplier, alternative supplier, certificate, lead time, MOQ, and country of origin. |
| MATBOM04 | What material compliance evidence is needed? | Request CoA, RoHS, REACH, FSC, recycled content, food-contact declaration, safety data sheet, or test report. |
| MATBOM05 | What cost or availability risk exists? | Flag volatile pricing, long lead time, allocation, obsolete parts, minimum order, and shipping constraints. |
| MATBOM06 | What substitution is allowed? | Define approved alternates, equivalence criteria, approval gate, labeling impact, and customer notification rule. |
| MATBOM07 | What material handling requirement applies? | Specify storage, humidity, temperature, shelf life, ESD, contamination control, and first-in-first-out use. |
| MATBOM08 | What BOM revision control is needed? | Link BOM version to drawings, CAD, samples, supplier quote, purchase order, and production lot. |
| MATBOM09 | What BOM validation should be run? | Check completeness, quantities, units, supplier status, revision match, cost rollup, and missing specs. |
| MATBOM10 | What BOM release artifact is required? | Produce approved BOM, material specs, compliance files, sourcing status, and change log. |

## Prototype And Sample Validation

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PROTOEV01 | What prototype type is needed? | Choose visual mockup, fit sample, functional sample, engineering sample, packaging sample, pilot sample, or golden sample. |
| PROTOEV02 | What prototype question must be answered? | Test appearance, fit, strength, usability, assembly, print quality, tooling risk, cost, or user acceptance. |
| PROTOEV03 | What sample build inputs are required? | Provide drawings, CAD, dieline, BOM, material specs, artwork, process notes, and acceptance criteria. |
| PROTOEV04 | What sample quantity and variation are needed? | Define count, colors, materials, suppliers, process variants, and destructive test units. |
| PROTOEV05 | What inspection should samples receive? | Check dimensions, fit, finish, function, print, color, barcode, assembly, packaging, and shipping condition. |
| PROTOEV06 | What test method validates the sample? | Use drop, compression, rub, moisture, temperature, aging, usability, fixture, or functional tests. |
| PROTOEV07 | What sample feedback must be recorded? | Capture defects, photos, measurements, reviewer comments, supplier notes, and required design changes. |
| PROTOEV08 | What sample approval status applies? | Classify approved, approved with changes, rejected, retest needed, or golden sample locked. |
| PROTOEV09 | What sample becomes the reference standard? | Store golden sample, master artwork, color standard, approved CAD, and signed sample record. |
| PROTOEV10 | What next gate follows sample validation? | Move to design revision, tooling, supplier quote, pilot build, compliance test, or production release. |

## Tooling And Process Plan

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|

| TOOLPROC02 | What process flow will produce the item? | Define operation sequence, machines, operators, inputs, outputs, inspection points, and handoffs. |
| TOOLPROC03 | What process parameter controls quality? | Specify temperature, pressure, speed, dwell, cure time, humidity, torque, feed rate, color profile, or calibration setting. |
| TOOLPROC04 | What setup instruction is required? | Provide machine setup, tool alignment, material loading, first-piece check, and startup approval. |
| TOOLPROC05 | What fixture or mistake-proofing is needed? | Add locating, orientation, gauges, sensors, templates, checklists, and interlocks. |
| TOOLPROC06 | What tooling qualification is required? | Run tool trial, dimensional check, capability study, first article, maintenance review, and approval. |
| TOOLPROC07 | What process failure mode must be controlled? | Link process FMEA, defect prevention, detection controls, reaction plan, and responsible owner. |
| TOOLPROC08 | What operator instruction is needed? | Create work instructions, visuals, acceptance examples, safety notes, and escalation steps. |
| TOOLPROC09 | What calibration or maintenance applies? | Define calibration intervals, preventive maintenance, spare parts, wear limits, and downtime response. |
| TOOLPROC10 | What process release record proves readiness? | Keep approved flow, parameters, tooling signoff, work instructions, and first-piece evidence. |

| SUPMAKE10 | What supplier release artifact is needed? | Store approved quote, supplier selection, quality terms, sample approval, schedule, and open risk log. |

## Quality Control Plan

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| QCPROC01 | What quality characteristics are critical? | Define critical-to-quality dimensions, function, appearance, print, safety, labeling, fit, and packaging attributes. |
| QCPROC02 | What inspection stages are required? | Set incoming, in-process, first-piece, patrol, final, pre-shipment, and receiving inspections. |
| QCPROC03 | What sampling plan applies? | Choose 100 percent inspection, AQL, statistical sampling, lot sampling, destructive testing, or audit sampling. |
| QCPROC04 | What measurement method is required? | Specify gauges, calipers, fixtures, color standards, barcode scans, torque tools, test rigs, and calibration status. |
| QCPROC05 | What acceptance criteria apply? | Define pass/fail limits, cosmetic standards, defect classes, tolerances, functional thresholds, and packaging condition. |
| QCPROC06 | What defect taxonomy is needed? | Classify critical, major, minor, cosmetic, functional, safety, labeling, and documentation defects. |
| QCPROC07 | What reaction plan follows a failure? | Define hold, sort, rework, scrap, deviation, escalation, supplier containment, and customer notification. |
| QCPROC08 | What quality record must be kept? | Capture inspection sheet, measurements, photos, lot number, inspector, sample size, defects, and disposition. |
| QCPROC09 | What quality trend should be monitored? | Track yield, defect rate, rework, scrap, complaint rate, supplier score, and process capability. |
| QCPROC10 | What quality release gate applies? | Require QC signoff before shipment, launch, customer delivery, or mass production continuation. |

## Dimensions Tolerances And Specifications

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| TOLSPEC01 | What dimensions control fit, function, or appearance? | Identify critical dimensions, reference datums, fold lines, holes, clearances, and stack-up paths. |
| TOLSPEC02 | What tolerance is realistic for the process? | Match tolerance to printing, cutting, folding, molding, machining, assembly, and measurement capability. |
| TOLSPEC03 | What specification controls color, finish, or texture? | Define Pantone, LAB, gloss, grain, coating, surface roughness, opacity, or finish standard. |
| TOLSPEC04 | What drawing standard should be used? | Apply title block, revision, units, scale, views, sections, notes, GD&T where needed, and material callouts. |
| TOLSPEC05 | What packaging dieline or artwork tolerance applies? | Specify bleed, safe zone, trim, fold, glue area, barcode quiet zone, print registration, and varnish mask. |
| TOLSPEC06 | What tolerance stack-up risk exists? | Analyze mating parts, accumulated variation, assembly clearance, packaging fit, and shipping compression. |
| TOLSPEC07 | What measurement reference is needed? | Define datum, fixture, measurement point, environmental condition, and tool precision. |
| TOLSPEC08 | What spec ambiguity must be removed? | Resolve missing units, unclear views, conflicting notes, unspecified finish, open tolerances, and undocumented assumptions. |
| TOLSPEC09 | What spec change requires approval? | Gate any change to critical dimensions, materials, color, safety notes, labeling, or fit interfaces. |
| TOLSPEC10 | What specification release package is required? | Provide controlled drawings, CAD, dielines, material specs, tolerance table, and approved revision. |

| PACKSHIP03 | What packout configuration applies? | Define units per carton, carton size, pallet pattern, insert order, accessory placement, and sealing method. |

| PACKSHIP05 | What warehouse handling constraint exists? | Account for stacking, forklift handling, humidity, temperature, shelf life, ESD, and storage orientation. |
| PACKSHIP06 | What shipping documentation is needed? | Prepare packing list, invoice, certificates, labels, customs docs, safety docs, and delivery requirements. |
| PACKSHIP07 | What packaging sustainability target applies? | Track material reduction, recyclability, recycled content, plastic reduction, right-sizing, and waste. |
| PACKSHIP08 | What logistics cost or damage risk exists? | Compare dimensional weight, breakage, returns, rework, freight mode, and handling complexity. |
| PACKSHIP09 | What receiving inspection should happen? | Check carton condition, count, label match, lot match, damage, moisture, and documentation. |
| PACKSHIP10 | What packout release evidence is needed? | Store packout spec, photos, test results, labels, pallet pattern, and shipment approval. |

## Nonconformance CAPA And Deviation

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| NCRCAPA01 | What nonconformance occurred? | Describe defect, affected item, lot, severity, detection point, evidence, and immediate impact. |
| NCRCAPA02 | What containment action is required? | Hold stock, stop shipment, quarantine lots, notify supplier, inspect inventory, and protect customers. |
| NCRCAPA03 | What disposition is allowed? | Decide use-as-is, rework, repair, scrap, return, downgrade, deviation approval, or concession. |
| NCRCAPA04 | What root cause analysis is needed? | Use 5 Whys, fishbone, process review, supplier review, measurement check, and design review. |
| NCRCAPA05 | What corrective action addresses root cause? | Update process, tooling, training, spec, supplier control, inspection, or design. |
| NCRCAPA06 | What preventive action avoids recurrence? | Add detection, poka-yoke, control plan update, audit, supplier qualification, or design rule. |
| NCRCAPA07 | What deviation approval is needed? | Capture temporary acceptance, risk assessment, customer approval, expiration, and affected lots. |
| NCRCAPA08 | What customer or regulator notice applies? | Determine whether defect triggers notification, recall, field action, credit, or report. |
| NCRCAPA09 | What effectiveness check is required? | Verify no recurrence, improved yield, closed complaints, passed audits, and completed training. |
| NCRCAPA10 | What NCR/CAPA record must be closed? | Store issue report, evidence, actions, approvals, verification, and lessons learned. |

| PRODCHG10 | What change closure evidence is required? | Store change request, impact assessment, approvals, validation, implementation proof, and post-change review. |
