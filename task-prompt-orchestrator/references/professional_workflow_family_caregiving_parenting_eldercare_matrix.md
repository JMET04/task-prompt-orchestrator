# Professional Workflow Family Caregiving Parenting Eldercare Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle family caregiving, parenting, childcare, eldercare, dependent care, school or family coordination, caregiving schedules, care routines, child development support, elder support, respite, care providers, family communication, care records, emergency plans, or caregiver sustainability. This matrix structures care coordination; it does not replace medical, legal, therapy, safeguarding, or emergency services.

## Family Caregiving Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| FAMCARE01 | Who needs care or support? | Identify child, teen, older adult, disabled family member, recovering person, dependent adult, or mixed household. |
| FAMCARE02 | What care outcome matters? | Define safety, routine stability, school success, independence, comfort, appointment readiness, family coordination, or caregiver relief. |
| FAMCARE03 | Who participates in the care system? | Map parents, guardians, relatives, aides, teachers, clinicians, social workers, neighbors, and emergency contacts. |
| FAMCARE04 | What authority or consent boundary applies? | Clarify guardian role, permission, privacy, medical proxy, school authorization, and professional handoff needs. |
| FAMCARE05 | What urgency level exists? | Separate routine planning, time-sensitive issue, safeguarding concern, health concern, or emergency requiring local services. |
| FAMCARE06 | What living or care setting matters? | Capture home, shared custody, school, daycare, assisted living, hospital discharge, respite site, or remote caregiving. |
| FAMCARE07 | What cultural or family preference matters? | Account for language, values, faith, food, discipline norms, privacy, gender comfort, and family decision style. |
| FAMCARE08 | What constraint shapes the plan? | Note work schedules, budget, transportation, distance, caregiver capacity, accessibility, documentation, and service availability. |
| FAMCARE09 | What professional boundary must be named? | Route diagnosis, treatment, legal rights, abuse, neglect, crisis, or eligibility decisions to qualified services. |
| FAMCARE10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce care brief, family map, boundary note, priority list, or care coordination charter. |
