# Professional Workflow AEC Real Estate Facilities Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle architecture, engineering, construction, real estate development, interior fit-out, property operations, facilities management, asset management, leasing, tenant experience, building maintenance, or built-environment projects.

## AEC Scope And Project Definition

| Code | Question | Prompt/workflow requirement |
|---|---|---|
| AECSCOPE01 | What built-environment workflow is being handled? | Define architecture, engineering, construction, interior fit-out, renovation, real estate development, leasing, facilities operations, or asset management scope. |
| AECSCOPE02 | What asset or site type is involved? | Identify residential, office, retail, hotel, industrial, healthcare, education, civic, mixed-use, infrastructure, landscape, campus, or temporary structure type. |
| AECSCOPE03 | Who are the primary stakeholders? | Map owner, developer, investor, architect, engineer, contractor, tenant, operator, facility team, authority, lender, insurer, community, and end users. |
| AECSCOPE04 | What lifecycle stage applies? | Classify feasibility, acquisition, concept, schematic design, design development, permitting, procurement, construction, commissioning, handover, operations, renovation, or disposition. |
| AECSCOPE05 | What business objective drives the project? | Record yield, occupancy, sale price, rent, operating cost, compliance, user experience, sustainability, resilience, brand, or public benefit goal. |
| AECSCOPE06 | What constraints define the envelope? | Capture budget, schedule, site, zoning, building code, utilities, financing, tenants, operations, logistics, heritage, climate, and safety constraints. |
| AECSCOPE07 | What deliverable is expected? | Define feasibility memo, design brief, drawing set, BIM model, cost plan, permit pack, tender pack, construction plan, lease deck, O&M plan, or asset report. |
| AECSCOPE08 | What authority or reviewer gates apply? | Identify planning authority, building department, fire marshal, accessibility reviewer, lender, insurer, board, tenant, operator, or commissioning authority. |
| AECSCOPE09 | What assumptions must be tracked? | Record site measurements, title data, utilities, code interpretation, market demand, cost escalation, construction method, occupancy, and operating assumptions. |
| AECSCOPE10 | What is explicitly out of scope? | Exclude unlicensed professional certification, structural signoff, legal title opinion, appraisal, tax advice, or safety-critical approval unless supplied by qualified reviewers. |
