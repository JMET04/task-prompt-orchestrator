# Professional Workflow IP Licensing Rights Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow uses, transforms, generates, distributes, publishes, or archives copyrighted material, code, prompts, datasets, CAD files, designs, images, audio, video, brand assets, trademarks, likeness, patents, standards, vendor deliverables, or any asset where ownership, license, attribution, commercial use, derivative rights, exclusivity, takedown, or rights evidence matters.

## Rights Scope

| Code | Rights question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RIGHTSCOPE01 | What asset or output needs a rights check? | Identify text, image, code, dataset, CAD file, packaging, prompt, template, brand asset, media, or report. |
| RIGHTSCOPE02 | What use is intended? | Classify internal use, client delivery, public release, commercial use, training, resale, derivative work, or archive. |
| RIGHTSCOPE03 | Who owns the original asset? | Identify user, employer, client, vendor, public source, open-source project, stock provider, platform, or unknown owner. |
| RIGHTSCOPE04 | Who should own the new output? | Define work-for-hire, client-owned, internal-owned, vendor-owned, jointly owned, licensed, or unknown ownership. |
| RIGHTSCOPE05 | What jurisdiction or platform rules matter? | Account for country, contract, marketplace, app store, stock site, model provider, or standards-body rules. |
| RIGHTSCOPE06 | What rights are out of scope? | Avoid assuming patent, trademark, privacy, publicity, moral rights, or sublicensing rights are cleared. |
| RIGHTSCOPE07 | What rights risk level applies? | Classify low, moderate, high, public, commercial, regulated, brand-sensitive, or litigation-sensitive use. |
| RIGHTSCOPE08 | What rights evidence is required? | Require license, contract, source URL, purchase receipt, permission, attribution, release, or provenance. |
| RIGHTSCOPE09 | What rights uncertainty must be disclosed? | Mark unknown, user-provided-only, unverified, inferred, or needs legal review. |
| RIGHTSCOPE10 | What rights scope artifact should be produced? | Leave a rights brief with asset, owner, intended use, license evidence, risk, and allowed uses. |

## Copyright Review

| Code | Rights question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| COPYREV01 | Is the source likely copyrighted? | Treat most creative text, images, video, audio, code, CAD, diagrams, and documents as protected unless proven otherwise. |
| COPYREV02 | What copyright exception might apply? | Identify quotation, commentary, education, research, interoperability, parody, or other narrow exceptions for review. |
| COPYREV03 | Is the amount used appropriate? | Limit excerpts, samples, screenshots, thumbnails, and copied text to the minimum needed. |
| COPYREV04 | Is the output too similar to a protected source? | Check substantial similarity, distinctive style, composition, character, melody, code structure, or trade dress. |
| COPYREV05 | Is transformation enough for the intended use? | Assess whether the new work adds original purpose, analysis, structure, or utility rather than copying. |
| COPYREV06 | Is the source public domain or expired? | Verify jurisdiction, publication date, author death date, government-work status, or explicit dedication. |
| COPYREV07 | What copyright material should be removed? | Remove excessive quotes, copied images, proprietary examples, scans, lyrics, scripts, or full-text reproductions. |
| COPYREV08 | What copyright review needs escalation? | Escalate high-value, public, commercial, disputed, or close-copy use to legal or rights owner. |
| COPYREV09 | What copyright notice is needed? | Add citation, attribution, license notice, copyright line, or use limitation where required. |
| COPYREV10 | What copyright evidence should be archived? | Keep source, excerpt scope, license, permission, review notes, and final approved use. |

## License Mapping

| Code | Rights question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| LICMAP01 | What license applies to the asset? | Identify proprietary, Creative Commons, open source, stock, platform, client, vendor, public domain, or unknown license. |
| LICMAP02 | What use does the license allow? | Check internal, commercial, modification, redistribution, sublicensing, attribution, and derivative rights. |
| LICMAP03 | What use does the license prohibit? | Check no-commercial, no-derivatives, share-alike, no-AI, field-of-use, geographic, or seat limits. |
| LICMAP04 | What license obligations attach to outputs? | Track attribution, source disclosure, copyleft, share-alike, notices, audit, or redistribution conditions. |
| LICMAP05 | What license is incompatible with the workflow? | Block licenses that conflict with commercial release, closed-source delivery, client ownership, or platform rules. |
| LICMAP06 | What license version matters? | Record exact license version, terms date, vendor plan, subscription tier, or contract version. |
| LICMAP07 | What license proof is needed? | Keep license text, URL, package metadata, receipt, contract, screenshot, or written permission. |
| LICMAP08 | What license ambiguity must be resolved? | Ask rights owner, choose alternative asset, or escalate when license is missing, vague, or contradictory. |
| LICMAP09 | What license notice must ship with the output? | Include attribution, third-party notices, open-source notices, stock credits, or license files. |
| LICMAP10 | What license map artifact should be produced? | Produce asset-to-license table with permissions, obligations, restrictions, and evidence. |

| DATARIGHTS08 | What dataset redistribution evidence is needed? | Keep license, export permission, provider terms, schema, version, and provenance. |
| DATARIGHTS09 | What data should be summarized instead of redistributed? | Use aggregate, derived metrics, excerpts, or synthetic examples when raw sharing is restricted. |
| DATARIGHTS10 | What data-rights artifact should be produced? | Produce data-rights table with source, allowed uses, restrictions, attribution, and retention. |

## Takedown And Infringement Response

| Code | Rights question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| TAKEDOWN01 | What infringement claim or takedown risk exists? | Track complaint, platform notice, rights-owner message, internal finding, or similarity concern. |
| TAKEDOWN02 | What asset or channel is affected? | Identify file, page, app, package, dataset, image, prompt, post, listing, or client deliverable. |
| TAKEDOWN03 | What immediate containment is needed? | Pause release, remove asset, disable link, unpublish, replace file, notify owner, or stop distribution. |
| TAKEDOWN04 | What evidence should be preserved? | Keep claim, source, accused asset, timestamps, permissions, licenses, communications, and versions. |
| TAKEDOWN05 | What response path applies? | Choose comply, dispute, modify, replace, seek license, escalate legal, or notify client. |
| TAKEDOWN06 | Who must decide the response? | Assign legal, rights owner, client, platform owner, product owner, or executive decision maker. |
| TAKEDOWN07 | What user or customer communication is needed? | Provide factual notice, replacement path, limitation, or status update without overclaiming. |
| TAKEDOWN08 | What remediation prevents recurrence? | Update asset library, license checks, attribution, review gates, vendor terms, or training. |
| TAKEDOWN09 | What takedown closure evidence is required? | Confirm removal, replacement, license, dispute result, platform status, and stakeholder notification. |
| TAKEDOWN10 | What takedown record should be archived? | Preserve claim, decision, actions, communications, evidence, and prevention updates. |
