# Professional Workflow Personal Mental Health Therapy Counseling Support Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle personal mental-health administration, therapist or counselor search, therapy appointments, psychiatry appointment coordination, treatment-goal notes, crisis resource planning, safety-plan organization, medication-record handoff, insurance or EAP billing, support groups, support network coordination, privacy and confidentiality boundaries, work or school communication boundaries, setback tracking, or recurring mental-health support review.

Use this for personal organization, appointment preparation, records, reminders, communication drafts, support coordination, and professional handoff preparation. Do not use it to diagnose, provide psychotherapy, assess suicide risk, provide crisis counseling, choose treatment, change medication or dose, interpret symptoms clinically, give legal advice, or replace clinicians, therapists, crisis hotlines, emergency services, insurers, employers, schools, or licensed professionals.

## MHSCOP - Mental Health Support Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| MHSCOP01 | What mental-health support situation is being organized? | Define therapy search, appointment admin, psychiatry coordination, crisis resources, EAP, records, support group, or review. |
| MHSCOP02 | Who is the workflow for? | Identify self, child, partner, parent, dependent, caregiver, student, employee, or authorized helper. |
| MHSCOP03 | What support type is involved? | Separate therapy, counseling, coaching-like support, psychiatry, peer support, crisis resources, workplace EAP, school support, or medical referral. |
| MHSCOP04 | What urgency boundary applies? | Distinguish routine planning, worsening stress, missed medication, appointment gap, safety concern, or immediate emergency requiring local crisis or emergency services. |
| MHSCOP05 | What user-stated concerns should be organized? | Capture symptoms, triggers, goals, barriers, preferences, and questions exactly as user-provided without diagnosing. |
| MHSCOP06 | What authority or professional owns decisions? | Map therapist, counselor, psychiatrist, primary-care clinician, crisis service, insurer, EAP, school counselor, or emergency responder. |
| MHSCOP07 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect diagnosis, therapy notes, medication list, crisis contacts, insurance IDs, school records, employment records, and consent status. |
| MHSCOP08 | What cultural, language, or access needs matter? | Note language, identity preference, disability access, telehealth need, cost constraints, faith/community context, and scheduling needs. |
| MHSCOP09 | What should not be decided here? | Do not decide diagnosis, risk level, treatment plan, medication change, hospitalization need, disability status, or legal right. |
| MHSCOP10 | What scope artifact should be produced? | Create mental-health admin brief, support map, appointment packet, privacy note, or professional handoff sheet. |
