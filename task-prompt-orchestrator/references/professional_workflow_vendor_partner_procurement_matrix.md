# Professional Workflow Vendor Partner Procurement Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow depends on external suppliers, agencies, contractors, platforms, data providers, software vendors, manufacturers, print shops, reviewers, consultants, partners, clients' systems, marketplaces, or any third party whose selection, contract, delivery, access, quality, risk, payment, or exit must be managed.

## Vendor Scope

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VENDSCOPE01 | What external party is needed? | Identify supplier, agency, contractor, platform, data provider, print shop, manufacturer, consultant, or partner. |
| VENDSCOPE02 | What outcome depends on the external party? | Link vendor work to deliverable, source, tool, channel, approval, manufacturing, support, or release. |
| VENDSCOPE03 | What work should stay internal? | Keep strategy, sensitive data, approvals, core IP, regulated decisions, or final accountability internal when required. |
| VENDSCOPE04 | What work can be outsourced safely? | Select bounded, specified, reviewable, reversible, or commodity work for external execution. |
| VENDSCOPE05 | What external boundary is high risk? | Flag access to secrets, customer data, regulated content, production systems, public claims, or physical safety. |
| VENDSCOPE06 | What vendor category applies? | Classify creative, technical, data, legal, finance, manufacturing, logistics, support, platform, or advisory vendor. |
| VENDSCOPE07 | What vendor timeline is needed? | Define request date, quote deadline, start, review, delivery, revision, payment, renewal, or exit dates. |
| VENDSCOPE08 | What vendor ownership stays with the workflow owner? | Preserve accountability for brief quality, approvals, acceptance, risk, communication, and final release. |
| VENDSCOPE09 | What vendor evidence is required? | Keep profile, proposal, quote, contract, access scope, delivery record, QA, invoice, and performance notes. |
| VENDSCOPE10 | What vendor scope artifact should be produced? | Produce vendor brief with outcome, scope, boundaries, risk, owner, timeline, and acceptance. |

## Requirements And Brief

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VENDREQ01 | What does the vendor need to deliver? | Define concrete artifact, service, milestone, sample, support, source file, or production output. |
| VENDREQ02 | What requirements must be unambiguous? | Specify scope, dimensions, format, specs, quality, language, platform, materials, data, and constraints. |
| VENDREQ03 | What acceptance criteria apply? | Define pass/fail tests, review method, tolerances, source evidence, approvals, and rework rules. |
| VENDREQ04 | What inputs will be provided? | List assets, source files, access, examples, brand rules, data, designs, CAD files, or documentation. |
| VENDREQ05 | What inputs must not be shared? | Exclude secrets, private data, unnecessary IP, internal reasoning, unrelated files, or restricted source material. |
| VENDREQ06 | What assumptions need vendor confirmation? | Confirm feasibility, lead time, dependencies, tooling, format, cost, risk, and responsibilities. |
| VENDREQ07 | What vendor questions should be answered before work starts? | Resolve ambiguity, exclusions, revisions, ownership, communication, timeline, and support. |
| VENDREQ08 | What sample or proof is required? | Request prototype, test print, sample data, demo build, proof sheet, mockup, or pilot output. |
| VENDREQ09 | What brief quality gate applies? | Review brief for completeness, contradictions, sensitive data, measurable criteria, and owner approval. |
| VENDREQ10 | What vendor brief artifact should be archived? | Keep final brief, requirements table, provided inputs, exclusions, and accepted assumptions. |

## Sourcing And Shortlist

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SOURCING01 | Where should candidate vendors be found? | Search approved lists, marketplaces, referrals, prior vendors, public directories, platform partners, or local suppliers. |
| SOURCING02 | What sourcing constraints apply? | Account for geography, language, certification, availability, budget, compliance, timeline, and conflict of interest. |
| SOURCING03 | What minimum qualification is required? | Require portfolio, references, license, certification, security posture, capacity, domain expertise, or tool compatibility. |
| SOURCING04 | What disqualifies a vendor? | Exclude missing capability, poor quality, unavailable timeline, policy conflict, legal risk, or unacceptable terms. |
| SOURCING05 | What comparison fields are needed? | Compare capability, cost, time, risk, quality, references, process, support, IP terms, and fit. |
| SOURCING06 | What incumbent vendor should be considered? | Check whether existing vendor, contract, integration, or relationship is suitable before sourcing new. |
| SOURCING07 | What diversity or resilience consideration applies? | Consider alternate suppliers, regional redundancy, single-point-of-failure, and vendor concentration. |
| SOURCING08 | What sourcing evidence should be captured? | Keep search path, candidate list, qualification notes, exclusions, and shortlist rationale. |
| SOURCING09 | What shortlist size is appropriate? | Choose single-source, two-bid, three-bid, open RFP, or approved-vendor path based on risk and policy. |
| SOURCING10 | What sourcing artifact should be produced? | Produce shortlist matrix with candidates, scores, risks, next steps, and recommendation. |

## RFP And Quote

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RFPQUOTE01 | Is a formal RFP or simple quote needed? | Choose request style based on spend, complexity, risk, policy, and vendor uncertainty. |
| RFPQUOTE02 | What must the vendor proposal include? | Ask for scope, method, timeline, deliverables, assumptions, exclusions, cost, team, risks, and terms. |
| RFPQUOTE03 | What pricing structure is acceptable? | Compare fixed price, time and materials, milestone, subscription, usage-based, retainer, or unit pricing. |
| RFPQUOTE04 | What response deadline applies? | Set proposal due date, clarification window, decision date, and delivery date. |
| RFPQUOTE05 | What clarification process is needed? | Provide Q&A, addenda, shared answers, call notes, and revised proposal handling. |
| RFPQUOTE06 | What confidentiality rule applies to bidding? | Share only necessary materials and protect competitive, client, or personal data. |
| RFPQUOTE07 | What evaluation criteria should be disclosed? | State how cost, quality, timeline, experience, risk, and compliance will be weighted. |
| RFPQUOTE08 | What quote assumptions need challenge? | Test unrealistic timelines, hidden fees, missing revisions, undefined acceptance, and vague exclusions. |
| RFPQUOTE09 | What quote evidence should be preserved? | Archive requests, responses, Q&A, quote versions, assumptions, exclusions, and comparison notes. |
| RFPQUOTE10 | What RFP/quote artifact should be produced? | Produce request packet, vendor response table, cost comparison, and award recommendation. |

## Evaluation And Selection

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VENDEVAL01 | What scoring model should be used? | Score capability, quality, timeline, cost, risk, compliance, communication, and strategic fit. |
| VENDEVAL02 | What proof should validate claims? | Request samples, references, case studies, pilot, certifications, security docs, or live demo. |
| VENDEVAL03 | What tradeoff is acceptable? | Balance cheaper versus faster, higher quality versus longer timeline, familiar vendor versus better fit. |
| VENDEVAL04 | What hidden risk should be checked? | Check subcontracting, capacity, IP ownership, data handling, financial stability, dependency, and vendor lock-in. |
| VENDEVAL05 | What stakeholder input is needed? | Consult technical owner, legal, finance, security, operations, end user, client, or manufacturing reviewer. |
| VENDEVAL06 | What selection bias should be avoided? | Avoid choosing solely by lowest price, familiarity, polished pitch, or unavailable perfect option. |
| VENDEVAL07 | What selection decision needs approval? | Gate high-spend, strategic, regulated, security-sensitive, or client-facing vendor choices. |
| VENDEVAL08 | What rejection feedback should be sent? | Provide concise, professional decline notice when appropriate and policy allows. |
| VENDEVAL09 | What selection rationale should be recorded? | Record scores, evidence, tradeoffs, risks, approver, and final vendor. |
| VENDEVAL10 | What evaluation artifact should be archived? | Keep scorecard, reference checks, pilot results, approval, and vendor decision memo. |

## Contract And Commercial Terms

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VNDCONTRACT01 | What agreement type is needed? | Choose NDA, MSA, SOW, PO, license, subscription, data-processing agreement, or informal written confirmation. |
| VNDCONTRACT02 | What scope must be contractual? | Include deliverables, milestones, acceptance, revisions, support, exclusions, dependencies, and change control. |
| VNDCONTRACT03 | What commercial terms matter? | Define price, payment schedule, expenses, taxes, renewal, termination, penalties, and refund rules. |
| VNDCONTRACT04 | What IP and usage rights are needed? | Specify ownership, license, source files, exclusivity, derivative rights, attribution, and reuse rights. |
| VNDCONTRACT05 | What confidentiality and data terms are needed? | Cover NDA, data processing, security controls, breach notice, retention, deletion, and subprocessors. |
| VNDCONTRACT06 | What SLA or service terms are needed? | Define uptime, response time, support hours, defect fixes, escalation, maintenance, and credits. |
| VNDCONTRACT07 | What compliance terms are required? | Include legal, procurement, accessibility, industry, export, insurance, certification, and audit obligations. |
| VNDCONTRACT08 | What termination and exit terms are needed? | Define notice, transition help, data return, deletion, final payment, and continuity. |
| VNDCONTRACT09 | What contract review is required? | Route legal, finance, security, procurement, or executive review when thresholds apply. |
| VNDCONTRACT10 | What contract artifact should be retained? | Store executed agreement, SOW, PO, approvals, amendments, and obligation summary. |

## Onboarding And Access

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VENDONB01 | What onboarding information does the vendor need? | Provide brief, contacts, schedule, tools, file locations, style rules, examples, and communication norms. |
| VENDONB02 | What access should the vendor receive? | Grant least-privilege access to only necessary files, systems, repos, channels, dashboards, or environments. |
| VENDONB03 | What access should be time-limited? | Set expiration for links, accounts, VPN, credentials, shared drives, and staging access. |
| VENDONB04 | What security training or acknowledgement is needed? | Require confidentiality, privacy, brand, safety, system-use, or client-specific acknowledgement. |
| VENDONB05 | What working cadence should be set? | Define kickoff, check-ins, demos, status updates, review windows, and escalation path. |
| VENDONB06 | What tool or format setup is needed? | Align on file formats, naming, CAD export, design tools, code repo, issue tracker, or delivery portal. |
| VENDONB07 | What acceptance workflow should the vendor understand? | Explain review owners, QA steps, revision limits, signoff, and payment link. |
| VENDONB08 | What onboarding blocker should stop work? | Stop on missing agreement, missing access control, unclear brief, security issue, or missing owner. |
| VENDONB09 | What onboarding evidence should be captured? | Keep kickoff notes, access list, acknowledgements, schedule, contacts, and readiness checklist. |
| VENDONB10 | What onboarding artifact should be produced? | Produce vendor onboarding pack with brief, access, cadence, contacts, acceptance, and escalation. |

## Vendor Delivery Management

| VENDDELIV08 | What delivery evidence should be kept? | Store submitted files, timestamps, comments, version, acceptance notes, and change requests. |
| VENDDELIV09 | What delivery issue should become a formal issue? | Track missed date, quality failure, scope dispute, access problem, cost overrun, or communication breakdown. |
| VENDDELIV10 | What delivery management artifact should exist? | Maintain milestone tracker, status log, decisions, blockers, and accepted deliverables. |

| VENDQA08 | What final acceptance evidence is needed? | Capture reviewer signoff, checklist, test result, sample approval, client acceptance, or certification. |
| VENDQA09 | What quality lesson should be fed back? | Update vendor brief, standard, sample, checklist, contract, or preferred-vendor notes. |
| VENDQA10 | What vendor QA artifact should be archived? | Keep review record, defects, revisions, acceptance evidence, and final approved package. |

## Change And Dispute

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VENDCHANGE01 | What vendor change request exists? | Track scope, cost, schedule, format, resource, spec, dependency, or acceptance change. |
| VENDCHANGE02 | Who can approve vendor changes? | Define buyer, project owner, client, procurement, finance, legal, or executive approval. |
| VENDCHANGE03 | What change impact must be assessed? | Evaluate cost, timeline, quality, legal, risk, downstream release, dependencies, and acceptance. |
| VENDCHANGE04 | What change should be rejected? | Reject vague, unnecessary, unapproved, out-of-budget, high-risk, or contract-violating changes. |
| VENDCHANGE05 | What dispute needs formal handling? | Escalate disagreement over scope, quality, payment, ownership, delay, confidentiality, or acceptance. |
| VENDCHANGE06 | What dispute evidence is needed? | Preserve brief, contract, messages, versions, submitted files, review notes, and decision history. |
| VENDCHANGE07 | What negotiation options exist? | Consider rework, discount, deadline shift, scope reduction, replacement, partial acceptance, or termination. |
| VENDCHANGE08 | What change communication is required? | Notify stakeholders of impact, decision, timeline, owner, and revised expectations. |
| VENDCHANGE09 | What contract amendment is needed? | Update SOW, PO, milestone, price, acceptance criteria, or delivery date when material. |
| VENDCHANGE10 | What change/dispute artifact should be kept? | Archive request, impact assessment, decision, amendment, and communication. |

| VENDPAY09 | What vendor should be preferred or avoided later? | Update preferred list, watchlist, blacklist, or conditional-use notes based on evidence. |
| VENDPAY10 | What payment/performance artifact should be archived? | Keep invoice match, approval, payment status, scorecard, and renewal decision. |

## Offboarding And Exit

| Code | Vendor question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| VENDOFF01 | What triggers vendor offboarding? | Trigger on project close, contract end, termination, replacement, poor performance, risk, or scope completion. |
| VENDOFF02 | What access must be revoked? | Remove accounts, shared links, repo access, cloud roles, VPN, secrets, drives, dashboards, and channels. |
| VENDOFF03 | What data or materials must be returned? | Collect source files, editable assets, data exports, credentials, documentation, proofs, and final packages. |
| VENDOFF04 | What data must be deleted or certified deleted? | Require deletion of private, client, regulated, proprietary, or obsolete working materials. |
| VENDOFF05 | What final acceptance or handoff is needed? | Confirm final deliverables, open issues, documentation, support transition, and owner handoff. |
| VENDOFF06 | What final payment or refund decision remains? | Close invoices, credits, retainers, refunds, penalties, and tax documents. |
| VENDOFF07 | What continuity risk exists after exit? | Plan replacement, internal knowledge transfer, documentation, support coverage, and data access. |
| VENDOFF08 | What vendor relationship note should be kept? | Record performance, lessons, recommendation, constraints, and future-use status. |
| VENDOFF09 | What offboarding verification is required? | Verify access removal, data return/deletion, final package, invoice close, and knowledge transfer. |
| VENDOFF10 | What offboarding artifact should be archived? | Keep offboarding checklist, revocation proof, final deliverables, deletion proof, and relationship summary. |
