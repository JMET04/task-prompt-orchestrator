# Professional Workflow Standards Certification Lab Testing Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow depends on product standards, certification marks, third-party lab testing, conformance evidence, safety/performance validation, regulatory test reports, surveillance audits, or certificate maintenance.

## Standards Certification Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| STDSCOPE01 | What product, service, system, process, or claim needs standard-based validation? | Define whether the subject is a physical product, package, component, software, AI system, process, facility, material, or public claim. |
| STDSCOPE02 | What market or channel requires certification? | Identify countries, customer requirements, retailers, marketplaces, distributors, government buyers, or regulated launch channels. |
| STDSCOPE03 | What certification goal is needed? | Choose compliance evidence, safety mark, performance rating, environmental label, management-system certification, or customer qualification. |
| STDSCOPE04 | What lifecycle stage is current? | Classify concept, design freeze, prototype, pre-test, formal test, certification submission, launch, surveillance, renewal, or redesign. |
| STDSCOPE05 | What risk level does nonconformance create? | Rate safety, legal, launch delay, recall, shipment hold, customer rejection, claim challenge, or brand risk. |
| STDSCOPE06 | What evidence already exists? | Inventory prior reports, certificates, declarations, supplier documents, lab data, design files, and previous approvals. |
| STDSCOPE07 | What authority will accept the evidence? | Map regulator, certification body, notified body, test lab, customer, retailer, auditor, or internal approver. |
| STDSCOPE08 | What standard version or effective date matters? | Track edition, amendment, transition deadline, withdrawal date, and grandfathering rule. |
| STDSCOPE09 | What item variant scope applies? | Define model families, sizes, colors, materials, SKUs, firmware versions, packaging variants, and private-label versions. |
| STDSCOPE10 | What standards deliverable is required? | Produce standards matrix, test plan, certification dossier, declaration, lab report, mark approval, or renewal packet. |

## Requirement And Standard Mapping

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| STDREQ01 | Which standards, regulations, protocols, or customer specs apply? | Build a controlled list from official standards, schemes, laws, customer manuals, and certification body rules. |
| STDREQ02 | Which clauses or test methods are in scope? | Break standards into applicable clauses, exclusions, test methods, samples, and evidence fields. |
| STDREQ03 | What product characteristics trigger each requirement? | Map voltage, radio function, food contact, material, age grade, load, chemical, software function, or packaging route. |
| STDREQ04 | What standard conflicts or overlaps exist? | Resolve duplicate tests, conflicting thresholds, market-specific deviations, and stricter customer requirements. |
| STDREQ05 | What exemption or reduced testing path may apply? | Capture rationale, eligibility, source, reviewer, and fallback if exemption is rejected. |
| STDREQ06 | What requirement owner is accountable? | Assign engineering, quality, regulatory, compliance, packaging, security, sustainability, supplier, or lab owner. |
| STDREQ07 | What evidence satisfies each clause? | Link test report, drawing, BOM, risk analysis, declaration, supplier certificate, inspection, or audit result. |
| STDREQ08 | What requirement is unverified or ambiguous? | Mark unknowns, request interpretation, contact lab/certifier, or escalate to regulatory specialist. |
| STDREQ09 | What requirement traceability is needed? | Link requirement, product variant, design input, test method, sample, result, approval, and release gate. |
| STDREQ10 | What standards matrix should be maintained? | Create a versioned compliance matrix with source, applicability, owner, status, evidence, and review date. |

## Test Strategy And Plan

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| TESTPLAN01 | What tests are required before trust or launch? | Define pre-compliance, engineering, qualification, formal certification, production, surveillance, or customer acceptance tests. |
| TESTPLAN02 | What test objective does each test serve? | Separate safety, performance, reliability, compatibility, labeling, durability, chemical, environmental, packaging, or usability goals. |
| TESTPLAN03 | What test sequence is best? | Run cheap screening before expensive formal tests, and destructive tests after non-destructive inspections. |
| TESTPLAN04 | What sample size and configuration are required? | Specify count, variants, worst case, accessories, firmware, packaging state, conditioning, and destructive reserves. |
| TESTPLAN05 | What pass/fail threshold applies? | Define exact limit, tolerance, acceptance rule, sample rule, retest policy, and uncertainty handling. |
| TESTPLAN06 | What pre-test review must happen? | Check drawings, BOM, labels, instructions, firmware, risk analysis, calibration, and sample readiness. |
| TESTPLAN07 | What test dependencies affect timing? | Track lab slots, sample build, conditioning time, fixture needs, certification body review, and report turnaround. |
| TESTPLAN08 | What budget and lead time are required? | Estimate lab cost, shipping, sample cost, retest reserve, certification fees, and schedule buffer. |
| TESTPLAN09 | What test risk should be mitigated early? | Identify likely failures, run pre-checks, redesign risk areas, and prepare alternate labs or test paths. |
| TESTPLAN10 | What approved test plan artifact is needed? | Store test matrix, samples, methods, schedule, responsible owners, lab, cost, and acceptance gates. |

| LABQUAL09 | What lab timeline risk exists? | Track backlog, sample arrival, fixture lead time, holiday closures, retest loops, and certifier review queue. |
| LABQUAL10 | What lab qualification evidence should be kept? | Preserve accreditation, scope, quote, purchase order, communication, sample receipt, and final deliverables. |

## Sample Selection And Preparation

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SAMPLESEL01 | Which samples represent the certified product? | Choose final-equivalent samples, worst-case variants, production-intent builds, or representative families. |
| SAMPLESEL02 | What sample configuration must be locked? | Record hardware, material, firmware, artwork, label, packaging, accessories, and revision. |
| SAMPLESEL03 | What conditioning or pre-treatment is required? | Define aging, humidity, temperature, charge state, cleaning, sterilization, drop preconditioning, or storage exposure. |
| SAMPLESEL04 | What sample labeling is needed? | Add sample ID, variant, revision, lot, date, test sequence, and chain-of-custody labels. |
| SAMPLESEL05 | What sample quantity covers all tests? | Include destructive tests, spares, repeats, retained samples, control samples, and customer or certifier samples. |
| SAMPLESEL06 | What sample build evidence is required? | Keep build record, BOM, process conditions, inspection status, photos, and release approval. |
| SAMPLESEL07 | What sample deviation must be disclosed? | Note prototype shortcuts, nonproduction tooling, substitute materials, missing labels, or firmware differences. |
| SAMPLESEL08 | What shipment and handling instructions apply? | Specify packaging, temperature, hazardous goods, customs docs, insurance, chain of custody, and arrival check. |
| SAMPLESEL09 | What retained sample policy applies? | Keep golden samples, failed samples, tested samples, and witness samples for retest or audit. |
| SAMPLESEL10 | What sample readiness gate must pass? | Confirm samples match test plan, are documented, inspected, labeled, shipped, and accepted by the lab. |

| TESTEXEC09 | What execution status should be reported? | Track not started, in progress, blocked, failed, passed, pending report, pending certifier review, or retest. |
| TESTEXEC10 | What execution record proves integrity? | Preserve method, setup, sample IDs, raw data, deviations, approvals, and lab notes. |

## Result Review And Technical Judgment

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RESULTREV01 | Did each test meet acceptance criteria? | Compare measured values to limits, uncertainty, sample rule, and required reporting format. |
| RESULTREV02 | Is the report complete and traceable? | Check sample IDs, revision, method, lab accreditation, dates, photos, raw data, and signatures. |
| RESULTREV03 | Are any results marginal or fragile? | Flag near-limit passes, high variability, conditional passes, inconsistent samples, and unclear notes. |
| RESULTREV04 | Do failures require root cause? | Trigger design review, material review, process review, sample audit, or method clarification. |
| RESULTREV05 | What design or process change follows results? | Link pass/fail data to redesign, supplier change, process control, labeling, or test strategy update. |
| RESULTREV06 | What retest or additional test is needed? | Decide partial retest, full retest, alternate method, expanded variant coverage, or certifier review. |
| RESULTREV07 | What claim can the result support? | Limit marketing, safety, performance, sustainability, or compliance claims to tested scope. |
| RESULTREV08 | What report limitations must be disclosed? | Note sample scope, prototype status, excluded clauses, market limits, expiration, and non-transferability. |
| RESULTREV09 | What approval decision can be made? | Decide approve, approve with conditions, block launch, redesign, retest, or escalate. |
| RESULTREV10 | What result review artifact is required? | Create report review checklist, gap log, decision note, evidence links, and next action owner. |

## Failure Remediation And Retest

|---|---|---|

| FAILREMED03 | What root cause path should be used? | Review design, material, supplier, process, sample build, test setup, and standard interpretation. |
| FAILREMED04 | What corrective option is available? | Modify design, change material, add warning, update process, change supplier, revise firmware, or narrow scope. |

| FAILREMED07 | What communication is needed during failure? | Update stakeholders, supplier, lab, customer, launch team, and management with status and decisions. |
| FAILREMED08 | What approval is required before retest? | Require design, quality, regulatory, lab, supplier, and commercial approval for fix and retest. |
| FAILREMED09 | What evidence proves remediation worked? | Store root cause, fix, new samples, retest report, comparison, approvals, and release decision. |
| FAILREMED10 | What prevention update follows? | Update design rules, supplier controls, test plan, sample checklist, training, and standards matrix. |
