# Professional Workflow Public Safety Emergency Crisis Response Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle public safety, emergency preparedness, crisis response, disaster planning, alerts, evacuation, incident command, field response, sheltering, relief logistics, vulnerable populations, crisis communications, recovery, drills, or after-action learning. This matrix structures preparedness and coordination work; it does not replace local emergency services or official instructions.

## Emergency Scope And Safety Boundary

| Code | Question | Prompt/workflow requirement |
|---|---|---|
| EMRGSCOPE01 | What emergency or hazard is involved? | Identify fire, flood, storm, earthquake, heat, outage, violence, accident, chemical release, cyber incident, epidemic, or infrastructure failure. |
| EMRGSCOPE02 | Who is affected? | Map residents, staff, students, patients, customers, visitors, children, older adults, disabled people, pets, and responders. |
| EMRGSCOPE03 | What authority is active? | Identify local emergency services, incident commander, facility lead, school official, public agency, utility, or organizational crisis team. |
| EMRGSCOPE04 | What phase applies? | Classify preparedness, warning, immediate response, stabilization, sheltering, relief, recovery, mitigation, or after-action review. |
| EMRGSCOPE05 | What immediate life-safety rule applies? | Prioritize evacuation, shelter-in-place, first responder call, medical escalation, fire safety, violence avoidance, or official instructions. |
| EMRGSCOPE06 | What information is reliable now? | Use official alerts, emergency services, weather service, utility notice, facility sensors, verified staff reports, and direct observation. |
| EMRGSCOPE07 | What uncertainty must be stated? | Mark unknown hazard extent, casualty count, road status, utility status, weather, access, communications, and resource availability. |
| EMRGSCOPE08 | What safety boundary limits advice? | Avoid telling people to ignore authorities, enter unsafe areas, perform risky rescues, or delay emergency services. |
| EMRGSCOPE09 | What coordination objective matters? | Define protect life, account for people, communicate instructions, mobilize resources, stabilize operations, or support recovery. |
| EMRGSCOPE10 | What emergency artifact is needed? | Produce safety brief, action checklist, contact tree, incident log, status board, or recovery plan. |

## Crisis Public Communications And Trust

| Code | Question | Prompt/workflow requirement |
|---|---|---|
| CRISCOMMS01 | Who needs information? | Identify affected people, families, staff, public, media, authorities, partners, donors, vendors, and elected leaders. |
| CRISCOMMS02 | What message objective applies? | Provide protective action, status update, resource location, reassurance, correction, accountability, or recovery information. |
| CRISCOMMS03 | What spokesperson and approval path apply? | Assign public information lead, executive approver, legal/risk review, agency coordination, and backup spokesperson. |
| CRISCOMMS04 | What must every message include? | Include what happened, where, who is affected, action to take, source, time, uncertainty, and next update. |
| CRISCOMMS05 | What accessibility and language needs exist? | Provide plain language, translation, captions, alt text, ASL where needed, low-bandwidth format, and multiple channels. |
| CRISCOMMS06 | What rumor control is needed? | Monitor misinformation, correct quickly, cite sources, avoid speculation, and keep old guidance from circulating. |
| CRISCOMMS07 | What sensitive information must be protected? | Avoid names, medical details, minors, victims, security vulnerabilities, personal data, and unconfirmed casualty details. |
| CRISCOMMS08 | What media handling is required? | Prepare holding statement, Q&A, press briefing, media line, interview boundaries, and update schedule. |
| CRISCOMMS09 | What community trust repair is needed? | Acknowledge harm, explain actions, invite feedback, show resources, commit to review, and close the loop. |
| CRISCOMMS10 | What communications record should be kept? | Archive messages, approvals, timestamps, channels, corrections, media inquiries, and public feedback. |

## Drills Exercises And After Action Learning

| DRILLLEARN08 | What corrective actions are required? | Assign owner, action, due date, resource need, policy update, training update, and verification method. |
| DRILLLEARN09 | What plan updates follow? | Update contacts, maps, roles, supplies, communication templates, vendor lists, and training materials. |
| DRILLLEARN10 | What after-action artifact is required? | Produce after-action report, improvement plan, drill log, attendance record, and next exercise schedule. |
