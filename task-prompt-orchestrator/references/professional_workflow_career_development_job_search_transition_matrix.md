# Professional Workflow Career Development Job Search Transition Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle career planning, job search, resumes, portfolios, networking, interviews, offers, negotiation, onboarding, promotion, career growth, role transition, layoffs, career breaks, or professional reinvention.

## Career Scope And Direction

| Code | Question | Prompt/workflow requirement |
|---|---|---|
| CARRSCOPE01 | What career situation is active? | Define first job, job search, promotion, pivot, layoff, return to work, freelance move, leadership track, or portfolio career. |
| CARRSCOPE02 | What outcome is desired? | State target role, industry, compensation range, location, flexibility, growth path, timeline, and non-negotiables. |
| CARRSCOPE03 | What constraints shape the path? | Capture visa/work authorization, caregiving, geography, health, education, finances, risk tolerance, and time capacity. |
| CARRSCOPE04 | What career assets exist? | Inventory experience, skills, credentials, network, portfolio, projects, reputation, references, and achievements. |
| CARRSCOPE05 | What career friction exists? | Identify unclear direction, skill gap, weak resume, low confidence, age bias concern, employment gap, or market mismatch. |
| CARRSCOPE06 | What values should guide choices? | Clarify autonomy, stability, impact, learning, income, prestige, craft, mission, flexibility, and lifestyle fit. |
| CARRSCOPE07 | What risks need boundaries? | Flag burnout, toxic workplace, underemployment, relocation strain, contract instability, and credential debt. |
| CARRSCOPE08 | What evidence should be gathered? | Collect job posts, salary data, performance reviews, project metrics, testimonials, market signals, and informational interviews. |
| CARRSCOPE09 | What decision horizon applies? | Choose immediate search, 30-day sprint, quarterly plan, annual career strategy, or multi-year transition. |
| CARRSCOPE10 | What career artifact is required? | Produce career brief, target role map, transition plan, search tracker, or decision memo. |

## Role Path And Market Mapping

| Code | Question | Prompt/workflow requirement |
|---|---|---|
| ROLEPATH01 | What roles should be mapped? | List target, adjacent, stretch, fallback, bridge, contract, internal, and entrepreneurial roles. |
| ROLEPATH02 | What market demand exists? | Analyze job postings, hiring trends, company growth, industry cycles, geographic demand, and remote availability. |
| ROLEPATH03 | What role requirements repeat? | Extract skills, tools, credentials, outcomes, seniority signals, domain knowledge, and soft-skill patterns. |
| ROLEPATH04 | What level is realistic? | Compare scope, autonomy, leadership, years of experience, compensation, portfolio proof, and interview expectations. |
| ROLEPATH05 | What company types fit? | Compare startup, enterprise, agency, nonprofit, government, academia, consulting, remote-first, and regulated employers. |
| ROLEPATH06 | What role tradeoffs matter? | Compare pay, growth, workload, stability, mission, manager quality, commute, flexibility, equity, and learning. |
| ROLEPATH07 | What bridge experience is needed? | Identify projects, certifications, volunteering, internships, contract work, open source, or internal stretch assignments. |
| ROLEPATH08 | What market language should be reused? | Capture role titles, keywords, metrics, verbs, tools, and competency phrases for resume and profile matching. |
| ROLEPATH09 | What role should be deprioritized? | Exclude roles with poor fit, low demand, unrealistic requirements, harmful culture, or weak strategic value. |
| ROLEPATH10 | What role map artifact is required? | Produce target-role matrix, job-posting analysis, market map, or role comparison table. |
