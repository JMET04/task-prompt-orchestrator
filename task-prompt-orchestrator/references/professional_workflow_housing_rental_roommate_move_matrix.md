# Professional Workflow Housing Rental Roommate Move Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage personal housing search, rental applications, listings, viewing, roommates, lease review, move-in, utilities, maintenance requests, landlord communication, rent payment, move-out, or housing records.

This supports organization, comparison, communication, documentation, and personal decision workflows. It does not replace legal advice, tenant-union representation, licensed real-estate services, housing authority decisions, safety inspections, financial advice, or emergency services.

## Housing Rental Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RENTSC01 | What housing workflow is needed? | Classify search, viewing, application, lease, roommate, move-in, utilities, maintenance, rent, complaint, or move-out. |
| RENTSC02 | Who is involved in the housing decision? | Identify renter, roommate, partner, family, guarantor, landlord, property manager, broker, or housing program. |
| RENTSC03 | What housing type applies? | Distinguish apartment, room rental, shared house, dorm, sublet, short-term rental, senior housing, or family home. |
| RENTSC04 | What location constraints matter? | Capture commute, school, transit, safety, accessibility, parking, neighborhood, services, and jurisdiction. |
| RENTSC05 | What budget boundary applies? | Include rent, deposit, fees, utilities, insurance, parking, moving costs, furniture, and emergency buffer. |
| RENTSC06 | What deadline drives the process? | Track lease end, move-in date, school term, job start, notice period, application deadline, and utility setup. |
| RENTSC07 | What legal or policy boundary exists? | Use official sources for tenant rights, fair housing, deposits, notices, and local rules; route disputes to qualified help. |
| RENTSC08 | What safety or habitability concern exists? | Flag locks, smoke alarms, mold, heat, pests, structural issues, harassment, or unsafe access. |
| RENTSC09 | What records must be preserved? | Keep listings, applications, lease, payments, photos, messages, notices, repair requests, and inspection forms. |
| RENTSC10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce housing brief, budget sheet, search criteria, timeline, or document checklist. |

## Home Search Criteria

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| HOMEFIND01 | What must-have criteria define the search? | Set rent cap, bedrooms, location, commute, pets, accessibility, laundry, parking, and move-in date. |
| HOMEFIND02 | What nice-to-have criteria can flex? | Rank balcony, amenities, storage, view, gym, dishwasher, floor level, or style. |
| HOMEFIND03 | What dealbreakers apply? | Identify unsafe area, long commute, no pets, stairs, smoking, pests, noise, high fees, or poor management. |
| HOMEFIND04 | What budget formula is acceptable? | Compare rent-to-income, utilities, transport savings, deposits, upfront costs, and recurring fees. |

| HOMEFIND06 | What scam signals should be checked? | Flag below-market rent, pressure, no viewing, wire request, stolen photos, fake landlord, or off-platform payment. |
| HOMEFIND07 | What accessibility or care need exists? | Check stairs, elevators, door widths, bathroom setup, caregiver access, transit, and nearby services. |
| HOMEFIND08 | What lifestyle fit matters? | Consider noise, guests, work-from-home, cooking, pets, shared spaces, and sleep schedule. |
| HOMEFIND09 | What comparison method helps? | Score listings by criteria, risk, cost, commute, photos, questions, and decision status. |
| HOMEFIND10 | What search artifact is required? | Produce search scorecard, listing tracker, budget comparison, or shortlist. |
