# Professional Workflow Arts Culture Entertainment Attendance Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must manage arts, culture, entertainment attendance, movies, concerts, theater, museums, exhibitions, festivals, books, games, tickets, venues, companions, accessibility, etiquette, memberships, cultural subscriptions, collectibles, amateur creative participation, fan communities, or experience review.

This supports discovery, planning, budgeting, coordination, attendance, and personal culture-life organization. It does not replace professional media production, legal rights advice, venue security, emergency services, ticket-platform enforcement, or expert appraisal.

## Culture Entertainment Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CULTSC01 | What culture or entertainment workflow is needed? | Classify movie, concert, theater, museum, exhibition, festival, reading, gaming, membership, collecting, fan event, or creative outing. |
| CULTSC02 | Who is participating? | Identify solo attendee, friends, family, date, children, elders, visitors, accessibility users, fans, or club members. |
| CULTSC03 | What experience goal matters? | Define fun, learning, inspiration, bonding, fandom, relaxation, cultural exposure, celebration, or routine enrichment. |
| CULTSC04 | What constraints shape the plan? | Capture budget, time, location, age rating, accessibility, language, sensory needs, safety, and schedule. |
| CULTSC05 | What format applies? | Distinguish live event, screening, exhibition, tour, digital stream, game session, book club, workshop, or festival. |
| CULTSC06 | What boundary applies? | Separate personal attendance planning from rights advice, professional production, ticket enforcement, security, and expert valuation. |
| CULTSC07 | What source is authoritative? | Use official venue, artist, publisher, museum, festival, ticket platform, library, or organizer information. |
| CULTSC08 | What risk should be managed? | Note scams, sold-out tickets, accessibility gaps, unsafe crowd, spoilers, age-inappropriate content, or high costs. |
| CULTSC09 | What record should be kept? | Save tickets, receipts, schedule, membership info, notes, photos where allowed, and preference history. |
| CULTSC10 | What scope artifact is required? | Produce experience brief, outing plan, budget note, or culture calendar. |
