# Professional Workflow Pregnancy Prenatal Postpartum Newborn Family Admin Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must handle pregnancy and new-baby family administration, prenatal appointment coordination, pregnancy record organization, birth preference planning, hospital or birth-center logistics, postpartum support, newborn appointments, feeding support coordination, baby supplies, registration documents, insurance or benefits updates, parental leave coordination, family support networks, or recurring baby-stage review.

Use this for personal organization, scheduling, records, communication drafts, checklists, support coordination, and professional handoff preparation. Do not use it to provide fertility, pregnancy, obstetric, midwifery, doula, pediatric, lactation, medication, nutrition, mental-health, legal, insurance, employment, or benefits advice, and do not replace clinicians, emergency services, pharmacists, lactation consultants, mental-health professionals, lawyers, employers, insurers, or official agencies.

## PREGSC - Pregnancy Family Scope

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| PREGSC01 | What pregnancy or new-baby stage is being organized? | Define trying-to-plan admin, pregnancy, prenatal care, birth preparation, postpartum, newborn, infant, adoption-related baby prep, or support coordination. |
| PREGSC02 | Who is included in the family context? | Identify pregnant person, partner, co-parent, baby, siblings, grandparents, caregivers, chosen family, doula, clinician, or advocate. |
| PREGSC03 | What care setting is involved? | Note OB/GYN, midwife, family doctor, birth center, hospital, pediatrician, lactation support, mental-health support, or community program. |
| PREGSC04 | What urgency or sensitivity level exists? | Separate routine admin, time-sensitive appointment, concerning symptom, postpartum crisis concern, newborn urgent issue, or emergency escalation. |
| PREGSC05 | What privacy boundary applies? | Protect pregnancy status, health data, due date, genetic results, birth plans, insurance details, workplace plans, and family conflict notes. |
| PREGSC06 | What household constraints shape the workflow? | Capture work, school, transportation, childcare, finances, housing, disability access, language, cultural needs, or immigration/document concerns. |
| PREGSC07 | What support preferences matter? | Record desired helper roles, visitor boundaries, feeding preferences, birth support preferences, privacy expectations, and communication style. |
| PREGSC08 | What source records are available? | Inventory appointment notes, lab results, ultrasound reports, medication lists, insurance cards, hospital packet, pediatrician forms, and leave documents. |
| PREGSC09 | What should not be decided here? | Do not decide medical care, birth interventions, medication changes, feeding method, legal status, leave eligibility, or insurance coverage. |
| PREGSC10 | What scope artifact should be produced? | Create pregnancy/new-baby admin brief, privacy note, support map, timeline, or professional handoff packet. |
