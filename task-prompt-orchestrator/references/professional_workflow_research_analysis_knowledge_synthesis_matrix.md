# Professional Workflow Research Analysis Knowledge Synthesis Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must plan, conduct, analyze, synthesize, review, communicate, or maintain research, analysis, insight generation, literature review, market research, competitive intelligence, policy research, user/customer research, trend analysis, evidence synthesis, or decision-support memos.

## Research Scope And Question Framing

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| RESEARCHQ01 | What research question must be answered? | Convert a vague topic into a question, hypothesis, comparison, decision, or insight need. |
| RESEARCHQ02 | Who will use the research output? | Identify executive, product, marketing, academic, legal, technical, policy, sales, or operations audience. |
| RESEARCHQ03 | What decision or action depends on the research? | Tie analysis to a concrete choice, recommendation, design move, investment, policy, or next experiment. |
| RESEARCHQ04 | What scope boundary prevents sprawl? | Define geography, time period, domain, segment, source class, product category, population, or scenario boundary. |
| RESEARCHQ05 | What depth is appropriate? | Choose quick scan, structured brief, evidence map, deep review, systematic review, benchmark, or advisory memo. |
| RESEARCHQ06 | What freshness requirement applies? | Decide whether current, latest, historical, longitudinal, version-specific, or evergreen evidence is needed. |
| RESEARCHQ07 | What assumptions should be declared early? | Capture market, user, technical, legal, data, model, operational, or sampling assumptions. |
| RESEARCHQ08 | What would change the answer materially? | Identify variables, counterexamples, missing source classes, stakeholder input, or new data that could alter conclusions. |
| RESEARCHQ09 | What ethical or access boundary applies? | Check consent, confidentiality, private data, sensitive populations, copyright, paywalled sources, and fair-use limits. |
| RESEARCHQ10 | What framing artifact is required? | Produce research brief, question tree, scope note, hypothesis list, decision context, or analysis charter. |

## Source Strategy And Corpus Design

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| CORPUSRS01 | What source classes belong in the corpus? | Select papers, reports, filings, interviews, surveys, datasets, reviews, logs, competitor pages, policies, or artifacts. |
| CORPUSRS02 | What primary sources must anchor the work? | Prefer official documents, original studies, filings, standards, first-party data, interviews, product evidence, or raw records. |
| CORPUSRS03 | What secondary sources help interpretation? | Use credible reviews, expert commentary, meta-analyses, industry reports, analyst notes, or domain explainers. |
| CORPUSRS04 | What exclusion criteria are needed? | Remove stale, duplicated, promotional, unverifiable, irrelevant, low-authority, inaccessible, or off-scope material. |
| CORPUSRS05 | What sampling strategy is defensible? | Choose exhaustive, stratified, purposive, representative, time-windowed, top-N, snowball, or saturation-based sampling. |
| CORPUSRS06 | What search/query plan is needed? | Define keywords, synonyms, domain terms, languages, repositories, databases, connectors, and query variants. |
| CORPUSRS07 | What corpus metadata must be captured? | Track title, author, date, source, URL/path, version, license, geography, sample, method, confidence, and access time. |
| CORPUSRS08 | What corpus gap is acceptable? | Mark missing source types, paywall limits, inaccessible records, sample bias, or unavailable current data. |
| CORPUSRS09 | What corpus quality gate applies? | Verify source diversity, authority, recency, coverage, deduplication, readable content, and citation traceability. |
| CORPUSRS10 | What corpus artifact is required? | Produce source manifest, bibliography, evidence table, interview list, dataset inventory, or search log. |

## Literature And Evidence Mapping

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| LITMAP01 | What schools, debates, or evidence clusters exist? | Group sources by theory, method, dataset, geography, era, intervention, technology, claim, or stakeholder view. |
| LITMAP02 | What claims recur across sources? | Extract consensus, contested claims, emerging patterns, assumptions, and repeated limitations. |
| LITMAP03 | What seminal or high-authority sources anchor the field? | Identify canonical papers, standards, reviews, major datasets, regulatory texts, filings, or official reports. |
| LITMAP04 | What evidence contradicts the dominant view? | Capture counterexamples, negative findings, failed experiments, minority positions, and boundary conditions. |
| LITMAP05 | What evidence quality varies by cluster? | Compare sample size, method rigor, source authority, recency, reproducibility, and independence. |
| LITMAP06 | What chronology explains development? | Map how concepts, technologies, policies, markets, or research streams changed over time. |
| LITMAP07 | What gap remains after mapping evidence? | Identify missing populations, metrics, methods, markets, longitudinal data, replication, or implementation evidence. |
| LITMAP08 | What citation relationship matters? | Trace origin claims, derivative summaries, direct citations, citation loops, and unsupported repeated statements. |
| LITMAP09 | What evidence map should be preserved? | Keep claim-source matrix, cluster map, timeline, debate table, bibliography, or gap log. |
| LITMAP10 | What literature output is required? | Produce related-work synthesis, annotated bibliography, evidence landscape, research gap memo, or citation audit. |

| SYNTHRS09 | What insight should be reusable later? | Store finding, source links, segment, confidence, implication, limitation, owner, and refresh trigger. |

## Uncertainty Bias And Limitation Handling

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| UNCERT01 | What is unknown after the research? | List unanswered questions, missing sources, weak evidence, inaccessible data, conflicting facts, and untested assumptions. |
| UNCERT02 | What confidence level fits each major finding? | Rate evidence strength using authority, triangulation, sample, method rigor, recency, and directness. |
| UNCERT03 | What bias may distort conclusions? | Check selection, publication, survivorship, confirmation, language, platform, commercial, recency, and interviewer bias. |
| UNCERT04 | What proxy or inference is being used? | Label estimates, analogies, extrapolations, benchmark transfers, model assumptions, or expert judgment. |
| UNCERT05 | What overclaim must be avoided? | Prevent universal claims, causal certainty, exaggerated market size, unsupported ranking, or hidden normative judgment. |
| UNCERT06 | What sensitivity or scenario check is needed? | Test whether conclusions change under different assumptions, data ranges, source weights, or segment boundaries. |
| UNCERT07 | What evidence would falsify the conclusion? | Identify counterdata, stakeholder feedback, current source updates, experimental results, or direct measurements. |
| UNCERT08 | What limitation should be visible to the reader? | Disclose source gaps, method limits, sample constraints, freshness, rights limits, and confidence boundaries. |
| UNCERT09 | What follow-up research would reduce uncertainty? | Define next sources, interviews, datasets, experiments, expert review, or monitoring triggers. |
| UNCERT10 | What uncertainty artifact is required? | Produce caveat table, confidence register, assumption log, sensitivity note, or unresolved question list. |

## Research Review Validation And Quality Control

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| REVIEWRS01 | What peer or SME review is required? | Route to domain expert, stakeholder, method reviewer, legal reviewer, data reviewer, or executive sponsor. |
| REVIEWRS02 | What source audit should be run? | Check source existence, dates, authority, quote accuracy, citation closure, duplicates, and unsupported claims. |
| REVIEWRS03 | What method QA should be run? | Verify scope, sample, coding, metrics, comparisons, calculations, and assumption handling. |
| REVIEWRS04 | What contradiction review is needed? | Search for counterevidence, alternative interpretations, outdated claims, and stakeholder objections. |
| REVIEWRS05 | What reproducibility check applies? | Ensure another reviewer can trace queries, corpus, extraction, coding, calculations, and synthesis decisions. |
| REVIEWRS06 | What factual freshness check is required? | Re-check unstable facts, current roles, prices, laws, releases, schedules, market data, and standards before delivery. |
| REVIEWRS07 | What confidentiality or rights review is required? | Confirm redaction, quote limits, license, attribution, private data boundaries, and client/source restrictions. |
| REVIEWRS08 | What acceptance criteria define research quality? | Require clear answer, traceable evidence, stated method, caveats, actionable implication, and no unsupported overclaim. |
| REVIEWRS09 | What correction process should be used? | Log issue, fix source or analysis, update confidence, document change, and notify affected downstream users. |
| REVIEWRS10 | What review artifact is required? | Produce QA checklist, citation audit, method review note, correction log, signoff record, or validation summary. |
