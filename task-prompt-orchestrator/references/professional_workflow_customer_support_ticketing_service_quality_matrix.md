# Professional Workflow Customer Support Ticketing Service Quality Matrix

Use this matrix when a workflow must receive, classify, route, respond to, resolve, escalate, quality-check, analyze, automate, document, or improve customer support requests, tickets, cases, conversations, help center content, service queues, support macros, support SLAs, support tooling, or frontline service operations.

## Support Scope And Service Promise

| Code | Workflow question | Workflow use |
|---|---|---|
| SUPSCOPE01 | What support outcome is in scope? | Define issue resolution, customer reassurance, technical troubleshooting, billing help, account access, product guidance, or incident communication. |
| SUPSCOPE02 | What customer segment or plan is affected? | Identify free, trial, paid, enterprise, VIP, regulated, partner, internal user, admin, or vulnerable customer group. |
| SUPSCOPE03 | What product, service, or workflow is involved? | Link ticket to feature, account, order, subscription, integration, policy, device, document, or operational process. |
| SUPSCOPE04 | What support promise applies? | Define hours, language, channel, response time, resolution target, escalation path, and out-of-scope boundaries. |
| SUPSCOPE05 | What customer impact level applies? | Classify inconvenience, blocked task, lost revenue, data risk, safety risk, legal risk, churn risk, or public reputation risk. |
| SUPSCOPE06 | What support policy must be followed? | Apply refund, cancellation, warranty, privacy, security, abuse, accessibility, account ownership, or billing policy. |
| SUPSCOPE07 | What tone and relationship context matters? | Match empathy, clarity, urgency, formality, apology, ownership, and customer history. |
| SUPSCOPE08 | What support boundary requires handoff? | Route sales, customer success, engineering, legal, finance, security, vendor, or executive matters to the right owner. |
| SUPSCOPE09 | What support artifact is required? | Produce service brief, support flow, response macro, ticket playbook, case note, or support readiness checklist. |
| SUPSCOPE10 | What proves the support workflow worked? | Use resolution, customer confirmation, SLA compliance, accurate notes, no reopen, quality pass, and learning captured. |
